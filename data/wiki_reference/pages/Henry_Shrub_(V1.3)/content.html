Henry Michael Shrub (October 21, 1924 - February 11, 2002), also known by his initials, HMS, was an American lawyer, judge, fighter pilot and politician who served as the President of the United States from his election in 2000 until his death in his second year in office in 2002. Shrub also served as Mayor of New Haven from 1964 to 1972, the Governor of Connecticut from 1972 to 1976, and as School Board Director from 1980 to 1988. As a lawyer, he was most prominent with the firm Fudge & Duffy, at which he worked from 1954 until his mayoral election in 1964. He also worked with Osborne Associates for several years. As a judge, he served as a magistrate for the United States District Court for the District of Connecticut for twelve years until his victorious presidential election. Shrub was also a distinguished U.S. Air Force officer during the Korean War and Cold War.
