Moe Marconi (1944 - 2019) was an American mail carrier. He was the younger brother of Chris Marconi, also known by his stage name CMarc, who wrote several songs about him and his family.

Early Life[]

Moe was born in 1944 as the second child of Vernon and Becky Marconi. Chris was six years old at the time. They enjoyed playing together as children.

Career[]

In 1963, Moe started working as a Jr. Mail Carrier for The City of Jacksonville. In six years, he was promoted twice:

- Mail Carrier (1966)

- Sr. Mail Carrier (1969)

Moe retired in 2009, at the age of 65, after 46 years of mail carrying.

Personal Life[]

Moe came out as gay to the family in the early 1970s. Chris offered him unconditional love and support, encouraging him to be true to himself. Chris became inspired to make a song or two about Moe, such as "Hey Moe I'm Home" (1983).

In 1974, Moe married Bob Galore, a professor. They divorced in 1994 after 20 years of marriage.

Death[]

Moe died peacefully in his sleep in 2019, aged 75.
