Jens von Bout (1936 - 2019) was a Dutch senior enlisted fighter pilot in the Royal Netherlands Air Force. From the early 1970s to the late 1990s, he served as a chief master sergeant that led many successful missions and had an influential impact on other pilots. He was the father of drug dealer, mobster and gambler Dar von Bout.

Childhood[]

Jens was born in the Amsterdam area near a fishing village to a family of fishmongers and other professions.

Military service[]

Between 1954 and 1955, Von Bout enlisted in the Royal Netherlands Air Force, merely a year or two after the branch's formation.

After more than forty years of service, Von Bout retired from active duty in 1998.

Personal life[]

Von Bout met Femke Dekker (1935 - 2015) between the late 1950s and early 1960s. At the time they started dating, Dekker was a rookie restaurant worker who would eventually work her way through the ranks into management positions. Jens and Femke married between the mid-1960s and early 1970s. They had two children, Lianne (1971 - 2009) and Dar (October 9, 1972 - December 27, 2024). Lianne worked as a firefighter until she was murdered by a rival group of Dar's mafia connections.

Jens died in 2019 at the age of 83. He is survived by his grandchildren and is predeceased by his wife and daughter.
