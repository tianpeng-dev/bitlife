Early life (2973-2991)
[
]
Cross was born in Lyon, France. He was the youngest of four children born to
Luther Cross II
and the younger of two born to his third girlfriend Jade Brun. He was named for his 15x great-uncle.
Cross went to Jubilee Preparatory School and graduated in 2991.
Career (2991-3037)
[
]
In 2991, Cross signed with Cobalt Studios as a country singer. He released his debut bluegrass album,
You Darken My Existence
, which went gold in less than a year.
In 2992, Cross released his second album,
Shine of a Nickel
, which went gold in 2998.
In 2997, Cross released his third album,
Magnolia Hideouts
, which went gold in 2998.
In 3002, Cross released his fourth album,
Tame Angles
, which went gold in less than a year.
In 3007, Cross released his fifth album,
Ain’t No Other Cowboy
, which went gold in less than a year.
In 3013, Cross released his sixth album,
Stranger Friends
, which went platinum in less than a year and double platinum in 3016.
In 3021, Cross released his seventh album,
The Chocolate Milk Stand
, which went double platinum in less than a year and diamond in 3023.
In 3023, Cross released his eighth album,
Beer Cans and Dirt Roads
, which went double platinum in less than a year.
In 3026, Cross released his ninth album,
Browner Acres
, which went gold in less than a year and platinum in 3027.
In 3032, Cross released his 10th album,
Calm Horses
, which went double platinum in less than a year and diamond in 3034.
In 3034, Cross released his 11th album,
You Brighten Up My Life
, which went gold in less than a year.
In 3037, Cross was released by Cobalt Studios.
Death
[
]
In 3059, Cross died of old age.
Relationship and children
[
]
In 2983, Cross started dating his classmate, Celia Foutaine (2973-3062), who became an insurance agent in 2992. They had two sons:
Marcos Cross II (2987-3004)
