Henry Michael Shrub (October 21, 1874 - February 11, 1952), also known by his initials, HMS, was an American lawyer, judge and politician who served as the President of the United States from his election in 1950 until his death in his second year in office in 1952. Shrub also served as Mayor of New Haven from 1914 to 1922, the Governor of Connecticut from 1922 to 1926, and as School Board Director from 1930 to 1938. As a lawyer, he was most prominent with the firm Fudge & Duffy, at which he worked from 1904 until his mayoral election in 1914. He also worked with Osborne Associates for several years. As a judge, he served as a magistrate for the United States District Court for the District of Connecticut for twelve years until his victorious presidential election.
