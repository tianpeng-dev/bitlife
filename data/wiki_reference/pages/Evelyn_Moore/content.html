Evelyn Moore (June 10, 1944 - December 1, 2022) was a French singer, composer, and model best known for her albums Daydreams and Enshrined, both holding world records for the most copies sold. Going by the stage name of Evelyn, her albums consistently broke diamond certification. Throughout her career, she also pursued endeavors of modeling, appearing as the centerfold for several magazines.

Early Life and Education[]

Evelyn was born on June 10th, 1944, to parents Daniel and Zara Moore in Australia. In 1946, following the Second World War, the family moved to France, and Evelyn's younger brother Paul was born that year, followed by Gabriel the next year. As a child, Evelyn had an intense interest in sports, and played both soccer and volleyball, as well as studying martial arts. However, an intense interest for music led her to quit soccer and take music and guitar lessons, She participated in the school choir as well as becoming captain of her team. By the time Moore was 17, she had the top belt in all five martial arts.

Upon graduating from high school, Evelyn was offered several athletic scholarships in America. She moved there and signed to play volleyball with Stanford University for the next four years of her career - majoring in music. She joined Alpha Omicron Pi and was part of the popular kids clique. After graduation, she briefly considered going pro in volleyball, but decided not to. Reflecting on this decision later, she made the right decision, as volleyball would've been a limited career for a dozen or so seasons, while music was something that was part of her life.

Musical Career[]

Upon finishing schooling in America, Evelyn moved back to France to pursue a singing career. She was invited to perform for three record labels - Equinox Studios, Interstellar Studios, and Olympus Records. After not being offered contracts with the first two, Olympus offered a four year contract for her to release her music. Despite the low bonus and royalty, she immediately agreed and seized the opportunity, adopting the stage name "Evelyn" at the time.

Rising Career[]

In 1966, Evelyn released her first album, Dancefloors. The album received a gold certification at the time, highlighting future success for the songwriter. She went on her first worldwide tour the year later, although it was only ten weeks long. Two years after her first album, she released her second album "Cityside", highlighting the change in her life after becoming a famous singer. The album went gold again, and Evelyn continued her pursuits, performing at her first music festival in Amsterdam, Netherland, to over 500,000 people. Evelyn would release one more album titled "Affection", before she re-signed with Olympus Records on a nine year contract. The album didn't perform well and barely received a gold certification.
