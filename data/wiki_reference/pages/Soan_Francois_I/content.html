Soan Francois I (3217-3292) was a French reporter and alternative rapper.

Early life (3217-3240)[]

Francois was born in Lyon, France. He was the only child born to Rayan Francois I and his first wife, Mathilde Pasteur. In 3223, Rayan married Elizabeth Cross II, making Soan her second child.

Francois went to Emlyon Business School and graduated in 3240 with a BA in Finance.

First reporting career (3240-3250)[]

In 3240, Francois became a junior reporter for Triton TV.

In 3242, Francois was promoted to reporter.

In 3244, Francois was promoted to senior reporter.

In 3247, Francois became famous.

Music career (3250-3262)[]

In 3250, Francois signed with Medley Records as a rhythm and blues singer. He released his debut alternative rap album, Timelines of a Dude.

In 3251, Francois released his second album, When I Get Busted.

In 3256, Francois released his third album, Glory of Velvet.

In 3261, Francois released his fourth album, Even the Wheelbarrow.

In 3262, Francois was released by Medley.

Second reporting career (3263-3279)[]

In 3263, Francois became a junior reporter for Farmland Broadcasting Corporation.

In 3265, Francois was promoted to reporter.

In 3267, Francois was promoted to senior reporter.

In 3279, Francois retired.

Death[]

In 3292, Francois died of natural causes.
