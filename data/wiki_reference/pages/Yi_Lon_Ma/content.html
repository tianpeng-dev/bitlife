Yi Lon Ma, commonly known as Elon Musk, (b. October 6, 1983 - d. ???) is a CEO of his company, TechVillage, and was a professional athlete for 19 years. He currently lives in Salt Lake City, United States, and he currently has a Pomeranian dog named Gentry.

Early life[]

Yi Lon was born in Shenzhen, China to retired travel agent Ying Zhang (b. 1960) and chef Jianjun Ma (b. 1939 - d. 2013). He had a dog named Buddy.

When Yi Lon was only 4 years old (1987), his mother was promoted to General Manager. When he was only 7 years old (1990), his Australian shepherd, Buddy, escaped his home, and he wasn't able to look for him anywhere.

At Harbor Upper Secondary School, he was an athlete. And at his university, he was offered a full athletic scholarship to play basketball at university.

Adulthood[]

When he was 19 years old (2002), he started his investment portfolio.

Utah Missionaries[]

After graduating his university when he was 22 years old (2005), and he made himself eligible for the pro basketball draft. He was forced to move to the United States in order to play for the Utah Missionaries as a shooting guard. 1 year later, he won the Rookie of the Year award for being the most "outstanding" rookie in the league and graciously accepted it. 4 years later, the Utah Missionaries offered him a new 6-year $47.4M contract.

5 years later, he purchased 540,000 shares of Gray Aerospace Defense for $26,516,226. His friend told Yi Lon the CEO of Gray Aerospace Defense gave his friend inside information about the company.

New York Bankers[]

1 year later, he was released by the Utah Missionaries at the end of his contract. Following his release, he was offered a 4-year $34.8M contract to play professional basketball for the New York Bankers as a shooting guard. 4 years later, the New York Bankers offered him a new 3-year $31.8M contract. He also sold his entire Gray Aerospace Defense holding for $42,307,281.

Minnesota Lumberdogs[]

3 years later, he was released by the New York Bankers at the end of his contract. Following his release, he was offered a 1-year $6.4M contract to play basketball for the Minnesota Lumberdogs as a shooting guard.

CEO of TechVillage[]

1 year later, he was released by the Minnesota Lumberdogs at the end of his contract. Following his release, he invested $60,400,000 into a new consumer electronics startup and named it 'TechVillage', and placed his salary as a CEO of TechVillage at $625,000, and performed market research on the computer monitor product segment. Introducing a new computer monitor product as TechVillage and named it 'Official Monitor of Salt Lake City'.
