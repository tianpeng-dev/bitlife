Theodore Frederick Bunker (March 30, 1937 - May 4, 2022), often referred to by his initials TFB, was an American lobbyist and politician who served as the Mayor of Kingsville from 2001 to 2009 and the Governor of Texas from 2013 to 2017. He unsuccessfully ran for the presidency three times. He lobbied for many successful businesses, including Pacific Banking Corporation, Unity Fashions and Corona Mills.

Prior to his lobbying and political careers, Bunker was an officer in the United States Navy, serving from the mid-1950s to the late 1980s, with a gap in the mid-to-late 1960s filled by enrollment in the Naval Academy. A pinnacle moment in his service was in 1986 when, on a deployment in Uruguayan waters, he sustained massive injuries from a mine explosion, after which he suffered PTSD. Bunker reached the rank of commander before retiring from the Navy in 1989.

Early life and education[]

Teddy was born in Kingsville, Texas to librarian Jacob Bunker (1905 - 1984) and insurance agent Darlene Bunker (1908 - 1984). He had a pointer named Freya (d. 1947) and two sisters, Olivia (1935 - 2019) and Desiree (born 1943). Jacob and Darlene divorced in 1945, when Teddy was eight years old. Several sources state that he was named after Teddy Roosevelt. Olivia was a legal secretary for Gulch & Brown. Desiree is a wedding planner for The Hunt Company. Desiree is also a dancer, having earned the degree in college.

From an early age, becoming a leader was one of Bunker's main aspirations. At Illumina Middle School, he joined the politics club to begin his training to get a seat. He also took up foreign language, environment, chess and creative writing. Bunker enjoyed chess, comparing the pieces to naval ships in a battle. Bunker attended Neptune Preparatory School, class of 1955. He wanted to join the student council in hopes of practicing the role of a president, but he was rejected. He rejoined the clubs of chess, environment, foreign language, and also took up key and yearbook. In his junior year, Bunker became the president of the foreign language club. He took this opportunity to practice holding true to a title.

US Navy[]

Bunker's own surname inspired his enlistment in the United States military in summer 1955, months before the breakout of the Vietnam War. In the Navy, he befriended his fellow seamen/seawomen, Casey Strange, Christopher Simms, Joe Palermo, Joy Blart, Katie Elliot, Madison Wu-Beers and the Cox sisters, Ashley and Trinity Cox.

...

In September 1965, Bunker enrolled in the United States Naval Academy, Class of 1969, to avoid conscription for the Vietnam War, majoring in politics. He befriended fellow freshman classmates and future sailors Ashley Powell, Cyril Petit, Darnell Butkus-Hunt, Devin Herrera, Kiara Picker (supposedly related to veterinarian Harry Pickler), Selena Price, Serena Pringle, and Victoria Thompson. Bunker became a member of Amnesty International, the archery club, the marching band, and even worked as an admissions assistant.

...

Graduating from the Academy in 1969, Bunker rejoined the Navy as an ensign.

...

In 1986, Bunker was shipped to Uruguay for a deployment. He successfully cleared several squares of water from mines. On his third or fourth dig, he accidentally detonated a mine. His chin was burnt, his forehead was charred, and his scrotum was scarred. He would suffer from PTSD for the next few years after the incident. In 1989, at 52 years old, Bunker retired from the Navy after thirty-four total years of service.

Personal life[]

Bunker married his high school sweetheart, Veronica Woodward (1937 - 2003), a mail carrier, in 1960. They had five children together and were married for forty-three years. Woodward was killed in a flood in 2003, during Teddy's second year in office of his first mayoral term. Her death prompted him to enforce new policies on natural disaster preparation. Bunker then started a second romance with his childhood friend, Genesis Mayweather (1937), and they were married from 2007 until his death.

Bunker was a fan of Star Wars since seeing A New Hope on the night of its premier, May 25, 1977 with his family and some of his fellow sea mates. Besides the complex storyline and character development, he was impressed with the lighting, special effects and design of the franchise. Whenever Bunker was on leave during service, he collected the merchandise and provided acting skills as an extra in the movies as either a jedi, stormtrooper, or other.

Death[]

On May 4, 2022, after watching a Star Wars movie in celebration of "May the 4th," Bunker suffered a massive heart attack and passed away in his home, aged 85. He is survived by his second wife, little sister, Desiree, five children and three living grandchildren (a fourth, grandson Keenan, died in 2018 at age eleven from complications associated with measles). With military honors presented at his funeral, Bunker was posthumously promoted by the Navy to the officer rank of captain.

He was predeceased by his parents, who both died in 1984. Jacob succumbed to cancer of the anus, of which he had been diagnosed two years earlier. Darlene suffered from a stroke. Teddy cremated both of them, spreading his father's ashes at his favorite beach, and his mother's ashes on her favorite hiking trail. In 2024, on the fortieth anniversaries of the spreading of Jacob and Darlene's ashes, hikers and beachgoers alike claimed to have had intriguing encounters with their spirits. Bunker was also predeceased by his older sister, Olivia, who died in 2019.
