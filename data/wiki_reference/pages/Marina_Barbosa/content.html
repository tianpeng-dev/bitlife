Marina Barbosa (August 19, 1749-1823) was a Portuguese-American volleyball player, restaurant worker, and founder of the Barbosa Legacy.

Early life[]

Barbosa was born in Rio de Janeiro, State of Brazil. She briefly went to the University of Coimbra in mainland Portugal, but dropped out in 1768. After emigrating to Baltimore, Province of Maryland, Barbosa went to King William’s School in Annapolis and joined the volleyball team. She graduated with a BA in English in 1772.

Volleyball career[]

In 1772, Barbosa joined the Portland Peppers as a libero.

In 1776, Barbosa was released by the Peppers and joined the San Francisco Somas. She was released in 1791.

Restaurant career[]

In 1792, Barbosa became a dishwasher at Union Restaurant. She became a general manager before retiring in 1815.

Death[]

In 1823, Barbosa died of natural causes at the age of 74. Her funeral was held in Baltimore and her ashes were scattered in a movie theater.

Relationship and children[]

From 1763 to 1768, Barbosa dated a student named Murillo Reis (1749-1826), who became a hairdresser in 1767. They had three children named Neymar (1766-1776), Joao-Pedro (1767-after 1829), and Joana Reis.
