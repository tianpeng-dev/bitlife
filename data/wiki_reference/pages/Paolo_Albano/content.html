Paolo Albano I (3865-after 3922) was an Italian businessman and photographer.

Early life (3865-3884)[]

Albano was born in Genoa, Italy. He is the oldest of three children born to Maurizio Albano and his ex-wife Viviana Zeppeli.

Business career (3884-3908)[]

In 3884, Albano assumed the family business, House of Fashion.

In 3889, Albano sold House of Fashion and acquired a consumer electronics company called Genoa Electric Supply.

In 3899, Albano became famous.

In 3908, Albano sold Genoa Electric Supply, making a profit of over $120 million.

Photography career (3909-3922)[]

In 3909, Albano became an apprentice photographer for Genoa Journal.

In 3912, Albano was promoted to photographer.

Albano was alive in 3922, but his death date is unknown.

Relationship and children[]

In 3884, Albano started dating a real estate agent named Alice Ferrari (3849-after 3922). They had two children, Monica I (3885-after 3922) and Maurizio Albano II.

Legal issues[]

In 3884, Albano was tried for the murder of his father and younger brother, but was acquitted.
