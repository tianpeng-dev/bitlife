Quincy Cross (2456-2528) was an American sommelier.

Early life (2456-2478)[]

Cross was born in Jacksonville, Florida. He was the youngest of three children born to Diego Cross and his ex-wife Ginger Roberts.

Cross went to the University of North Florida and graduated in 2478 with a BA in Art History.

First career (2478-2489)[]

In 2478, Cross became an introductory sommelier for Jennifer’s Diner.

In 2481, Cross was promoted to certified sommelier.

In 2484, Cross was promoted to advanced sommelier.

In 2487, Cross was promoted to master sommelier.

In 2489, Cross resigned.

In 2491, Cross won $100,000 at Jacksonville Nation Casino.

Second career (2492-2522)[]

In 2492, Cross became an introductory sommelier for Café Nova.

In 2495, Cross was promoted to certified sommelier.

In 2498, Cross was promoted to advanced sommelier.

In 2501, Cross was promoted to master sommelier.

In 2522, Cross retired.

Death[]

In 2528, Cross died of old age.
