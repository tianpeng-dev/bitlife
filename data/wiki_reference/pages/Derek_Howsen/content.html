Derek Charles Howsen (April 1, 1935 - May 14, 2024) was an American lawyer, judge, college football player, and United States Marine Corps veteran. As a Marine, he is best remembered for his deployment to the Phillipines in 1984, where he successfully disarmed several landmines, earning him a Medal of Honor. After three decades of service, Howsen retired from the Marines at the rank of Sergeant Major and started his new career as a lawyer in the 1990s, which lasted for another three decades.

Early life and education[]

Derek was born in Arlignton County, Virginia to Dennis Howsen (1903 - 1976) and Camille Howsen (1906 - 1994). His father was a writer, and his mother was a magician. A middle child, he had two brothers, Donald (1934 - 2019) and Vernon (born 1942). Donald was a dancer for Blart Productions, though he originally started off as a troop entertainer in the Korean War. Vernon is a retired receptionist from Jupiter Mills who dodged the Vietnam draft without going to college. The family owned a mongrel named Tuffin (1930 - 1940). His great-grandfather, Charles Howsen (c. 1840s - c. 1910s), was a Civil War veteran, his grandfather, Charles Jr. (c. 1870s - c. 1940s), was a Spanish-American War Veteran, and his uncle, Charles Howsen III (c. 1900 - c. 1970s), was a World War I veteran.

Howsen had a passion for sports at a young age. He also saw himself as a military brat due to living in the same city where the USMC is headquartered. When he was six years old, he played soccer in a league with some of his friends. This experience would inspire him to pursue his talents in athletics, though maybe not as a business. In middle school, he played football and was also a member of the cheerleading team. As a cheerleader, he would often don an alternate persona, Derin, and dress up in a traditional feminine cheer uniform to express his school spirit. He also played football at St. Steve's High School, managing to become captain the year before graduating. After graduating high school in 1953, he accepted a full athletic scholarship to play football at Marymount University (then known as Marymount College). Howsen was also a seasoned member of the jocks clique throughout his education.

College football and law school[]

At Marymount, aside from being on the football team and a member of the jocks yet again, Howsen was also a member of the Alpha Sigma Phi fraternity. As captain, he led his team(s) to many win championships in the NCAA III Division and other conferences. Upon graduating from Marymount, he became eligible for the 1957 NFL draft. As much as he enjoyed football, Howsen turned down the opportunity to go pro, for he saw the sport as more of a fun and valuable hobby than as a business and did not wish to live under any pressures of fame. With a degree in criminal justice, Howsen enrolled in law school, where he joined the football team once more. He played well during his three years at law school, and always sought to leave a positive impact on the football industry, even if he wasn't playing professionally.

Military service[]

In 1960, after completing law school, Howsen voluntarily enlisted in the Marines to avoid the pressures of being drafted.

Law career[]

After retiring from active duty, Howsen set to work on becoming a lawyer to make use of his law school education.

Personal life[]

Howsen had his first kiss with Carson Lovato, a photographer, at the age of fifteen. From that moment, they started going out. After dating for six years, Lovato proposed to Howsen in 1956. They married that same year and went on to have four children and seven grandchildren together:

Death[]

Howsen died from a heart attack at the age of 89 on May 14, 2024. He is survived by his wife, four children, younger brother, and six surviving grandchildren. He is buried in the Arlington National Cemetary, where is grave is marked with all of his military honors.
