Sammy Krueger (né Slayer, 2713-2797) was an American football player and travel agent.

Early life (2713-2735)[]

Krueger was born Sammy Slayer in Jacksonville, Florida. In 2714, he was adopted by Miley Krueger and her boyfriend Cesar Roma, and his last name was changed to Krueger.

In 2718, Krueger insulted 5 classmates and was suspended for a day for starting a game of hangman during a lesson. This affected his relationship with his mother.

In 2724, Krueger burgled a lumberjack’s house. He was convicted of burglary and sentenced to one year in juvenile detention. He escaped less than a year later and returned home.

In 2727, Krueger was expelled from Kayak Prep for disrespecting the principal. He was then enrolled at Trayvon Slayer High. He was on the Trayvon Slayer football team from 2727 to 2731.

In 2731, Krueger enrolled at the University of North Florida. He was a member of Alpha Epsilon Pi and played football for the Ospreys. He graduated in 2735 with a BS in Nursing.

Sports career (2735-2753)[]

In 2735, Krueger joined the Detroit Pylons as a quarterback.

After Krueger’s rookie season, the Pylons finished in 31st place in the BitLife Football League.

During Krueger’s second season, he threw a game-winning touchdown, beating the Houston Textiles. Because of this game, the Pylons finished in 22nd.

After Krueger’s third season, the Pylons finished in 27th.

After Krueger’s fourth season, the Pylons finished in 16th.

After Krueger’s fifth season, the Pylons finished in 10th.

After Krueger’s sixth season, the Pylons finished in seventh and he won the Most Improved Player Award.

After Krueger’s seventh season, the Pylons finished in 21st and he won the Most Improved Player Award for the second time.

After Krueger’s eighth season, the Pylons finished in 12th.

After Krueger’s ninth season, the Pylons finished in 16th and he won the Offensive Player of the Year Award.

During Krueger’s 10th season, he ran the ball into the end zone for a game winning touchdown, beating the New York Vets. The Pylons finished in 10th and he won Offensive Player of the Year for the second time.

After Krueger’s 11th season, the Pylons finished in 13th.

After Krueger’s 12th season, the Pylons finished in sixth and he won Offensive Player of the Year for the third time.

After Krueger’s 13th season, the Pylons finished third.

After Krueger’s 14th season, the Pylons finished 13th and he won Offensive Player of the Year for the fourth time.

After Krueger’s 15th season, the Pylons finished 15th and he won Offensive Player of the Year for the fifth time.

After Krueger’s 16th season, the Pylons finished 14th.

During Krueger’s 17th season, he missed four games after having surgery on his ruptured Achilles tendon. However, the Pylons finished seventh.

After Krueger’s 18th season, the Pylons finished sixth. He retired.

Travel agency career (2754-2779)[]

In 2754, Krueger became a travel associate for Jorge Baker-Stanley.

In 2758, Krueger was promoted to tour operator.

In 2762, Krueger was promoted to general manager.

In 2779, Krueger retired.

Death[]

In 2797, Krueger died of a stroke. His body was donated to science.

Relationship and child[]

In 2725, Krueger started dating a student named Amy Casanova (2713-2792), who later became a restaurant worker. They had a son, Kendrick Krueger (b. 2730).
