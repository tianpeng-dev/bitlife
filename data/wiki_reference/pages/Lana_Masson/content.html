Lana Masson (2825-after 2881) was a French actor, bluegrass singer, and pilot.

Early life (2825-2847)[]

Masson was born in Lyon, France. Her parents were Mathilde Krueger and her boyfriend Gabriel Masson. She has an older sister named Alice, who is a dancer. She went to Lyon Secondary School.

In 2843, Masson enrolled at Claude Bernard University. She graduated in 2847 with a BS in Chemistry.

Acting career (2847-2851)[]

In 2847, Masson started playing Aimee McPhee in season 3 of the TV show Lawson’s Creek, which was cancelled after the season.

In 2848, Masson started playing Flores in season 5 of Daughters of Anarchy, which was cancelled after season 6.

In 2850, Masson started playing Aria Rose in Fuch’s Creek, which was cancelled after one season.

Music career (2851-2862)[]

In 2851, Masson signed with Aurora Beats as a country singer. She released her debut bluegrass album, The Page of Us, which was certified diamond by the National Syndicate of Photographic Publishing in 2852.

In 2852, Masson released her second album, Country Dudes, which was certified diamond by SNEP less than a year later.

In 2853, Masson released her third album, You Brighten Up My Life, which was certified diamond by SNEP in 2854.

In 2856, Masson released her fourth album, Come On Down, which was certified diamond by SNEP in 2858.

In 2859, Masson released her fifth album, Come On Under, which was certified diamond by SNEP less than a year later.

In 2862, Masson released her sixth and final album, Family Friends, which was certified diamond by SNEP less than a year later. She walked out on her contract.

Pilot career (2863-2881)[]

In 2863, Masson became a pilot trainee for Dupuy Airlines.

In 2869, Masson was promoted to copilot.

In 2875, Masson was promoted to pilot.

Masson was alive in 2881, but her death date is unknown.

Relationship and children[]

From 2835 to 2844, Masson started dating a student named Clement Marie (2825-after 2881), who later became a trucker. They had two sons, Noah (2842-after 2881) and Esteban Marie.
