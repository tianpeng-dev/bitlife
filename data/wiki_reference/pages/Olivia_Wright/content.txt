Olivia Wright
(July 9, 1922 - November 29, 2022) was an American commercial airline pilot. She flew for Alloy Airways for nearly five decades, from the early 1950s until the cusp of the 21st century. Prior to becoming a pilot, she spent the first half of the 1940s working multiple odd jobs before spending the other half of the decade obtaining her college degree.

Early life
[
]
Olivia was born in Dayton, Ohio to Erica (1888 - 1963) and Yusef Wright (1885 - 1959). Her mother was a real estate agent, while her father was a photographer and Middle Eastern immigrant. Olivia had three siblings:
Shauna (1920 - 2011) - Real estate agent for Daisy Simms Realtors
Grayson (1925 - 2008) - Restaurateur at D'Marcus' Cafe; possibly served in WW2
Colt (1927 - 2008) - Trucker for Granite Trucking & Services
The family also had a cat named Atticus (c. 1921 - 1933).
An enthusiastic child, Olivia was very active in her youth and dreamt of flying from a young age. In middle and high school, she was a cheerleader. She became the captain of her high school cheerleading team in her junior/senior year. She was also a member of her middle school's math club, of which she became treasurer in eighth grade, and her high school's recycling club, of which she became vice-president in her sophomore/junior year. Given her status as a talented cheerleader, Olivia was a member of the "mean girls" clique.
Pre-pilot history
[
]
After Wright graduated from high school in 1940, she planned on going to college but found it difficult to find an affordable school due to price changes in the aftermath of the Great Depression. Opting to wait on continuing her education, she found a job as a stripper for Darell's Cabaret. After working there for a year, she became a foot model for The Atmosphere Agency. From 1942 to 1943, Wright worked as a pornographic actress, primarily appearing in Allied propaganda adult films. She returned to her job as a foot model after ending her short adult film career. In 1944, she became a magician for Circus Eagle. After that, she was a stuntwoman for Dayton Films. Wright's last job before college was being a mechanic for Dayton Auto Emporium from 1946 to 1947.
In 1947, Wright enrolled in the University of Dayton, Class of 1951, on a scholarship. An engineering major, she became a cheerleader once more, earning her captaincy in her junior/senior year in 1950. She also joined the school's foreign language and sewing clubs. While she didn't fit in with the mean girls this time, Wright found connectivity with the clique of talented students.
Flying career
[
]
At age the maximum age requirement of 29 years old in 1951, Wright became a pilot trainee at Alloy Airways. She had taken over 40 hours of flying lessons and earned her pilot license earlier that year. After six years of trainee flying, she was promoted to co-pilot in 1957. Another six years later, she became a full-time, primary pilot.

Personal life
[
]
Wright was bisexual and was married four times. She met her first husband, fellow pilot Elmer Martinez (1887 - 1962), during her first year as a pilot in 1951. They got married a year later and would have six children together:
Laila (born 1952) - Policy analyst (retired)
Penelope (born 1954) - IT Support for United Services (retired)
Anthony (born 1956) - Flight attendant for Alloy Airways (retired)
Mickey (born 1957) - Mailman (retired)
Maddie (born 1959) - IT Support for United Services (retired)
Oprah (born 1962) - Dayton PD officer (Chief of Police)
Additionally, Wright was the stepmother of Kristen Martinez (1922 - 2003), Elmer's daughter from a previous marriage. Kristen was envious of her stepmother for being the same age as her. Elmer died in 1962 from old age. That same year, Wright started dating childhood friend Cassandra Stanley (1922 - 2006). They were married for 39 years, from 1967 until Cassandra's death in 2006, making it Wright's longest marriage. Later that year, she married an ex-fling named Kira, who died the following year. From 2007 to 2010, she dated retired grocer William "Bill" Robinson. After Robinson's passing in 2010, Wright dated fellow retired pilot Chip Vasquez until his death in 2016. In 2017, she began dating retired college professor Marquis Colbert, a possible relative of NYPD officer
Tom Colbert
. They wed that same year and were married until Wright's death. Wright was the stepmother to Colbert's four children, Robert (born 1954), Alvaro (born 1956), Bobbie Sue, and Tara, as well as the step-grandmother of Marquis's grandchildren.
Wright died on November 29, 2022, aged 100. She is survived by her husband, ten children and fifteen grandchildren.
