Mandlenkosi Ndlela (born December 18, 1988) is an amaZulu South African Apprentice Auto Mechanic for Duplooy Durban. He's a father of three, a boy, a girl, and another on the way as of 2008.

Early Life[]

Ndlela was born a male on December 18th, 1988, in Durban, South Africa, as the result of an affair between a 48-year-old travel agent, Tholakele Khumalo, and a 46-year-old pornographer, Bokamoso Ndlela. He had a half-brother, Mandla Ntuthuko, 9 years his elder, who was the son of Tholakele's husband, Elon Ntuthuko, whom Mandlenkosi never met.

Later that year, he came into possession of a VHS tape of the family reunion that Christmas.

In 1989, Mandlenkosi's father married one Uminathi Masemola, a 27-year-old Banker. They went on a brief honeymoon in Kuala Lumpur Malaysia. Uminathi Masemola was unkind to Mandlenkosi.

In 1990, Tholakele Khumalo attempted to have Mandlenkosi vaccinated at the local doctor's office. Terrified of the approaching needle, Mandlenkosi threw a conniption fit. Around then, Mandlenkosi made his first friend, Kungawo Khune, a neighbor's son.

Growing up, Mandlenkosi spent time with his father, who introduced him to new hobbies and offered important stability and discipline. His mother was more neglectful, leaving him alone to go to parties and hookup with people. His stepmother, Uminathi, refused to spend time with or even talk to him. At a young age, Mandlenkosi learned to bake with his friend Kungawo. An intelligent kid, Mandlenkosi learned to read by the age of 4. He watched a spelling bee with his father and managed to repeat each word he saw spelled letter for letter.

By 1994, Bokamoso became concerned about the strained relationship between Mandlenkosi and Uminathi.

In 1995, Mandlenkosi was enrolled at an All-Boys Primary School run by the well-liked no-nonsense 74-year-old Headmaster S'bu Makeba, by his mother Tholakele. The same year, his father started teaching him how to swim.

In 1996, Mandlenkosi went on a road trip around South Africa with his mother and half-brother, which he didn't enjoy much but did appreciate. The same year, Mandlenkosi started taking Kung Fu with his mother's permission. His father encouraged him to do so for the benefit to his health and capabilities. He started going on walks as part of his training.

In 1997, Mandlenkosi started trying to get a dog, and the next year, his mother let him get a 5-year-old male brown and white mutt named Avalanche. The same year, he befriended a classmate named S'kulekile Masilela.

In 1999, Bokamoso died of natural causes at the relatively old age of 57. He left Mandlenkosi over 1 million USD in inheritance. Kungawo was there for Mandlenkosi while he grieved and they became best friends.

In 2000, Tholakele retired, so Mandlenkosi stopped relying on her and focused on being more self-sufficient and disciplined. He was then enrolled at an All-Boys Secondary School run by a strict 35-year-old man named Gift Mkhabela. By then, Mandlenkosi was consistently going to the gym.

In 2002, Avalanche died peacefully at the age of 9 (63 in dog years). Mandlenkosi had him buried in the Ndlela family plot.

In 2003, Mandlenkosi earned his black belt in Kung Fu.

In 2005, Tholakele died of natural causes at the age of 55, leaving Mandlenkosi essentially orphaned while still a minor. His stepmother was given guardianship, however she cared too little to exercise it. Thus, Mandlenkosi acted as if emancipated. He inherited almost 400 grand from her in liquid cash.

In 2006, Mandlenkosi graduated from secondary school and moved on to community college.

Career[]

In 2001, Mandlenkosi began working as a delivery boy for The Durban Daily, making 4$ an hour.

In 2006, Mandlenkosi received a raise of 3 dollars on the hour.

In 2008, Mandlenkosi began an apprenticeship at Duplooy Durban, an auto shop, for a salary of over 10 grand a year.
