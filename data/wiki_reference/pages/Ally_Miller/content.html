Allison Miller (April 25, 1933 - June 9, 2021) was an American associate of the yakuza family Takayama, politician and school worker.

Early years and education[]

Miller was concieved on her parents' honeymoon in Jacksonville. Her mother was Nevaeh Miller (1898 - 1991), a trucker who made deliveries across the country for poverty victims of the Great Depression. Her father was Dylan Miller (1889 - 1965), a cameraman who worked on silent films of the late 1910s and early 1920s. Ally had an older brother, Jackie (1928 - 1947), a travel associate for Jacksonville Travel service. Jackie died from a cocaine overdose during his first year on the job. Due to missing records, it is uncertain if the overdose was accidental or intentional.

Service in the Marines and factory work[]

In 1957, Miller enlisted in the United States Marine Corps. She was enjoying her life as Marine at first; her pay was decent, and she was good friend with her fellow Marines. But one night, she had a bad dream about being ordered to gun down possible enemies, only to learn that they were civillians. She realized that this was a possibility should America enter the Vietnam War. She told her commanding officer and doctors about this, but her superior officers did not believe in the reality of her premonitions and ordered her not to say anything else to anyone.

By 1958, Miller could handle the nightmares no more. On one early morning, when everyone else was asleep, she deserted her platoon and came home to her family. The next day, she started working at the Cherry Mills factory in order to keep a low profile for as long as possible. She gained the trust of most of her coworkers to keep her secret. She partook in several team building exercises, such as a basketball game in 1961. She was arrested for desertion in 1962, but the charges were dropped due to a lack of jurisdiction and possibly falsified warrant.

Death[]

Miller died from a stroke on June 9, 2021 at age 88. She is survived by her three children and three grandchildren.
