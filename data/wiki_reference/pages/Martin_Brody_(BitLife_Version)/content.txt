Martin Brody
(June 18, 1932 - June 20, 2025) was an American police officer, fisherman and sea explorer from Amity Island, New England. During the summer of 1974, he, along with some oceanic friends, would embark on the legendary shark hunt that would serve as the basis for the thriller novel
Jaws
, and its subsequent groundbreaking film adaptation by Steven Spielberg.

Early and personal life
[

Brody was born on Amity Island to Heather (1895 - 1986) and Maxwell "Max" Brody (1895 - 1979). His mother was a hairdresser, and his father was a grocer and fisher who had served in the Navy in World War I. He had a little sister named Ebony (1934 - 2019), a jewelry saleswoman, a pet hamster named Snow (1936 - 1941), and his best friend was Clarence Doyle (1931 - 2019), a firefighter.
...
In 1942, Brody had his first kiss with Viviane Spielberg (no official relation to Steven Spielberg). He asked her out shortly after that, but she rejected him at first. He asked again the next year, and she still said no. Finally, in 1944, in 7th grade, Martin asked her one last time, and she said yes. After ten years of dating, they married in 1954. Vivianne would go on to become a photographer. Brody and Spielberg had six children and sixteen grandchildren together:
