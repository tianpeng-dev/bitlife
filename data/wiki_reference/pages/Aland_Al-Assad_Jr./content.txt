Origins
[
]
Aland was born into the prestigious Al-Assad Family on March 3, 1992 with great joy. A day before Aland's birth, the boy's father,
Aland al-Assad
(Sr.), claimed to experience a vision of his eldest son saving the members of the Assadian Kingdom of the Realm, at the cost of his own life. Aland Sr. described his eldest son's birth as a "miracle that I could've never witnessed" and named his son in honour of himself. His mother, Maria Al-Assad, was overjoyed at the sight of a baby boy, which signaled the arrival of a new "Crown Prince" of the Assadian Kingdom of the Realm.
Aland Jr. was born and raised in Brazil, but when his father got a one-in-a-lifetime opportunity to play for Arsenal FC, Aland and his siblings moved to Hull in the United Kingdom, and became a natrualized British citizen. Aland was often teased in school for his dual citizenship for Brazil and the UK. However, despite the constant teasing, Aland still managed to infiltrate the school's student council, becoming the Vice President of the Student Council. He graduated with flying colours at the end of his senior year at high school. He was a member of the university's soccer team. He graduated from university with a degree in history.
British Army Service
[
]
Aland briefly joined the British Army with his childhood best friend, Damian Darling, immediately after the pair finished university. Aland has admitted that the pair, "hadn't really done much in the Army aside from shooting practices and grenade throwing" in his memoir, "Aland Al-Assad: Volume One". Aland was honourably discharged when he achieved the rank of Captain at the age of 26 in order to live a simple family life without the troubles of war.
Later Life & Political Career
[
]
Aland got a job as a History Teacher at Hull Public Schools immediately when he resigned from the Army. He mainly got the job because he had a degree in history, and the fact that his younger brother, Sartaj, was the Principal of Hull Public Schools. He spent 49 years as a teacher until he quit his job on the day of 72nd birthday.
Already known in the Hull Department of Education as the "History Teacher from Heaven", Aland was elected as Mayor of Hull, aged 72, representing the Labour Party. However, his tenure as Mayor would unfortunately would be short.
Death
[
]
Aland had a vision that his heir, John Paul, would die a tragic and painful death. That said death would be falling off a cliff while taking pictures of said cliff. John Paul's death would be bound to happen; however, Aland could change that... if he gave his own life up. Not wanting his heir and favourite son to die at an early age, Aland agreed to give up his life. In his final page of his diary, he wrote, "The heir shall live. All hail my Son, John Paul Yousef Al-Assad, as your new Ruler and Saviour. God Bless." Aland was found dead in his home, aged 73. Many residents of Hull were heartbroken over the loss of their mayor, especially since he died while in office. His funeral was public, with about 60,000 Hull residents attending his funeral, along with family members of his, in remembrance of the late Mayor. With Aland's death, the vision that Aland Sr. witnessed had become true.
Legacy
[
]
Aland's son and heir, John Paul, succeeded his father as King John I. In his newly introduced
Doctrina Regis Ioannis
(lit. Doctrine of King John), King John I proclaimed his father as a "Saviour to the People" and called him, "St. Aland the Sacrificer" affectionately. King John I was also known to publicly praise his father's military service, commitment to providing educational services to British children, and unwavering love to the people of Hull by serving as their Mayor.
