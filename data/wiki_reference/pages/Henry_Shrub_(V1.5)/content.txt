Henry Michael Shrub
(October 21, 1904 - February 11, 1982), also known by his initials,
HMS
, was an American lawyer, judge, fighter pilot and politician who served as the President of the United States from his election in 1980 until his death in his second year in office in 1982. Shrub also served as Mayor of New Haven from 1944 to 1952, the Governor of Connecticut from 1952 to 1956, and as School Board Director from 1960 to 1968. As a lawyer, he was most prominent with the firm Fudge & Duffy, at which he worked from 1934 until his mayoral election in 1944. He also worked with Osborne Associates for several years. As a judge, he served as a magistrate for the United States District Court for the District of Connecticut for twelve years until his victorious presidential election.
