Jayla Sprinkle
(2007-after December 11, 2094) was an American basketball player, actress, and burglar.

Early life (2007-2029)
[
]
Sprinkle was born in Los Angeles, California. She was the only child of Tom Harris (né Sprinkle, 1978-2018) and his first wife Farah Packard.
In 2018, Tom Harris married
Pamela Harris
.
In 2021, Sprinkle enrolled at Celestial Preparatory School. She was on the basketball team for one year.
In 2025, Sprinkle graduated from high school. She enrolled at the University of California. She was a member of Alpha Delta Pi and played basketball for the Bruins.
Sports career (2029-2048)
[
]
In 2029, Sprinkle graduated from college with a Bachelor of Science in Psychology. She joined the Los Angeles Flickers as a point guard.
In 2041, Sprinkle was released by the Flickers and joined the Connecticut Superstars.
In 2048, Sprinkle was released by the Superstars.
First prison sentence (2048-2052)
[
]
In 2048, Sprinkle was caught burgling a gym teacher’s house and attempting to run from the police. She hired a public defender and plead not guilty, but was convicted and sentenced to four years in Los Angeles State Prison.

Second, third, and fourth prison sentences (2072-2094)
[
]
In 2072, Sprinkle was caught burgling a referee’s house and attempting to run from the police. She hired a public defender and plead not guilty, but was convicted and sentenced to eight years in Williamson Institute.
In 2080, Sprinkle was released from prison. Less than a year later, she was caught burgling a newspaper editor’s house and attempting to run from the police. She hired a public defender and plead not guilty, but was convicted and sentenced to 12 years in Williamson Institute.
In 2088, Sprinkle appealed her third conviction to the California Court of Appeal for the Second District and hired Chapman & Ryder to represent her. Her conviction was reversed and she was released. Less than a year later, she was caught burgling a socialite’s house and attempting to run from the police. She hired a public defender and plead not guilty, but was convicted and sentenced to 12 years in Williamson Institute.
Death
[
]
On December 11, 2094, Sprinkle died of old age in prison. Nobody in her family attended her funeral.
Marriages, relationships, and children
[
]
From 2024 to 2025, Sprinkle dated a student named Chauncey Mayfield (2007-2088). They had a son, Benjamin Mayfield (2025-after 2097).
In 2025, Sprinkle married a famous CEO named Clark Cluckerberg (1996-2025), who was murdered less than a year later. She had two stepdaughters, Stephanie (2023-2034) and
Grace Cluckerberg
, from Cluckerberg’s prior marriage to a woman named Tasty Hooker-Fish.
After Cluckerberg was murdered, Sprinkle and Mayfield started dating again.
In 2088, Sprinkle started dating a museum worker named Gilbert Rose (2041-after December 11, 2094).
