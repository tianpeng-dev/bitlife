Alia Laurel Basin (née Pickford; born September 15, 1970) is a renowned British singer-songwriter. She is known for her soulful voice and poignant lyrics. Raised in a musically inclined family, Alia was exposed to various genres from a young age, which influenced her eclectic style. Basin began writing songs in her teenage years, drawing inspiration from both her personal life, childhood, struggles, and the world around her. After graduating from Highschool in 1988, Alia signed a contract with Big Load Records, reportedly worth $3M.

Since the 1990s, Basin has released multiple platinum & diamond albums. Her debut album, "Alia Pickford" (1989), was released when she was just 19 and sold over 500,000 copies. The album yielded 2 top 10 singles, including her smash hit debut single "I Definitely Did Just Come Here To Love". Alia's ability to blend genres and craft songs that resonate deeply with listeners has led to widespread commercial success and critical acclaim. In 1992, Alia released her eponymous second studio album. The electro pop album marked a significant departure from her debut with it's genre-bending sound. Released to critical acclaim, the album spawned a record-breaking 5 #1 singles, including the lead single "Ears to Break". "Alia" was eventually certified Diamond by the RIAA, going on to sell upwards of 22 million copies.

During this time, Alia began a relationship with supermodel Dalton Basin. The pair dated for 9 years, before eventually tying the knot in 2001. In 1994, Basin and her then boyfriend Dalton, welcomed their first child, Carlos Basin, into the world. Alia released her 3rd studio album, 'Stage 0', in 1995. The album signaled a departure from her previous electro pop inspired project. Stage 0 was certified 4x platinum by the RIAA in 1997, and has sold over 9,000,000 copies today in the US. In 1996, she gave birth to her second son, Isaac Basin. The star revealed she was diagnosed with post-partum disorder following his birth. 1997 was a quiet year on the recording front for Alia. She embarked on her 15-week "Stage 0 Tour" across 45 cities in the US. The tour also visited parts of Europe, Asia, and Australia. A South American leg was planned but later cancelled for unknown reasons.

The following year Alia released her 4th studio album, "Knockout". The space-age influenced album spawned 6 top 10 singles and went on to sell 10 million copies in the US & UK. Basin cited artists such as Madonna, Mary Mayo, and Les Baxter as her biggest inspirations. The same year she embarked her 2nd concert tour, "Cherish Me World Tour '99". Critics praised her for her showmanship, vocals, and choreography. The tour grossed $56M in revenue. In 2001, Basin suffered a first trimester miscarriage, the star admitted to using cocaine to cope with the loss. Later that year, her 5th studio album, "Preteen Aspirations" was released. The album continued her space-age sound first shown on her 1995 album, and went on to sell over 21 million copies. It is now regarded as one of the best albums ever released in the 21st century.

Her 6th and 7th studio albums followed themes of motherhood, the afterlife, and enlightenment. In 2005, Basin was honored with the Brit Award for Outstanding Contribution to Music for her hard work in the industry. In 2006, Alia gave birth to her 3rd child and first daughter, Molly Basin. The 36 year old star described the moment as a career defining point in her life. 3 years later, she returned the studio and released "Chemical Reaction". The album peaked at #3 on the Billboard 200 and sold 1,148,000 copies, becoming her 2nd lowest selling studio album. The star's 4th child and 2nd daughter, Imogen Basin, was born the following year. In 2012, Basin released her first compilation album, "Alia Basin: The Immaculate Collection". It sold over 23 million copies and was certified 23x platinum by the RIAA.

In 2015, Alia took to twitter to announce that she had plans to release another studio album. '9' was released 4 months later and sold 4,000,000 copies, with many citing it as a return to form for the singer. Throughout the years Basin released a few more studio albums with all of them surpassing 5 million copies sold.

Alia is one of the world's best selling artists & female artists, with sales of over 200 million copies worldwide. She was named the highest ranking female artist of the 20th century in the UK, 4th biggest female artist of the 21st century in the US, and one of the best selling artists of the 1990s and 2000s. She also has 8 diamond studio albums, the most among any British female act.

Early Life[]

Alia Laurel Pickford was born on September 15th, 1970 in Hull, United Kingdom, to an English mother, Georgia Pickford (b. 1952) and a Welsh father, Percy Pickford (1938-2024). Her mother worked as an Apprentice Makeup Artist, while her father worked as a forensic scientist. Pickford recalled not having a close relationship with her father as a child, due to his demanding work schedule. During her youth, Pickford often sat & watched performances on TV, imagining it was her on the screen. This led to her parents signing her up for vocal lessons at the age of 8, with her attending a performing arts school by the age of 14.
