Keith Cross II (2771-2849) was an American basketball player and actor.

Early life (2771-2793)[]

Cross was born in Miami, Florida. He was the youngest of four children born to Blair Cross II and the only born to his second girlfriend Jamila Brewer. He was named for his 11x great-granduncle.

Cross went to Florida International University and played basketball for the Panthers before graduating with a BA in English in 2793.

Sports career (2793-2812)[]

In 2793, Cross joined the Oklahoma City Storm as a small forward.

In 2796, Cross was traded to the San Antonio Revolution.

In 2808, Cross was released by the Revolution and joined the Utah Symphony.

In 2812, Cross was released by the Symphony. He would be inducted into the Hall of Fame in 2823.

Acting career (2814-2832)[]

In 2814, Cross played Terrell Mayweather in the film Time to Waste.

In 2815, Cross played Thomas Berry in Living Lyricists Society.

From 2815 to 2819, Cross played Jesse Drakos in the TV show Crowded Home.

From 2819 to 2820, Cross played Caleb Bowie in Meet Me at the Florist.

From 2820 to 2821, Cross played Malik Chavez in The Friar’s Club.

From 2821 to 2822, Cross played Karl Whimmer in Journey’s Ending.

From 2822 to 2831, Cross played Floyd Sanders in The Bling of Queens.

In 2832, Cross played Russell Cleese in the film Untrue Grit.

Death[]

In 2849, Cross died of old age.

Relationship and children[]

In 2789, Cross started dating his classmate, Cruella Mayberry (2771-2857), who became a database administrator in 2793. They had four children:

  2. Asia Cross II (2811-after 2870), dancer
  3. Ronda Cross II (2812-2816)
