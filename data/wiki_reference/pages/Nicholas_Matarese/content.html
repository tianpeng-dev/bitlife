Nicholas "The Executioner" Matarese (October 14th, 1913 - May 4th, 2026) was an American crime boss who was head of the Chicago Outfit from 1972 to 2020, silently ruling the midwestern mob underworld with an iron fist for five decades.

Early Years[]

Born in the Near West Side of Chicago in 1913 to immigrants from Naples, Italy, Nicky's family was incredibly poor and underprivileged, enduring Italian discrimination and a lack of food on the table. Growing up in these types of conditions grew an ambition inside of Nick, wanting to better himself and his family however he could, as long as it would help their situation. With this, he became a thief at only 8 years old, stealing jewelry and expensive merchandise, selling it at a much lower price to trusted buyers.

As he began to get older and reach his teenage years, he'd dive into higher-risk money-making schemes such as burglaries and car thefts. It was around this time when he would start to gain the attention of local gangsters who operated in the area, carefully making connections that he knew wouldn't fuck him over later. As his status grew in the neighborhood, many of the older gangsters realized not only did he have a sharp mind,

but he never got pinched.

The Rise[]

In 1931, Nick would get put on record with Chicago Outfit member Carmine Albano, a Sicilian born gangster who was originally apart of the Black Hand. With the help of Carmine, Nick spread out his wings and started to get into extortion and bookmaking, running numbers for a large gambling operation that Carmine owned. Over time, Nick grew a new reputation as not just clever but as a gangster who knew how to intimidate, when to beat, how to beat, and how to get away with it all.

He would remain as an associate in the family well into his 20s until 1943 when he was proposed for membership by Carmine. By this time, Nick already had around 4 to 5 hits under his belt, but they were all personal hits for Carmine that were never spoken about to the higher ups. Due to this, Nick was ordered to kill a local businessman who owed too much and knew too much about the family. The hit was successful as Nick stabbed the man in the stomach repeatedly before throwing him into a lake. With this, he was formally inducted into the family by outfit boss Frank Manna.

After being made, Nicholas was gifted Carmine's bookmaking operation and became in-charge of a large gambling circle that would only grow bigger as he gained connections from out of state, reeling in thirsty gamblers and other mob soldiers from other families. It wasn't long until he had become a millionaire at the age of 31, just a year after being made. Only 8 years later, he would be given his own crew and become an official caporegime. That same year, Nicky would branch out to Las Vegas and make connections with big shot casino owners, partnering up with them while skimming their profits. What were they gonna do, call the police or some shit?

In 1957, Nicky would attend the infamous Apalachin disaster held at New York gangster Joe Barbara's home with Frank Manna, boss of the Outfit. Luckily for him and the group he came with, including the boss, managed to get the hell out of there by running into the woods along with dozens of other gangsters from families across the country.

Two years later in September of 1959, there was an attempt on Nicky's life while walking to his car from a restaurant he had a partnership in. Ten shots rang out from a black sedan, none of them hitting Nicky but landing into his car as it shattered his windshield and pierced through the metal. The incident was quickly resolved a few weeks later as it was revealed that a much smaller rival family had attempted to kill him in order to take over his rackets. The perpetrators were both decapitated by Nicky himself in a basement just outside of Chicago, then leaving their heads in the trunk of a sedan. Nicky's reputation as a ruthless gangster was rising significantly, but so was the heat from law enforcement. He'd make the decision to lay low for a while, simply letting the money flow in from his rackets.

In 1962, Nicky was promoted to underboss after the passing of Dario Toscano. Just a few years earlier, Nicky's mentor Carmine Albano had been crowned the new boss of the Outfit after the assassination of Frank Manna. Nick and Carmine were finally the heads of the family, an untouchable duo who would reign for ten years until Carmine's peaceful passing in 1972.

The Boss[]

Nicholas was crowned boss after a landslide vote from The Commission, becoming the most powerful man in Chicago and the midwestern United States. Even before becoming boss, Nicky was already considered the strongest mob figure in the region due to his reputation and multi-million-dollar operations.

Nicky's income would only triple from here, making him one of the wealthiest LCN bosses in the nation bringing in tens of millions of dollars a year. Not much is known about his reign as boss due to his low-profile lifestyle, living in a simple three-bedroom home and driving around in used cars. It would be rumored that he once forced a made member to kill his own son, only adding onto his legend of ruthlessness.

In 1980, a train coming in from Canada carrying near thirty million dollars' worth of jewelry was intercepted and robbed by multiple masked individuals who stole all of the jewels and disappeared into the night, never to be seen by law enforcement. It is said that Nicky had orchestrated the heist, taking a large chunk of the cash after the jewels were eventually sold off.

Nick would rule the Chicago Outfit for forty more years until he eventually stepped down at the incredible age of 107 due to his declining health. He eventually would pass away in 2026 surrounded by his loved ones in the comfort of his own home, dying at 112 and never spending a day of his life in prison.

Personal life[]

Nicky was actually a pretty decent student, taking part in a boxing club and becoming a well-known fighter in the school. A few years later, he bought a modest 3 bedroom 2-bathroom home in a Chicago suburb after marrying his lover, Karen. They would have two kids named Virginia and William, both of them would grow up to become successful in finance. In Nick's later years he would regularly be seen mowing the lawn of his home, or even reading the newspaper right on his front porch.
