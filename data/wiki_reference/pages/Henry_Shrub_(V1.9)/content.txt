Henry Michael Shrub
(October 21, 1864 - February 11, 1942), also known by his initials,
HMS
, was an American lawyer, judge and politician who served as the President of the United States from his election in 1940 until his death in his second year in office in 1942. Shrub also served as Mayor of New Haven from 1904 to 1912, the Governor of Connecticut from 1912 to 1916, and as School Board Director from 1920 to 1928. As a lawyer, he was most prominent with the firm Fudge & Duffy, at which he worked from 1894 until his mayoral election in 1904. He also worked with Osborne Associates for several years. As a judge, he served as a magistrate for the United States District Court for the District of Connecticut for twelve years until his victorious presidential election.
