Latrell Sangeloa (1990 - 2016), also known by his stage name The Linguistic Latte, was an American magician. He was the younger brother of actress, model and solider Ivy Sangeloa.

Early life[]

Latrell was born in San Angelo, Texas, the fourth and last child of Henry (1923 - 2010) and Mikayla Sangeloa (born 1951). His older brothers are Gaylord (born 1969) and Winston (born 1982), and Ivy was his older sister. Despite being the youngest of his siblings, who had been born decades before him, he got along with them very well, and loved them all the same.

Circus career[]

After graduating high school, Latrell jumped at the chance to join the circus. He started his position as a magician for Abacus Circus between 2008 and 2009, around the time Ivy retired from the Air Force and began her acting career.

Personal life and death[]

Unlike most of his older siblings except for Winston, Latrell never married or had any children of his own but was a loving and caring uncle to his siblings' children. He would teach many of them how to do magic so they could be inspired to follow their dreams when they grew up.

In 2016, Latrell was playing some golf. Suddenly, at one moment, he was struck in the head with a ball that flew at him very fast. He was rushed to the hospital, but he didn't make it. His whole family attended his funeral. Ivy took the loss the hardest, even consuming valium during the production of her latest film Call it Fashion. The golfer that hit the ball that took Latrell's life, whose name was never formally released to the public, pleaded guilty to involuntary manslaughter and was sentenced to several years in prison with 3 to 6 months' probation, having taken a plea deal to avoid a life sentence.
