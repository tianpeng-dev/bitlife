Theodore Frederick Bunker
(March 30, 1867 - May 4, 1952), often referred to by his initials
TFB
, was an American lobbyist and politician who served as the Mayor of Kingsville from 1931 to 1939 and the Governor of Texas from 1943 to 1947. He unsuccessfully ran for the presidency three times. He lobbied for many successful businesses, including Pacific Banking Corporation, Unity Fashions and Corona Mills.
Prior to his lobbying and political careers, Teddy Bunker was a leading Naval commander in a service spanning from 1885 to 1919, with a gap between 1895 and 1899 filled by enrollment in university and the Naval Academy. A pinnacle moment in his service was in 1916 during World War One when, on a deployment mission in Uruguayan waters, he sustained massive injuries from a mine explosion.

Bunker was born in the Texas city that would later become today's Kingsville, to librarian Jacob Bunker (1835 - 1914) and insurance agent Darlene Bunker (1838 - 1914). He had a pointer named Freya (died 1877) and two sisters, Olivia (1865 - 1949) and Desiree (1873 - ????). Jacob and Darlene divorced in 1875, when Teddy was eight years old.
From an early age, becoming President was one of Bunker's main desires. At Illumina Middle School, he joined the politics club to begin his training to get a seat. He also took up foreign language, environment, chess and creative writing. Bunker enjoyed chess, comparing the pieces to naval ships in a battle. Bunker attended Neptune Preparatory School, class of 1885. He wanted to join the student council in hopes of practicing the role of a president, but he was rejected. He rejoined the clubs of chess, environment, foreign language, and also took up key and yearbook. In his junior year, Bunker became the president of the foreign language club. He took this opportunity to practice the role and holding the title.
