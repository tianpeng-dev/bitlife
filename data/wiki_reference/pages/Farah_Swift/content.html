Farah Swift (also spelled Farrah; September 9, 1961 - April 18, 2024) was an American photographer and convicted felon. She took pictures for the Wichita Times for nearly twenty years, establishing herself as an icon in Kansas' news industry. After a long history of legal troubles that originated in her late teens, Swift spent the last two decades of her life in prison for assault, larceny, and felony escape.

Early life[]

Farah was born in Wichita, Kansas with Arabic and Persian ancestry to receptionist and World War II veteran Vincent "Vinnie" Swift (1920 - 1992) and firefighter Kaelynn McClure (1922 - 2007). She had an older brother, Pierre (born 1960), a restaurateur who would eventually become the assistant manager of Dylan's Diner. Pierre married fellow restaurateur Laila Chen (born 1952) in 2002. Farah had two pets growing up, a vizsla named Izzy and a goldfish named Butch.

Swift had many aspirations as a child, such as photography, dancing, and others such as acting, having seen many movies with her parents and brother. One of her favorite films growing up was Mary Poppins, which she saw in the theater at the age of three. She tried to go to university for dancing, but she was rejected. Legend has it that her rejection from dance school prompted her to begin her spree of crimes.

Photography career[]

Swift was hired as an apprentice photographer in 1984 for Wichita Times. She worked for them for three years until her arrest in 1987, and then for another fourteen years (after which she had escaped from jail in the same year) until her arrest in 2001. In her 17-year career, Swift took many pictures of historical moments from the 1980s to the early 2000s.

Personal life[]

Swift met her boyfriend, Anthony "Tony" Hernandez (born 1964) in 1987. They never married, but they raised a son, DeShawn (born 1991). Tony left her in 2008, a year after her mother died, while she was serving her sentence. DeShawn began his career as a receptionist between 2009 and 2010, accepting a job at Jade Electronics. In 2018, DeShawn married hairdresser Vivianne Dover (born 1992). Swift was allowed temporary supervised release to attend her son's wedding.

Farah had various interests, which were psychologically connected to her motives for her crimes. For music, she grew up on music of the counterculture era, but she also enjoyed modern music in her later years, with one of her favorite 21st century artists being Taylor Swift (no relation).

Legal issues[]

After graduating high school and turning 18 in 1979, Swift wanted to make a quick buck by means of vigilantism. She attempted to pickpocket a gangster, but the plan backfired when he got the attention of a nearby cop. Swift was charged, tried, and convicted of larcency and sentenced to a year in prison. After getting out in 1980, she worked as a server at Diego's Restaurant for two years, aiming to pay off her probation expenses. In 1982, Swift tried to pickpocket another gangster, only to get the same results from the previous attempt. This time, she served two years in prison.

In 1987, a stranger called Swift's father a turd burglar. This made her snap and assault him, snapping his anus. The stranger fought back by attacking her with a golf club, whacking her back. Swift was arrested and charged with assault. Since she now had some money from her photography career, she was able to hire Taffe & Associates to represent her in court. She was found guilty and sentenced to three years in prison. Swift hired T&A again to appeal her sentence, but to no avail. She escaped from jail and was able to get her job back like nothing had happened. After Swift's 2001 conviction, the hiring manager from 1987 was sued for aiding an escaped convict. They were also nearly fired, but they managed to voluntarily resign before the official termination process could begin.

For the next fourteen years, Swift would be on the run from the authorities, sparking one of the biggest statewide manhunts in Kansas history, even being listed on the FBI's Ten Most Wanted Fugitives every once and a while. Eventually, she was captured in 2001. Her new sentence was 11 years. She tried to appeal with the help of Baker & Owens, only to lose. She tried to escape and failed, resulting in an eight year extension on her sentence. In 2011, after inciting a riot amongst other prisoners, she tried to escape again, to no avail, thus adding another eight years.

Death[]

Swift was diagnosed with heart disease in 2023. She died in prison a year later on April 18, 2024 at the age of 62, a mere four years before she was scheduled to be released. Her funeral was attended by DeShawn and Pierre.
