Ernesto Rivera (1942-2026) was a Spanish-Japanese pianist and politican.

Early life (1942-1960)[]

Ernesto was born in Madrid, Spain. His parents were Alba Gil and Gonzalo Rivera. He had an older sister named Emma.

In 1948, he started taking piano lessons. He started working as a pianist in 1960.

Pianist career (1960-1995)[]

In 1961, he realeased his first album, Ezequiel y Bob muertos, which went gold. In 1962, this album went platinun.

In 1965, he realeaded his second album, afterlife, which went platinum. In 1968, this album went double platinum.

In 1968, he realeased his third album, Porcus, which went platinum. In 1978, this album went double platinum.

In 1972, he realeased his fourth album, La cena en la Krab, which went double platinum.

In 1975, he realeased his fifth album, Temporada 2. In 1976, this album went gold.

In 1994, he was realeased after the end of his contract. He stopped being famous in 1995. That same year, he emigrated to Japan and started a career as a president.

President career (1999-2007)[]

In 1995, he decided to run for school boars director, but lost the election.

In 1999, he decided to run fot school board election again. This time, he won the election and completed his first term in 2003. Then, he decided to run for mayor of Hiroshima. He won the election and completed his first term on 2007. Then, he decided to run for governor of Hiroshima, both in 2007 and in 2011, but, in the two cases, the result was the same: he was unable to make it onto the ballot.

Death[]

He died peacefully in 2026, at the age of 84 years.
