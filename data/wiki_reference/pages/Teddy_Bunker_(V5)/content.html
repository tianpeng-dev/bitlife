Theodore Frederick Bunker (March 30, 1917 - May 4, 2002), often referred to by his initials TFB, was an American lobbyist and politician who served as the Mayor of Kingsville from 1981 to 1989 and the Governor of Texas from 1992 to 1997. He unsuccessfully ran for the presidency three times. He lobbied for many successful businesses, including Pacific Banking Corporation, Unity Fashions and Corona Mills.

Prior to his lobbying and political careers, Teddy Bunker was a leading Naval commander in a service spanning from 1935 to 1969, with a gap between 1945 and 1949 filled by enrollment in university and the Naval Academy. A pinnacle moment in his service was in 1966 during the Vietnam War when, on a deployment in Uruguayan waters, he sustained massive injuries from a mine explosion.

Early life and education[]

Teddy was born in Kingsville, Texas to librarian Jacob Bunker (1885 - 1964) and insurance agent Darlene Bunker (1888 - 1964). He had a pointer named Freya (died 1927) and two sisters, Olivia (1915 - 1999) and Desiree (born 1923). Jacob and Darlene divorced in 1925, when Teddy was eight years old. Several sources state that he was named after Teddy Roosevelt.

From an early age, becoming President was one of Bunker's main desires. At Illumina Middle School, he joined the politics club to begin his training to get a seat. He also took up foreign language, environment, chess and creative writing. Bunker enjoyed chess, comparing the pieces to naval ships in a battle. Bunker attended Neptune Preparatory School, class of 1935. He wanted to join the student council in hopes of practicing the role of a president, but he was rejected. He rejoined the clubs of chess, environment, foreign language, and also took up key and yearbook. In his junior year, Bunker became the president of the foreign language club. He took this opportunity to practice the role and holding the title.
