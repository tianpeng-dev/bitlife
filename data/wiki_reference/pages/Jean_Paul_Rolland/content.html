Jean Paul Rolland (2940-after 2996) was a French-Australian rugby player and jeweler.

Early life (2940-2962)[]

Rolland was born in Lyon, France. His parents were Lilou Henry and her ex-boyfriend Antoine Rolland.

In 2943, Rolland emigrated to Manchester, England.

In 2958, Rolland emigrated back to Lyon and enrolled at Les Chartreux. He was on the rugby team. He graduated in 2962 with a Licentiate in Psychology.

Sports career (2962-2977)[]

In 2962, Rolland emigrated to Brisbane, Australia and joined the Brisbane Horses as a scrum half. He won a Most Improved Player award while on the team.

In 2967, Rolland was released by the Horses and joined the Newcastle Spearmen. He won Most Improved Player, a championship, and two Player of the Year awards while on the team.

In 2977, Rolland retired.

Jewelry career (2977-2996)[]

In 2978, Rolland became a sales associate at Reid Brothers Jewelry.

In 2984, Rolland was promoted to assistant manager.

In 2990, Rolland was promoted to store manager.

Rolland was alive in 2996, but his death date is unknown.

Relationship and child[]

From 2958 to 2959, Rolland dated a student named Faustine Mathieu (2940-after 2996). They had a son, Victor Rolland.
