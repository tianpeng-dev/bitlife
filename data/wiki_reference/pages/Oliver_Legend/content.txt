Oliver Joseph James Legend J.D. Honours
(November 8, 1906 - June 4, 2024) was an American lawyer, businessman, politician, media personality, and supercentenarian who served as President of the United States from 1990 to 1994 and again from 1998 to 2002. A brilliant, assertive, and hardworking man, Legend graduated from law school in 1931 at the top of his class, and worked as a lawyer for twenty years. He then founded a food truck business called "The Sunflower Food Truck" in 1951, and later sold the business in 1964. In 1966, Legend began his political career, serving as School Board Director of the Salt Lake City School Board from 1966 to 1974, Mayor of Salt Lake City from 1974 to 1978, Governor of Utah from 1978 to 1986, and President of the United States from 1990 to 1994, and again from 1998 to 2002. Legend was remembered as a strong, effective, and assertive leader. Following his retirement from politics, Legend gained fame for his longevity, and of June 2024 was the oldest lived American-born man ever.

Early life
[
]
Oliver Joseph James Legend was born on November 8, 1906 in Salt Lake City, Utah to Angelica Legend (1871-1947), a real estate agent. In a 1970 interview, Legend said that his mother had a drunken hookup with a stranger, and discovered she was pregnant with him, so his father is unknown. In 1907, his mother married Brock Ortiz, a cameraman with a child, Ciara Ortiz (1898-1980), his new stepsister. Legend's mother changed her name to Angelica Ortiz. During his early childhood, he disliked his stepfather and so did his stepfather, and after he threatened to abandon him at a store in 1914, he told his mother. A year later, Angelica and Brock divorced.
Legend started elementary school at Shaggy McGregor Elementary School in 1911. During his time in school, he was a very studious student, and got high grades. He was often bullied, so he called on his ex-stepsister Ciara to help him, and she would usually beat up the bully. In 1917, he started middle school at St. Tyreese's Middle School, where he joined the Politics Club, and participated in political debate, mainly about World War 1 and America's role. In 1920, he started high school at Kim-Affleck High School, and joined his school's football team. He was cut from the team two years later as his new job as a pool cleaner interfered with his ability to stay on the team.
In 1924, Legend graduated from high school, and applied to university to study criminal justice, and was accepted. After his scholarship application was rejected and his mother refused to pay his tuition, he took out a student loan. During his time at university, Legend joined the fraternity Beta Theta Pi (βΘΠ). Legend graduated from university in 1928, at the top of his class and was the valedictorian. He then applied to law school, and was offered a full scholarship which he accepted. He graduated from law school in 1931.
Legal career (1931-1951)
[
]
Legend was hired by a fellow member of Beta Theta Pi as a Junior Associate at Stringer & Associates shortly after graduating and got to work. He rose through the ranks, eventually being promoted to Partner in 1943. He quickly became a respected lawyer, and argued several cases before prominent American courts, including six that he argued before the US Supreme Court. When the US declared war on Japan, and entered Word War 2 in 1941, Legend successfully argued before the Supreme Court that he would be more helpful at home than fighting war and was granted a legal injunction making him exempt from the draft, a rare occurrence. The case was not made public, and it didn't make Legend famous at all, yet he was happy about the result as he was never a soldier.
His time at his legal profession caused him to partially neglect his family, but he tried to make time for them. During his twenty-year career, Legend also wrote around 20 legal treatises that revolutionized the study of law, and published a law textbook, which he claimed was the textbook he wished he had when he was in law school. The textbook was a success among his legal contemporaries.
In 1951, Legend retired from Stringer & Associates after working for them for 20 years. He began collecting a pension of $69,394 per year. He had political aspirations, but he realized that he needed more money to enter politics. He then announced that he was starting a business.
Business career (1951-1964)
[
]
In 1951, shortly after retiring from law, Legend founded The Sunflower Food Truck, a food truck business, with $266,000. During his time as CEO, he took a salary of $18,800 per year. He first introduced a BBQ brisket that he named King BBQ Brisket. The brisket sold well most of the time, but there were times when it struggled to sell. On the advice of his business consultant, Legend introduced a new funnel cake in 1953 that he named Mama's Funnel Cake. The cake ended up being the company's most popular product, but there were times were it struggled to sell. Again, Legend worked so much at his business, 60 hours a week, that he neglected his family, and didn't spend much time with them. He tried to make time for them, but it was hard to make time to spend time with them.
Legend nearly ended his business career and political aspirations in 1963, as he cooked the books for his business by adding $13,300 to The Sunflower Food Truck's reported income. He was caught, and was arrested for securities fraud. Legend hired Witherspoon & Chu to represent him in court, and even represented himself a bit. He denied that he'd cooked the books, and if he had, he claimed that it was an accounting error. Legend won his case and was found not guilty. He later described the incident as a huge scare for him, and said that after that, he was more careful. However, a year later, he was arrested again for securities fraud, but was found not guilty again. Legend originally wanted to sell the business in 1961, after he'd been working there for ten years, but it was hard for him to sell the business. In 1964, he sold the business to a college professor for $403,000. While he profited from the business, he said that it would've been better for him to continue his legal career.
Political career (1966-2002)
[
]
Legend spent two years after retiring from business with his family and friends. He spent time with them, reconnected with them, and repaired his relationships with them. However, in 1966, he decided the time had come for him to run for politics, and eventually achieve his lifelong dream of becoming US president. He decided to start out by running for School Board Director of the Salt Lake City School Board. Legend budgeted $64,000 towards his campaign, and ran against 71-year-old Marshall Rojas. Legend defeated Rojas and won the election, and got to work as School Board Director.
Director of the Salt Lake City School Board (1966-1974)
[
]
Legend made revolutionary changes to the Salt Lake City school system. He introduced basic legal studies into the curriculum, and set all the schools to run from Monday to Friday, with five-hour school days. He also introduced new technology into his schools, believing that it would benefit students. In 1970, Legend announced his run for reelection with a $43,000 budget, and faced off against 74-year-old Kiera Ryder. Legend defeated Ryder and won the election, being reelected for a second term. During his second term, he criminalized smoking in his schools, and worked to ensure a better learning environment for his schools. After completing his second term in 1974 and no longer being eligible for reelection, he announced his run for Mayor of Salt Lake City.
Mayor of Salt Lake City (1974-1978)
[
]
Legend budgeted $289,000 towards his campaign, and represented the Democratic Party in the 1974 Mayoral Election. He faced off against the Republican candidate, 78-year-old Reggie Cobbledick. Despite promising to treat Cobbledick with respect, Legend made up quite a few rumours about him, including saying that Cobbledick would poach tigers if given the opportunity. Despite this, Legend defeated Cobbledick, and won the election. He then got to work as Mayor of Salt Lake City.
During his term, Legend acted assertively on targeting crime. He even charged a prominent corporation for its illegal practices and environmental irresponsibility. However, this resulted in an unemployment spike in Salt Lake City. He also introduced more medical scholarships to solve a shortage of doctors. In 1976, an incident occurred in which Legend accidentally said the F-word during a press conference. Legend insisted that he hadn't, and said that he'd said "sofa king", and later clarified that he meant to describe his opponent's laziness as so regal, that he deserved the title of "sofa king" lazy. Very few believed him, and in a 2009 interview, Legend said, "Who am I kidding? You're right, I definitely said it!"
Legend created more low-income housing, which was well received at the time, but the houses were made from asbestos. This later resulted in him being targeted by a 2010 class-action lawsuit, but he was unable to be sued, as he'd secretly created an earmark that gave him legal immunity after leaving office. After finishing his term in 1978, Legend decided to run for reelection, but pulled out of the nomination race, saying that he wanted and needed to go bigger. He announced he was running for Governor of Utah.
Governor of Utah (1978-1986)
[
]
Legend budgeted $350,000 towards his campaign, and represented the Democratic Party. He ran against 61-year-old Chuck Cocker, who was representing the Green Party. Cocker tried to spite Legend by commissioning a billboard featuring his head on a snake's body, but Legend ignored it, saying he wouldn't stoop to his level. Legend easily defeated Cocker and was elected Governor of Utah.
As Governor of Utah, Legend worked tirelessly to improve his state. He'd originally supported a planned growth policy, but after discovering the policy would allow a private gold course to be built on land allocated for affordable housing, he reversed his support. Some accused him of being a flip-flopper. After receiving a report on a potential pandemic, Legend decided to create a task force for it. While the task force was never used during his governorship, remnants of the task force were used to fight the COVID-19 pandemic in Utah, decades later. After completing his first term in 1982, Legend announced his run for reelection.
Legend ran as Democrat again, and budgeted $461,000 towards his campaign. This time, he faced off against 36-year-old Andre Hux, who represented the Green Party. During the campaign, Legend's camping manager quit and joined Hux's team. He was forced to find a new one. Despite this, Legend easily defeated Hux, and was reelected as Governor of Utah. During his second term, he urged his constituents to focus in the environment, and introduced a program to allow students to develop video games with the new computers that were hitting the market. The program didn't take off so well. Legend also built numerous new parks across Utah, and increased the education budget by 30%. Upon finishing his second term Legend then began fundraising for his eventual run for president.
President of the United States, First Presidency (1990-1994)
[
]
Legend spent four years fundraising, to gather enough funds for his presidential run. After feeling he'd earned enough, he announced his presidential campaign, from his house, in 1990. He budgeted $52,073,000 towards his campaign, and represented the Democratic Party. He faced off against Cody Wu, the 52-year-old Green Party candidate. During the campaign, Legend gained notability after he calmly listened to an angry protester at one of his rallies rant, and then calmly responded to each of her complaints. Legend's levelheadedness, and his popularity allowed him to easily win the 1990 Presidential Election, and he became President of the United States. During his inauguration speech, he said that being president was his lifelong dream since he was five.
As president, he passed a series of laws restricting foreign cartels from making drugs in the US, and even spoke directly with officials in Mexico to get the minimum wage there increased. In 1993, Legend threw his shoe at a member of the opposing public during a meeting, after the member threw their shoe at him. Legend was criticized for this by some, and it hurt his approval ratings a bit. After completing his first term, Legend announced his run for reelection, and budgeted $53,030,00 towards his campaign. He faced off against 41-year-old Dwight Washington, the Republican candidate. During the campaign, he took to playing video games to gain the votes of young people, which backfired. Legend lost the election to Washington, and while initially upset, he announced that he would return in four years.
Between the Presidencies (1994-1998)
[
]
Between his first and second presidencies, he fund-raised to gain more money, and spent time with his family. He also was a vocal critic of President Dwight Washington, and many say that Legend is the reason Washington decided not to seek a second term. In 1998, Legend announced his third presidential campaign. He budgeted $69,239,000 towards his campaign, and ran as a Democrat. He faced off against Sara Caruso, the 57-year-old Green Party candidate. Caruso frequently attacked Legend's advanced age during the season, claiming that he wasn't competent enough to run his nursing home. However, Legend kept a cool demeanor, and continued to campaign. The election was tied, and so the US House of Representatives met to elect the president. In a close vote, Legend defeated Caruso and was elected President again.
President of the United States, Second Presidency (1998-2002)
[
]
During his second presidency, Legend dealt with hostile countries with an assertive approach. He also commissioned a new Mercedes-Benz as his new presidential vehicle. In 2001, Legend introduced a new plan to convert all of America's energy to renewable energy in just two years. The plan was praised by environmentalists, but criticized by his fellow politicians, and was scrapped after he left office. He also offered to rent land to a Haitian politician so they could build a military base, however, the politician said that the cost was to high. No deal was made. In 2002, Legend finished his second term, and announced his retirement from politics, as he was no longer eligible for reelection. At 96 when leaving office, Legend was the oldest person ever to be elected and serve as US President.
Personal life
[
]
Legend was close to his mother during his early life, but as he entered his career, he wasn't able to spend as much time with her. When his mother died in 1947, he planned her funeral and buried her. He inherited $263,124 from her. Legend never knew his father, but he hoped that wherever he was, he was proud of him. Legend and his ex-stepsister Ciara shared a friendly relationship, and Legend attended her funeral in 1980. Legend detested his former stepfather, Brock Ortiz, and was happy when his mother divorced her.
Legend never dated in high school, as he was too afraid to approach women. In 1927, while he was in university, his classmate Dixie Chapman (1906-1971) asked him out. The two began dating, and Legend later proposed to her in 1933, and she accepted. The two married a year later at a courthouse, and Dixie took Legend's last name. Dixie and Legend had a bit of a rough relationship, as Dixie felt Legend didn't love her because he was always working. Legend tried to make time for her, and has affirmed that he did love her. They were married for thirty-seven years, and had 4 children together: Allison (1937-2012), Terrell (1939-2021), Sabrina (b. 1946), and Max (b. 1951). In 1971, Dixie was trampled by fans at a stadium during a riot, and died. Legend was devastated, and didn't date for a long time after that.
Legend later began dating Dixie Hayes (b. 1964) in 1991. He proposed to her a year later, and she accepted. The two married in 1995, and Dixie hyphenated her last name so she was Dixie Hayes-Legend. The two remained married until Legend's death, and had two children together: Elektra (b. 1999) and Walter (2004-2012).
Because he was always working, he didn't spend that much time with his children when they were growing up. However, he later tried to spend time with them, and make it up. He planned the funerals of Allison and Walter in 2012. He was especially devastated by Walter's death, as Walter was so young, and died from sickle-cell disease, which he tried to get treated. He planned his son Terrell's funeral in 2021, and gave a tearful eulogy. Legend also supported Elektra when she came out as gay in 2013.
Later life
[
]
Legend spent the rest of his life in retirement, but that didn't mean he disappeared from the public eye. He appeared in several commercials, and gained an active presence on social media, evening having a YouTube channel where he regularly posted. Legend also created his own brand of merchandise, that he named "TheLegend", and even started an OnlyFans account in 2022 (he didn't post any explicit content though). He earned hundreds of millions of dollars from these ventures. Legend was renowned internationally for his longevity, as he was still active at 115. Legend remained in excellent health throughout his life, and was on a Mediterranean Diet since 1932. When asked what the secret for a long life was, he said that he's not going to tell, as it's for people to discover on their own.
Death
[
]
Oliver Joseph James Legend died on June 4, 2024 while sleeping peacefully at the age of 117. His funeral was attended by his second wife, Dixie Hayes-Legend; his three living children Sabrina, Max, and Elektra; and many of his fans and supporters. As a former US president, he was given a state funeral in Washington DC. At the time of his death, he was worth $2.323 billion.
