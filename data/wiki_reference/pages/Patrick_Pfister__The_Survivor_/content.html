Patrick Laystom' is a fictional character of the Bitlife universe.

Patrick Laystom (January 26, 1895 - July 20, 1994) was a former aristocrat and a well-known composer of the 1920s. Born in Bern, Switzerland, his parents were Tabea Lehmann (1871 - 1955) and Lars Laystom (1830 - 1916). He had an older brother, Silvan Laystom (1889 - 1931), and a younger sister, Tabea II Laystom (1897 - 1979).

Early Life (1895 - 1919)[]

Childhood and friends[]

Since a young age, Patrick resulted as a brilliant person, having an high knowladge in many subjects at school. In his childhood and teenage years, he never had many friends for his loner personality. As a little kid, his parents made him travel many times all across the world and from that moment a passion for travelling was born.

Teenage Years[]

As he turned 15, Patrick enrolled in a private secondary school at "St. Johanson Upper Secondary School". He studied difficult subjects all day long and teachers always said that he was the best student they ever had in their whole life. Many times he wanted to help his classmates to study for exams just to have some friends and eventually in 1911 he started to hang out with some nice people. During this time, his parents had many fights for unpaid debts and they actually lost interest in eachother. The atmosphere in the household was always tense. One night, while trying to sleep peacefully in his bed, Patrick started to realise how till that moment, he was never attracted to women but most likely to men.

Dropping Out School And Titanic[]

In 1912, after turning 17, Patrick dropped out from school to persuade his dream of living the good life in the amazing "New World", the United States. For this he packed his bags, went to Cherbourg, France, and boarded one of the most famous transatlantic of all time, the "R.M.S Titanic" on April 10th, 1912 at 6:30 P.M. He was a first class passenger and made many friends on the ship. Unfortunatly, on the night of April 14th, 1912, the Titanic struck an iceberg and started to slowly get water in the lower parts. It sunk 2 hours and 30 minutes later at 2:20 A.M of April 15th. Patrick was found on a piece of wall by lifeboat 9 at around 3:55 A.M and got rescued among the other few survivors. He arrived at New York 3 days later and moved to Wichita, Kansas.

University and Work[]

Right away Patrick signed himself at university in the music program and got very good marks in all the years. He took his degree in the summer of 1919 and got a job as a music composer at 'Caveman Studios' and started to get known among other producers of music. Co-workers always said that Patrick was a sweet-heart, always caring about other people and got a lot of empathy that not everyone had.

Meeting Lebron Slayer[]

On a normal work day of 1920, Patrick met Lebron Slayer (1894 - 1987), a charismatic and funny young man that always made everyone laugh. The two of them got to know more of eachother at a fancy work dinner with all their co-workers. That night they talked, laughed and eventually Patrick opened up talking about his experience on the Titanic and how he got many traumas from the experience. There was a sorta of spark between Lebron and Patrick and they noticed that. Sometimes after the dinner they started to secretly date and spend much more time together. Lebron friends speculated about the relationship and were really supportive at that time.

Depression and Recovery[]

During the first months of relationship, Patrick noticed that Lebron was a total different person outisde of work. He was drunk most of the times and some nights he didn't even came home. Patrick was always crying in those long six years of dating and then, in 1926 they officially broke up and never talked again. This event brought sadness and anger, and Patrick fell in a deep depression that was accentueted by the sudden death of Patrick's older brother in 1931. Pushed by his closest friends, he started to go get counseling session to get better and happier than ever. In 1935, when he turned 40, Patrick recovered from depression.

World War II[]

When WWII begun in 1939, Patrick started to exercise to be a strong soldier so that if the war would've been brought in America, he was ready to fight for his country. On December 7th, 1941, Pearl Harbor was bombed and the United States entered the war and allied with Great Britain and Russia. Patrick was deployed and fought very hard and survived till the last day of the war on September 2nd, 1945. He came back home on November 30th.

Retirement and last years[]

After retiring in 1963, Patrick started to enjoy every little moment of his life, travelling in other countries such as: Argentina, Brazil, France, Spain, Greece and Italy. He even went on a cruise trying to fight his fear of the sea 55 years after the sinking of Titanic. He got to know new people, that quickly became friends for him and dedicated his days to do as many things as possible to make that life remarkable. When he was 84 his little sister died while sleeping peacefully, so he went back to Switzerland to pay respect to her funeral and to visit one last time his hometown. From 1990 to 1992 Patrick wrote a biographical book about his life that was published by his friends after his death.

Death[]

On the morning of July 20th, 1994, at the age of 99, Patrick Pfister passed away peacefully in his bed. He was found by one of his friends, Katherine, that went to his house to bring him a gift. His funerals took place 2 days later and all the people he knew were present. After his death, in 1999 the mayor of Wichita payed respect to the long and adventurous life of Patrick with a beautiful statue in the plaza of the city. To this day young generations bring him flowers and remember his life choices. On April 15th, 2022, on the 120th anniversary of the Titanic disaster, the mayor of Wichita held a memorial for him.
