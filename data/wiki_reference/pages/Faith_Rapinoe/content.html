Early Life[]

Faith Rapinoe as a kid was simple he liked to walk backwards, he became friends with many different children, he remained calm during vaccination, really simple. He liked to find random ways to argue with his sister just because he found them entertaining. When he started primary school he was just a normal kid, that liked walking a lot. He even liked martial arts spending a lot of time to learn Jiu-Jitsu reaching red belt within a year. Entering Middle school he immediately found interest in basketball and joined the basketball team at his middle school. He proceeded to still like walking.

Career[]

Faith was eligible for the nba draft where he was drafted as the 5th pick in the 1st round to the Utah Scat for a 3 year long deal worth $2.8M playing as a small forward. Faith accepted the contract.

Rookie Season[]

Faith trained in every skill he could during his rookie season really aiming for the rookie of the year award. He was a decent shooter with amazing handles decent passing IQ and he knew how to do some cool tricks his defence was slightly above average and his rebounding was too, however his celebrations were pretty hype. However his rookie season didn't really go well with the Utah Scat having the 18th worst record and barely made it into the playoffs as an 8th seed where they lost in the first round. However Faith had a decent rookie stat line of 11.9 PPG 3 APG (Assists per game) 1 BPG (Block per game) 2.7 RPG (Rebounds per game) and 1 STG (Steals per game) remember this terminology because im not reexplaining it. He won the Rookie of the Year just which was exactly how he wished for however he knew he couldn't suddenly just stop working hard.

2nd Season[]

Faith continued his old High School routine with his basketball training to hopefully gain an edge over other players in the league. He was slowly but surely getting in shape so that he looked more like the other guys in the league. He was started to get some good shooting while still having those amazing handles, even better Passing IQ and definitely even more of a show off than before with some great defensive capabilities especially in his rebounding and his celebrations were still hype. He ended the season with the Utah Scat once again being the 8th seed in the playoffs and with a 35-47 record they overall came 15th in the league. Luckily Faith managed to drop 14.3 PPG 3.5 APG 1.4 BPG 3.0 RPG 1.0 SPG winning the Sixth Man of the year award.
