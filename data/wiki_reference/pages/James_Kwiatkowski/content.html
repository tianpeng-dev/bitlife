James Kwiatkowski (1916 - 1943) was an American soldier known for his heroism in World War II. He was the second-youngest son of lawyer Cezary Kwiatkowski and the older brother of U.S. President William Kwiatkowski.

Early and personal life[]

James was born in Pheonix, Arizona as the fourth child of Cezary Kwiatkowski and Mary Grace Kwiatkowski.

Military service and death[]

Following the 1941 attack on Pearl Harbor, James eagerly enlisted in the military. Around the same time, his brother, William, joined the Army as an officer.
