Edward Meyer (1854-1975) was an American nude art model, ragtime singer, politician, lawyer, and megacentenarian. He was the 38th president of the United States.

Early life[]

Meyer was born in Baltimore, Maryland. His mother was Aubrey Prats. He graduated from a community college with an Associate Degree.

Art career[]

In 1874, Meyer became a nude art model for Baltimore City Public Schools. He was fired in 1894.

Music career[]

In 1897, Meyer signed with Blueprint Records as a ragtime singer and released his first album. He retired in 1925.

Political and legal career[]

In 1925, Meyer was elected to the Board of Education of Baltimore County and served two terms.

In 1931, Meyer became the Mayor of Baltimore. He served one term. After losing the gubernatorial election of 1934, he went to the University of Maryland. He graduated in 1938 with a BS in political science.

In 1939, Meyer was re-elected as mayor and served a second term.

In 1943, Meyer became the Governor of Maryland and served one term. After losing the gubernatorial election of 1946, he went to the University of Maryland Francis Key Carey School of Law. He passed the bar exam in 1949.

In 1951, Meyer became a magistrate. He eventually became a district court judge for the District of Maryland before resigning to become governor.

In 1966, Meyer was re-elected as Governor of Maryland and served a second term.

In 1972, Meyer was elected as President of the United States.

Death[]

In 1975, Meyer died of natural causes in office. He was the oldest man ever. His ashes were scattered in the Atlantic Ocean.

Relationship and child[]

In 1892, Meyer started dating a jeweler named Kimberly Colon (1874-1984). They have a daughter, Lauren Gulch.
