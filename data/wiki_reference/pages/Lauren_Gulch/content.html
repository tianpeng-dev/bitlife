Lauren Gulch (née Meyer, 1913 - August 27, 2024) was an American Marine Corps officer and politician.

Early life[]

Gulch was born Lauren Meyer in Baltimore, Maryland. Her father was Eddie Meyer. She went to the University of Maryland and graduated with a BS in Psychology in 1935.

Military career (1935-1977)[]

In 1935, Gulch joined the United States Marine Corps as an officer. She retired in 1977.

Political career (1983-2015)[]

In 1983, Gulch ran for election to the Board of Education of Baltimore County, but lost. She then went to UMB again, this time graduating with a BS in Political Science.

In 1987, Gulch was elected to the BOE. She served two terms.

In 1995, Gulch became the mayor of Baltimore. She served two terms.

After losing the 2002 gubernatorial election, Gulch went to the University of Maryland Francis Key Carey School of Law. She passed the bar exam in 2005.

In 2007, Gulch became the Governor of Maryland. She served two terms.

Death[]

On August 27, 2024, Gulch died of old age. Her husband attended her funeral.

Marriages and stepchild[]

From 1942 to 1993, Gulch was married to a US Navy enlistee named Marquis Gulch (1891-1994).

In 1993, Gulch married a restaurant worker named Zion Crunk (1945-after 2059). Crunk had a son named Walt Crunk from a previous marriage to a woman named Tiara Feingold.
