Yusei Anderson (né Samada, 3230-after 3286) was a Japanese-British rugby player and mortician.

Early life (3230-3252)[]

Anderson was born Yusei Samada in Japan. In 3233, he was adopted by Aurora Anderson and his last name was changed to hers. He emigrated to Cardiff, Wales.

In 3248, Anderson emigrated to Bordeaux, France and enrolled at the University of Bordeaux. He was on the rugby team and graduated in 3252 with a Licentiate in Chemistry.

Sports career (3252-3267)[]

In 3252, Anderson emigrated to Sydney, Australia and joined the Manly Warringah Jaegers as a center. He won a Most Improved Player award on the team.

In 3259, Anderson was released by the Jaegers. He emigrated back to Cardiff and joined the Hull Kingston Groovers. He has won a championship on the team.

In 3267, Anderson retired.

Mortuary career (3267-3286)[]

In 3268, Anderson became a mortician for Zenith Mortuary.

In 3271, Anderson was promoted to Sr. Embalmer.

In 3274, Anderson was promoted to Funeral Director.

Anderson was alive in 3286, but his death date is unknown.

Relationship and child[]

From 3248 to 3249, Anderson dated a student named Amelia Moreau (b. 3230). They had a son, Aaron Anderson.
