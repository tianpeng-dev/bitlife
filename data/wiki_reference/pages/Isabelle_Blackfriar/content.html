Isabelle Rose Blackfriar (January 7, 1983 - March 25, 2024) was an award-winning British actress with a career that spanned twenty-one years. She is well known for her portrayal of Damora in "Protectors of the Galaxy", in which she won a Silver Globe for Best Supporting Actress; and for her role as Lieutenant America in "The Revengers". Blackfriar was renowned for her versatility in playing multiple characters, and she also had an impressive singing voice that caused her to briefly consider a career in the music industry. She starred in several award-winning movies, and many box-office hits during her career, and won several awards in her career, including a Bitcademy Award for Best Actress in 2009 and a Silver Globe Award for Best Supporting Actress in 2011. Tragically, Blackfriar was killed during the filming of "Rapunzel" at the age of 41 after being attacked by an eagle on set.

Early Life[]

Isabelle Rose Blackfriar was born on January 7, 1983 in London, United Kingdom to Rose Blackfriar (1942-2016), a hairdresser. Blackfriar was conceived by artificial insemination, so her father is unknown, although records state that the sperm donor who impregnated Blackfriar's mother was an extremely good-looking individual. Blackfriar had an older half-brother, Niles (1981-1992), who had the same mother as her.

During her childhood, Blackfriar befriended many people, and was seen as an outgoing child. In 1990, her mother made her take piano lessons, which Blackfriar never continued in. However, Blackfriar also took voice lessons and became quite a good singer. She also took karate lessons in 1991, and gained a red belt in karate. In 1992, her half-brother Niles died after he dropped a hairdryer into the bathtub while he was taking a bath, electrocuting himself. Blackfriar attended his funeral, and in later interviews, she said that Niles' death devastated her. Blackfriar started attending Olly Baker Primary School in 1989, and began attending London Secondary School in 1994, which she graduated from in 2001.

Career[]

Early Acting Career (2001-2007)[]

Blackfriar began taking acting lessons at the age of 8, and continued taking them right up until she graduated from secondary school in 2001. Following her graduation, she began starring as an extra in movies like "Foggy Coasts", and "Are You There?". Blackfriar hired Poppy Cockwallace-Craig as her talent agent, and asked her to get her an audition for a role. Cockwallace-Craig was originally successful in arranging auditions, but Blackfriar wasn't offered any roles, whether it be a movie role or TV role. Eventually, it became hard for for Cockwallace-Craig to secure a role for Blackfriar, and she dropped her in 2002. Blackfriar tried to get an audition for the role of Gretel in "Hansel and Gretel", but never got a callback. She then briefly tried to get a contract as a singer, but never got a callback.

In 2003, while working as an extra in the film "Bevins Place", Blackfriar was discovered by executives from Legacy TV. They offered her the chance to play as Perez in the upcoming third season of "The Ravine". She happily accepted it and got to work. Her role on "The Ravine" propelled her to stardom, and she earned £414,000 from her role. Unfortunately, "The Ravine" was cancelled shortly after the release of the third season. Blackfriar was then back to working as an extra. In late 2004, she was offered the chance to play Sierra in the new TV show "Hannah Indiana". Blackfriar happily accepted it and got to work. She would star in that role until "Hannah Indiana" was cancelled in 2008. She earned a total of £2,287,200 from the show. In 2006, Blackfriar hired Nancy Morgan (b. 1948) as her new talent agent. Morgan remained her agent until Blackfriar's death.

Movie Stardom (2008-2016)[]

Following the cancellation of "Hannah Indiana", Blackfriar then sought out another role. In 2008, her agent secured her an audition for the lead role of Dale Evans in "Jade Rabbit". Blackfriar auditioned for, and was given the role, which she accepted. The film was a moderate success, grossing £18,200,000 against a £8,400,00 budget. Blackfriar earned £389,580 from it. However, she was nominated for a Bitcademy Award for Best Actress, which she won. She later then starred as an old man in the film "One in the Holster", earning £2,174,400 from the role. She then starred as Damora in the film "Protectors of the Galaxy", which became her most iconic role. "Protectors of the Galaxy" was a commercial success, grossing £658,080,000 against a £105,000,000 budget. Blackfriar earned £12,394,805 from this role, and was nominated for and won a Silver Globe Award for Best Supporting Actress. Her acceptance speech for the award went viral, and lines from it are still quoted to this day.

Following this, Blackfriar starred in the Silver Globe Award winning movie "The Revengers" as the lead role Lieutenant America, earning £1,177,800 from it. She then starred in the movies "Majority Report", "A Most Deceptive Game", and "A Final Engagement"; in that order. While filming "Majority Report", Blackfriar became very close to her director, Russell Suckley, who was also the director for "The Revengers" and close friends with her. The two hooked up a few times, resulting in Blackfriar becoming pregnant with Suckley's child. She gave birth to Kai Blackfriar-Suckley in 2013. In 2016, Blackfriar's mother Rose fell down a flight of stairs at her house, and died at the age of 74. Blackfriar mourned her mother's death and had her buried. She inherited £282,738 from her.

Later Career (2016-2024)[]

Blackfriar later starred films like "Galactic Issues", "Intrastellar", and "Mermaid of the Light". These films had less budgets than films she was used to working on, yet she happily worked hard in them. She was nominated for a Bitcademy Award for Best Supporting Actress for "Galactic Issues", yet she didn't win. In 2019, she published her memoir "Entitled", which became a bestseller and made £502,801. Blackfriar achieved success with the movie "Wings at War", which earned her £7,066,800. She was nominated for and won a Bitcademy Award for Best Supporting Actress in "Wings of War" and gave another impeccable acceptance speech. She then starred as the Queen in "Snow White", in which she was nominated for but didn't win, a Bitcademy Award for Best Supporting Actress. She then starred in "Mr. & Mrs. Smythe" as Angelina Smythe, and then finally starred in the film "Rapunzel" as the Prince, which would be her final role before her untimely death.

Personal Life and Relationships[]

Blackfriar had many friends throughout life, and was seen as an outgoing and friendly person. In 1999, she met her first boyfriend Luke Jagger (b. 1983), at school and the two began dating. The two were together for 10 years, and Jagger proposed to Blackfriar many times, but she rejected his proposals. In 2009, while filming "The Jade Rabbit", Blackfriar began a brief fling with her director, Phil Crook. Her boyfriend was opposed to this, and berated Blackfriar for cheating on him. Blackfriar asked out Crook, but Crook rejected her, as he was still married and he didn't want to leave his wife for her. Follwing this, she asked out her co-star Samuel Pearce, and the two started dating, and Jagger left her.

In 2010, Blackfriar asked out Crook, who was recently divorced, and the two started dating. Pearce left her. In 2009, Blackfriar hooked up with her director Russell Suckley, and the following year gave birth to a baby boy Kai Blackfriar-Suckley (b. 2013). Crook and Blackfriar dated until 2013, when Blackfriar broke up with him and started dating Finn Beckinsale. Blackfriar later filed a restraining order against Crook in 2014, as he was using her phone's GPS to track her location. In 2015, Blackfriar began dating her co-star Elijah Bell, who she married in 2016. Elijah changed his name to Elijah Blackfriar-Bell. The two had one daughter, Fanny Blackfriar-Bell (b. 2017). The same year, Blackfriar also cheated on her husband with her co-star Robin. In 2018, Blackfriar and Elijah divorced. Blackfriar gave birth to Rose Blackfriar (b. 2019), who she named after her late mother, and was fathered by Robin. That same year, she began dating her co-star on "Mermaid of the Light", Arlo Miller. The two married later that year, and Arlo changed his name to Arlo Blackfriar-Miller.

In 2020, Blackfriar divorced Arlo, and began dating her co-star George Cohen. Cohen proposed to her in 2023 and she accepted. The two were going to be married before Blackfriar's tragic death suddenly happened.

Blackfriar was very close to her family. She wished she could know her father, and was close to her half-brother and devastated by his death in 1992. She planned her mother's funeral in 2016, and chose to honour her by naming her daughter after her.

Death[]

On March 25, 2024, while filming on the set of "Rapunzel", Blackfriar was asked to handle an eagle, which she happily accepted. Unfortunately, the eagle became violent and attacked her, clawing her in several vital places. Blackfriar was taken to hospital, where she died seven hours later from her injuries. Filming on "Rapunzel" was paused indefinitely after this, and it is unknown whether it will ever resume. Blackfriar's funeral was held on April 1, 2024, and her fiance; and her children Kai, Fanny, and Rose attended her funeral; as well as many of her fans. Free copies of her book "Entitled" were handed out at the service. At the time of her death, Blackfriar was worth £27,068,568.
