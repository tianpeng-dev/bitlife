Dene Mason Givesoft
(August 7, 1931 - June 16, 2025) was an American race car driver. Originally a lobbyist, he achieved his lifelong dream of becoming a racer later in his life, thus embarking on a four-decade long career from 1973 to 2016. Participating in hundreds of different races with a variety of vehicles throughout the years, Givesoft won a total of 37 championships.

Early life and education
[
]
Dene was born in Speedway, Indiana to model Kiana Tyson (1900 - 1985) and World War I veteran-turned-trucker
Mason Givesoft
(1898 - 1981). Although he was an only child, he had many friends growing up, even his pet cat, Larry (1929 - 1946), and was very athletic. He got his first bike at the age of six as a gift from his father, and he rode it for many years until he started high school, at which point he gave it to his best friend. Dene was also an avid runner, participating in track and cross country in middle and high school.
Givesoft attended Indiana University Bloomington, Class of 1953. Majoring in philosophy, he planned on going into a high-paying job so he could save enough money to fund his childhood dream of becoming a race car driver.
Lobbying career
[
]
After graduating from college, Givesoft went on a spree of modeling gigs, only half of which were successful. Later, he found a job as a lobbyist for Serenade Mills, where he would work for the next 20 years, receiving two promotions in 1956 and 1959. Dene would also save money by living with his parents until he could find an affordable home for himself and his own family, and he also relied heavily on public transportation, which was cheaper than buying a car. Throughout the 1960s, he would take every opportunity to see a race at the tracks, and sometimes he would even take up betting to raise some more money.

Genesoft retired from Serenade Mills in 1973. By that point, he had earned enough money to buy a racing garage and his first sports car. After participating in a few standard races at the speedway and Madgab Lake, he entered his first torunament, the Lightning Cup, and he won, thus earning his first gold trophy in his rookie season.
