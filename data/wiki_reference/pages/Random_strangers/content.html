Random strangers

Launched on iOS:

September 2018 (default)

Launched on Android:

February 2019 (default)

Within BitLife there are many random strangers, including moneyed strangers, who you may interact with. They can be good or bad.

Things random strangers do[]

  • Insult your character or their loved one by calling them a bad name. Your character can forgive them, insult them back, or assault them or even beg them to watch their language. Sometimes they could be assaulted by the stranger and could fight back, walk away, beg them to keep your hands to themselves, or call police.
  • Scenario says "[Action], a [stranger] calls you [Insult]!" (formerly "Someone called you [Insult]")
    • If the stranger assaults your character, they can be sued.
  • Commit a crime or publicly disturb the peace. A character can intervene, do nothing, or call the police. Sometimes they could be assaulted by the stranger and could fight back, walk away, beg them to keep your hands to themselves, or call police.
  • Scenario says "While walking to place of interest, you witness a [stranger causing trouble]!" (formerly e.g. "You witness someone littering!"
  • If a pet runs away and your character tried looking but couldn't find it, sometimes the stranger will find it for you. You can thank them or let them keep it. The stranger may also return it if your character releases it.
  • Random strangers can respond to a character doing a Freelance Gig and it will say their name and how many hours your character worked for them multiplied by the hourly rate.
  • Random strangers can buy your car for the price your character offered, or could haggle them for a lower price.
  • If your character commits a Crime, random strangers could be involved.
    • Their houses can be burgled.
    • If your character is caught stealing a car, the stranger who owns the car will stop them, either calling the police or assaulting them.
    • Random Strangers can be options for who to murder or hire a hitman to kill.
    • They can be pickpocketed.
  • If you turn on Notifications, random strangers are involved in a few.
    • If your character does not pick up money off the ground quickly enough, a random stranger will pick it up first.
    • If your character's car is about to get stolen, but they respond quick enough to the notification, they can stop the random stranger from stealing it.
  • Sell houses. If you sell an expensive house, a random moneyed stranger instead will come to sell houses

.
