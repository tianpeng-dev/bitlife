Derek Charles Howsen (April 1, 1953 - May 15, 2024), nicknamed "The Howzer," was an American professional football quarterback, military officer, museum worker and owner. He spent his entire 13-year playing career (1975-1988) with the New York Giants, spawning a dynasty with the franchise in which he led the team to win two championships in a row. He won several awards throughout his career. Howsen retired from football at the age of 35 to pursue a career in the United States Marine Corps, in which he rose to the rank of Brigadier General. In his later years, he practiced extreme Machiavellianism, opening a museum to display many unique artifacts, three of which included his two championship rings and the taxidermized body of his late son.

Early life and education[]

Derek was born in Arlington County, Virginia to Dennis Howsen (1921 - 1994) and Camille Howsen (1924 - 2012). His mother was a magician, and his father was a writer and World War II veteran. He was a middle child with an older and younger brother, a restaurant worker named Donald (born 1952) and a makeup artist named Antonio (born 1960). His grandfather, Charles Howsen III (c. 1900 - c. 1970s), was a World War I veteran, his great-grandfather, Charles Howsen Jr. (c. 1870s - c. 1940s), was a Spanish-American War veteran, and his great-great-grandfather, Charles Howsen Sr. (c. 1840s - c. 1910s), was a Civil War veteran. The Howsen family owned a loving and loyal pet mongrel named Tuffin (1948 - 1958), who was seen as a feminine version of Toto from The Wizard of Oz, which ended up being one of Howsen's favorite movies growing up.

Howsen's passion for football stemmed from a young age. He started playing competitively at Erect Middle School. He was also a member of his middle school's yearbook club, of which he became secretary in eighth grade. Howsen further excelled at the sport at Kim Pratt High School, earning his captaincy in his junior-to-senior years. He had also been named treasurer of his high school yearbook club at age 16. Derek graduated from high school in 1971 with high honors, and he had won many athletic awards throughout his high school career. He then enrolled in university making himself exempt from the Vietnam draft. Howsen accepted a full athletic scholarship to play for the school's football team, becoming captain once again in his junior-senior years. He was also a member of the rock-climbing club and outdoors club. He became the rock-climbing club's vice-president in 1973. A seasoned member of the jocks clique, he sucessfully rushed and was admitted to the Alpha Phi Alpha fraternity. Howsen graduated in 1975 with a bachelor's in archeology.

Football career[]

Upon graduating from college, Howsen made himself eligible for the pro football draft for the 1975 NFL season. He was selected in the first round by the New York Giants, accpeting his first contract of $1.4 million over three years as a quarterback.

Post-Marines[]

After retiring from the Marines in 2015, Howsen would dedicate the rest of his life to museum work. He served as the owner and curator of the Howsen Family Museum, which he had opened in 2008.

Personal life[]

Howsen shared his first kiss with Tina Krueger in his last year of elementary school. The pair subsequently started dating. Krueger became a firefighter and served the Arlington County Fire Department for five decades, retiring in 2019 as a chief. During his last year of college in 1974, Howsen proposed to her, but was rejected. The next year, after being signed to the New York G's, he proposed again, to which Krueger accepted. They married that year, and Tina took Derek's surname. They had three children and three grandchildren together:

  • Savannah (born 1978) - Jeweler at Long-Rojas
    • Autumn Harvey-Cocker (born 2019)
  • Latrell (1981 - 2014) - Chef at Cafe Arcadia
  • Aria (born 1986) - Agent for Lilypad Insurance Group
    • Lindsay Handy (born 2013)
    • Dylan Jimenez (born 2016)

Latrell died in 2014 at the age of 33 after being mauled by a Doberman pincher. Howsen taxidermized him and appraised his body so he could put him on display at the museum. In an interview, Derek stated that it was a special way of remembering his son.

Death[]

Howsen died from a stroke on May 15, 2024 at the age of 71. He is survived by his wife, two surviving children, three grandchildren, four brothers, and many nieces and nephews. The Marines posthumously promoted Howsen to the rank of Major General. He is buried in the Arlington National Cemetary, where is grave is marked with all of his military and athletic honors.
