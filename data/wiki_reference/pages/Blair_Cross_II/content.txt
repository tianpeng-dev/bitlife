Blair Cross II
(2713-2803) was an American mailman and alternative metal singer.

Early life (2713-2731)
[
]
Cross was born in Miami, Florida. He was the youngest of five children born to
DeShawn Cross II
and the youngest of four born to his second girlfriend Valeria Herrera. He was named for his 10x great-granduncle.
Cross graduated from high school in 2731.
Postal career (2731-2748)
[
]
In 2732, Cross became a junior mail carrier for the City of Miami.
In 2735, Cross was promoted to mail carrier.
In 2738, Cross was promoted to senior mail carrier.
In 2748, Cross resigned.
Music career (2748-2781)
[
]
In 2748, Cross signed with Kama’aina Records as an alternative singer. He released his debut alternative metal album,
We’re Here.
In 2749, Cross released his second album,
Hit the Bottle.
In 2754, Cross released his third album,
Grateful for Tulips.
In 2759, Cross released his fourth album,
The Princess Is Dead
, which went gold in 2760.
In 2764, Cross released his fifth album,
Final Second
, which went gold in less than a year and platinum in 2779.
In 2769, Cross released his sixth album,
What Killed Curiosity
, which went gold in 2770.
In 2774, Cross released his seventh album,
We’re Still.
In 2777, Cross released his eighth album,
The King Is Dead.
In 2779, Cross released his ninth album,
They Think I’m a Fake.
In 2780, Cross released his 10th album,
Hit the Deck.
In 2781, Cross was released by Kama’aina Records.
Death
[
]
In 2813, Cross died of old age.
Relationships and children
[
]
From 2742 to 2770, Cross dated his co-worker, a waste collector named Brittany Grabber (2706-after 2789). They had three children:
Molly Cross III (2743-2830), school administrator
Megan Cross II (2744-2830), receptionist
Brian Cross II (2745-2825), Army enlistee
In 2770, Cross started dating an Air Force enlistee named Jamila Brewer (2751-after 2832). They had a son:
Keith Cross II
