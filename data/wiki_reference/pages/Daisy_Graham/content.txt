Daisy Graham
(June 16, 1970 - January 28, 2024) was an Australian-Dutch businesswoman, sailor, and convicted felon. She died in prison after being convicted of attempted murder.

Biography

Early life
[
]
Daisy Graham was born in Perth, Australia, on June 16, 1970, to Chris Graham (1911-1989), a 59-year-old makeup artist, and Margot Graham (born 1946), a 24-year-old grocer. She had a sister, Thi (born 1973), and a brother, Matthew (born 1975). She had an unremarkable childhood, graduating secondary school at age 17 and immediately starting university with a major in information systems. In 1989, during the height of the AIDS epidemic, Graham's father died of the condition, severely impacting Daisy. She was also diagnosed with genital herpes during her university years.
Move to the Netherlands
[
]
Graham graduated in 1991, and started a job as a business analyst at Bronze Investments. She made good money, but was unsatisfied in her life, and ultimately decided to start a new life by immigrating to the Netherlands in 1996. As a university graduate, she was commissioned in the Netherlands Coastguard when she enlisted upon her arrival. During this time, her siblings also joined the Australian Army, defining the Grahams as a military family.
During her military career, Graham's combative nature began to shine through. In a 2002 incident at a nightclub, she assaulted a go-go dancer during a confrontation, ripping her brain, squeezing her private parts, and biting her butt. However, Graham's foot was elbowed and her larynx crushed, impairing her ability to speak. In 2005, she began dating Siem Heide, but Heide cheated on her the next year, causing Graham to assault him by whipping his neck. She was charged with assault, but was acquitted.
Decline and later life
[
]
In 2007, due to performance issues, Graham was dishonorably discharged from the Coastguard as a lieutenant. Despondent, she turned to dark thoughts. One day, while walking down the street, she attempted to strangle a hippie at random. The hippie fled and called the police, causing officers to apprehended Graham. Charged with attempted murder, she was sentenced to forty years in prison.
Despite their relationship's history of domestic violence, Heide stayed by Graham's side during her imprisonment. Aged 52, she improbably contracted bubonic plague. She died in detention on January 28, 2024. Her siblings and mother attended her funeral, while her boyfriend and nephew Harrison refused to come.
