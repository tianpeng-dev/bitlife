Otto Nietzche
(20 November 1949 - 24 April 2004) was a German politician, museum worker and soldier who served as the director of the Cologne School Board from 1975 to 1983. He is best known for his ambitious but unsuccessful attempts to run for high political offices, such as the governorship of his home state and the Chancellorship of the country.

Early life
[
]
Otto was born in Cologne, Germany to jeweler Lara Klum (1915 - 2003) and hairdresser Nicholas "Nick" Nietzche (1914 - 1996). He had two siblings, Friedrich (1944 - 2017), a hairdresser like his father, and Hannah (born 1954), a flight attendant. He also had a stepbrother named Liam from his mother's brief marriage to retired restaurant worker Kilian Becker (1924 - 1998). He had a pet mongrel named Marlon (1945 - 1959).

Otto died of a heart attack on 24 April 2004 at the age of 54. He is survived by his wife, four children, five grandchildren and three siblings. His gravestone is marked with his medal of honor.
