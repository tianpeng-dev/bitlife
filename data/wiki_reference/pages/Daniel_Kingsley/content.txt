Daniel Joseph Kingsley JD, Esq
(March 8, 1925 - July 30, 2024) was a British lawyer and politician who served as Prime Minister of the United Kingdom from 1984 to 2004, after previously serving as Chair of the Glasgow City Council from 1974 to 1979, Mayor of Glasgow from 1969 to 1974, and Director of the Glasgow School Board from 1964 to 1969. Born in Glasgow, Kingsley studied criminal justice in university, and later became a lawyer. After working for 14 years, Kingsley began his political career, and eventually led the Labour Party to victory in the 1984 elections. Kingsley served for 4 terms, each lasting five years, and worked to modernize Great Britain, and improve the quality of life of Britons. At the end of his career, he had one of the highest approval ratings in history.

Early life
[
]
Daniel Joseph Kingsley was born on March 8, 1925, in Glasgow, Scotland to Gary Kingsley (1885-1956), a librarian, and Harriet Kingsley (1900-1983), a mail carrier. Kingsley had an older brother, Graham (1924-1997). When Kingsley was one, his parents divorced, and his mother married Nathan Shakespeare (1904-1948), a travel agent, in 1927, changing her name to Harriet Kingsley-Shakespeare. The two also had Kingsley's half-sister, Helena Shakespeare (1927-2003) that same year. The family also had an Australian Shepherd named Tess, who died in 1940 from old age.
During his childhood, Kingsley was a studious student. He was also outgoing, and popular among his peers. He began attending Partner Primary School in 1930 and got good grades. Kingsley also took tambourine lessons in 1933 upon his mother's request. He also took karate lessons from 1934 to 1937, earning a red belt. Kingsley began attending secondary school at Mason Secondary School in 1936, and it was there that he joined his school's soccer team, later becoming captain of the team in 1940. Kingsley began hanging around with the popular kids at school, and also began dating Eleanor Kensington, who he dated for several years.
Kingsley graduated from secondary school in 1943 and was offered a full athletic scholarship to play soccer at university, which he accepted, and began studying criminal justice, and working as a lab assistant. He quit the soccer team a year later, as he needed time to focus on his part time job, and on his studies. Kingsley graduated from university in 1947, at the top of his class, and applied to law school. After his application for a scholarship was rejected, Kingsley asked his mother to pay his tuition, which she agreed to do so. He began studying at university, and it was during this time that his stepfather Nathan was killed by a falling icicle. While Kingsley was close to him, he paid his respects at his funeral. Kingsley graduated from law school in 1950, and began looking for work
Legal career
[
]
Kingsley was hired by Powell & Ward, as a junior associate, and began working as a lawyer in 1950. He argued many cases before prominent British courts and worked hard at his career. Over the years, he was promoted by Powell & Ward, eventually becoming a partner in 1963. It was also during this time that Kingsley met his future wife Isabelle Snodgrass (b. 1938), who he married in 1958. In 1956, Kingsley's father died, and he was devastated. He planned his funeral and buried him. Kingsley inherited £1,793,505 from his father.
Kingsley worked hard at the law, but also had higher aspirations. He believed that since his name was Kingsley, he had to go farther in life. Ever since childhood, he'd dreamed of becoming Prime Minister, and he believed that he was destined to be. He decided that he would start out his political career by running for Director of the Glasgow School Board, and progress from that.
Pre-Prime Ministerial Political Career (1964-1984)
[
]
In 1964, Kingsley budgeted £77,000 towards his run for Director of the Glasgow School Board. He faced off against 71-year-old Mia Griffiths and treated her with respect. He easily defeated Griffiths and won the election. He arrived at Powell & Ward the day after the election, and told them that he was quitting, and was on to bigger and better things. His boss, Harrison, wished him good luck.
Director of the Glasgow School Board (1964-1969)
[
]
Upon being sworn in as Director of the Glasgow School Board, Kingsley set out to make his school district a better place. He confronted a catering company that was dumping leftovers into a nearby pond and told them to clean up their act. Kingsley was known for having a strong willpower, as when a friend of his asked him to propose a mandate that would benefit his textbook business, Kingsley immediately reported his friend to the authorities. He gave several speeches during his tenure, and visited a homeless shelter in 1967, which he later said that the experience humbled him. After completing his first term as School Board Director, he announced that he would not seek reelection. Kingsley then announced that he was going on to bigger and better things and was running for Mayor of Glasgow.
Mayor of Glasgow (1969-1974)
[
]
Kingsley budgeted £324,000 towards his mayoral campaign and represented the Labour Party. He faced off against 51-year-old Jack Cockwallace, who represented the Socialist Party. Kingsley treated Cockwallace with respect, but Cockwallace played dirty during the campaign. After Cockwallace told the media that Kingsley believed all babies came from sin, Kingsley clapped back by starting a rumor that Cockwallace burned money for fun. Kingsely defeated Cockwallace, and won the election, becoming Mayor of Glasgow. That same year, his daughter Olivia was born.
Kingsley worked hard as mayor and implemented a luxury tax to cover a deficit caused by his administration spending too much on eating out, alcohol, and massages. This tax received mixed reception. He also reversed his support for a policy after discovering it would let a private golf course build on land allocated for affordable housing. Kingsley was known for his dedication to protecting the environment and changed the location of a new cancer research facility to avoid harmful environmental issues. After completing his first term as mayor, he announced that he would not run for reelection. Kingsely said that he was going on to bigger and better things and said that he was goign to run fo Chair of the Glasgow City Council.
Chair of the Glasgow City Council (1974-1979)
[
]
Upon being elected Chair, Kingsley gave a speech emphasizing the importance of focusing on the environment. He later formed a pandemic task force in 1975 to prepare his county for a potential pandemic. The task force was later disbanded by his successor. Kingsley created low-income housing to fight a rising influx of homelessness, and also built a massive lake to be used as a wild duck refuge after there was a massive influx of ducks. He also purchased a brand-new Rolls-Royce armoured limousine to be used as his official car. When Kingsley found out that a corporation in his county was doing environmentally dangerous business practices, he went and confronted the CEO directly, telling him to clean up his act. The CEO obliged. In 1979, Kingsley completed his first term as Chair of the Glasgow City Council, and announced that he would not seek reelection, but would run for leadership of the Labour Party, in the hopes of becoming Prime Minister
1979 United Kingdom Election
[
]
Kingsley was elected leader of the Labour Party, and shortly after a general election was called. He budgeted £20,155,000 towards his campaign, and faced off against 75-year-old Georgia Harrison, the leader of the Conservative Party.  During his campaign, Kingsley was offered a large donation from a company linked to drug money, which he politely declined. After Harrison made up a rumour accusing Kingsley of assaulting a plus-size model, Kingsley created a rumour saying that Harrison would poach tigers if she had the chance to do so. The election was held, and the Conservatives won 364 out of 650 seats in the House of Commons, defeating the Labour Party, which only won 260 seats. Kingsley graciously accepted defeat, and became Leader of the Official Opposition.
Opposition Leader (1979-1984)
[
]
In 1980, Kingsley published his autobiography "A Man Named Daniel". The book became a bestseller and grossed £711,133 in sales. That same year, his daughter Jennifer died after falling out of an upside-down rollercoaster. Kingsley was devastated, as she was only seventeen, and planned her funeral. In 1983, Kingsley's mother died from old age. He planned her funeral, and buried her. He and his siblings each inherited £390,855 from her.
In 1984, Prime Minister Georgia Harrison, Kingsley's former opponent, called an election, and announced that she wouldn't run for a second term. Kingsley saw the chance to achieve victory, and budgeted £51,369,000 towards his campaign. He faced off against 79-year-old Evelyn Chiswell, the new leader of the Conservative Party. Kingsley treated her with respect. During the election, he promised to make sure that animal cruelty was prosecuted by strict, but fair laws. However, the media reported a story accusing Kingsley of selling drugs. Kingsley retaliated by starting a rumour accusing Chiswell of always driving distractedly by takign phone calls behind the wheel. In a closely contested election, the Labour Party won 319 out of 650 seats, more than the Conservatives, who won 306 seats. Despite not having a majority, Kingsley was invited by the Queen to form government, and was sworn in as Prime Minister. At his swearing in, Kingsley said that he was happy to serve as Prime Minister.
Prime Minister of the United Kingdom (1984-2004)
[
]
First Term (1984-1989)
[
]
As Prime Minister, Kingsley worked to improve Britain for the better. He built more rehab centers to combat the spread of a new narcotic in 1985. He also used the British Army and police services to wage war against foreign cartels distributing drugs in the United Kingdom, which he called a "War on Drugs". Due to misspending of government funds, Kingsley cut spending on education, and later covered up a deficit in the budget by reallocating funds that were to be spent on national security. These decisions were unpopular, with the public, and his approval ratings dropped. He began involving his family in his job after his wife complained that he wasn't spending enough time with her.
At the end of his first term, the Labour minority government was unpopular, and many wanted someone better to lead. Kingsley said that he would lead his party into the election, and campaigned with the slogan "We suck, but We're better than Tory". Kingsley budgeted £82,601,000 towards his campaign, and faced off against the new Conservative Leader, 54-year-old Victoria Earlswood. Kingsley chose to treat her with respect. He promised to provide prescription medication at no cost to everyone in the UK, and also exposed Earlswood for accepting illegal donations from a weapons corporation. The election was held, and both the Conservative and Labour parties won the same number of seats, 319. The Queen, however, invited Kingsley to form government, as he'd governed for five years with a minority, and believed that he could govern for five more years with a minority. Kinglsey accepted this, and got to work.
Second Term (1989-1994)
[
]
During his second term, Kinglsey sought to gain more popularity with the people, and actually listened to the people's issues, and acted on them. He staged many Labour Party rallies to boost his support, and in 1993, he increased spending on education by 30%. However, he notoriously passed a law to ban improper fractions in 1990, earning the outrage from math teachers. In an interview, he said that the ban on improper fractions was a joke. Also in  1990, he attended a Red Cross charity event. Kingsley's ratings soared, and many supported the Labour Party.
After completing his second term, he announced he would lead Labour into the 1994 election. Kingsley budgeted £114,914,000 towards his campaign, and faced off against 73-year-old Emilia Thompson, the leader of the Libertarian Party. During the campaign, Kingsley said that he deeply opposed the death penalty, and said that it should be illegal completely. He also was interviewed by a popular talk show, which supported his opponent Thompson. In the election, the Labour party won 384 out of 650 seats, winning a landslide victory over the Libertarian Party, which only won 254 seats. Kingsley accepted this, and got to work.
Third Term (1994-1999)
[
]
In his third term, Kingsley found it easier to govern, as he had a majority government. In 1995, a group of terrorists threatened to blow up Big Ben, if Kingsley didn't give them £100,000,000. Kingsley informed the terrorists that his government does not, and will never negotiate with terrorists. The terrorists were later captured. He also authorized an investigation into claims that he'd accepted quid pro quid from a foreign nation, and found no evidence. In 1997, Kingsley's big brother Graham died, and he attended his funeral, and mourned him. Kingsley also introduced devolution, as he wanted his home of Scotland to have more freedom, so he created the Scottish Parliament. He also passed a law in 1998, banning homework, which earned him the respect of students, and the ire of teachers everywhere. That same year, he allowed all asylum seeking refugees from a bloody conflict in a neighbouring country, to enter the UK.
In 1999, Kingsley finished his third term, and announced he was leading the Labours into the 1999 election. He budgeted £121,314,000 towards his campaign, and faced off against the new leader of the Libertarian Party, 66-year-old Percy Evans. Evans ran a smear campaign against Kingsley, and Kingsley addressed the public about this, and confirmed that Evans' allegations were not true. The election was held, and the Labours won 371 seats, easily defeating the Libertarian Party. Kingsley accepted this, and got to work.
Fourth Term (1999-2004)
[
]
In his fourth term, Kingsley announced a new policy of world peace, and said that the United Kingdom could deal with hostile foreign powers, non-violently. He also increased spending on intelligence, after receiving word of a potential terrorist threat. In 2001, Kingsley joined a crowd of topless women protesting gender discrimination, by taking off his shirt and running towards them. This was widely criticized by his opponents and critics, and his approval ratings dropped because of this. In 2002, he passed a law that would criminalize the killing of Bigfoot, if it ever found. The law was criticized by many, who claimed that Kingsley wasn't focusing on the needs of Great Britain. In 2004, Kingsley announced that in the upcoming election, he would not seek a fifth term. Kingsley said that he'd served his time in office, and announced his retirement from politics. He'd served as Prime Minister for 20 years, and was now 79 years old. After leaving office, Kingsley published another memoir, "Badass". The book was a bestseller and grossed £277,569 in sales.
Personal life
[
]
Kingsley kept a good relationship with most of his family, and was close to most them. A notable exception was his stepfather, Nathan Shakespeare. Kingsley and Nathan didn't have much of a relationship, yet Kingsley mourned his stepfather's death in 1948, believing that he was too young to die so tragically. Kingsley was close to his older brother Graham, who often advised him in matters. Kingsley attended his brother's funeral in 1997. He was also close to his half sister Helena, and treated her as if she was his full sister. Kingsley kept a good relationship with Helena's husband Joseph Cockfield (1920-2003), and attended both of their funerals in 2003. Kingsley was also close to his parents. He deeply loved his father Gary, and planned his funeral in 1956; and was also close to his mother Harriet, despite suspecting she may have cheated on his father. Kingsley planned his mother's funeral in 1983.
Kingsley was popular among his classmates in school, and made many friends for life. In 1940, he began dating his classmate Eleanor Kensington. They dated for six years, before Kingsley broke up with her in 1946. In 1951, he began dating Sofia Banbury. He proposed to her in 1953, and she accepted. However, a year later, Kingsley tried to get Banbury to sign a prenuptial agreement, and she refused, and left him. Kingsley became depressed after this. He met Isabelle Snodgrass (b. 1938) in 1956, and the two began dating. Kingsley proposed to her a year later and she accepted. The two married in 1958, going to the Bahamas for their honeymoon. Isabelle and Kingsley were married for 66 years, and stayed together until Kingsley's death in 2024. The two loved each other deeply, and considered themselves to be soulmates.
Kingsley and Isabelle had seven children: Nancy (b. 1959), Simon (b. 1960), Jennifer (1963-1980), Olivia (b. 1969), David (b. 1972), Katrina (b. 1974), and Edwin (b. 1979). Kingsley was supportive towards his children, and loved them all. He also paid for their university tuitions, believing that university was very important. Kinglsey gave his unconditional love and support when Edwin came out of the closet as bisexual in 1993. In 1980, his daughter Jennifer went to an amusement park, and died after falling out of an upside-down rollercoaster at the age of seventeen. Kingsley was devastated, and mourned her. He attempted to sue the amusement park, but it had already dissolved and filed for bankruptcy before Kingsley assembled a legal team. In 1999, he pressured his son Simon, to get married, but Simon refused.
Later life
[
]
After retiring from politics, Kingsley retreated into a private life. He returned back to his birthplace of Glasgow, and while he appeared in a few commercials and talk shows, he mostly spent time with his family, and with his wife. His name was still quite famous, and he earned plenty of money from brand deals, endorsements, and his two books that he wrote. Kingsley was still interested in politics, as he would sometimes comment on current events, and in 2023, he went to the local legislature to check out the filibuster going on there.
Death
[
]
On July 30, 2024, Kingsley died while sleeping in the early hours of the morning, in Glasgow, Scotland. He was 99 years old. His funeral was held a month later on August 30, 2024, and his wife Isabelle and his six surviving children; Nancy, Simon, Olivia, David, Katrina, and Edwin. As a former Prime Minister of the United Kingdom, he was offered a state funeral, which he'd previously refused when he was alive, so his funeral was private. At the time of his death, Kingsley was worth £2.903 billion ($3,692,616,000 in US Dollars).
