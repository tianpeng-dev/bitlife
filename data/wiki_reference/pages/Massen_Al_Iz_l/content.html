Massen Ibn Adam Al Izîl (born January 19, 1951) is the sitting Crown Prince of Morocco and Sovereign Prince of Jihan in Eastern Morocco, the son of Adam Al Izîl, reigning King of Morocco. He is an infant Sanhaja boy from the House of Izîl, which claims descent from a royal lineage of Numidian Kings. According to family tradition, Massen was a direct patrilineal descendant of Masinissa through the 1st century Berber King Juba II.

Early Life[]

Massen Ibn Adam Al Izîl was born on January 19, 1951, in Rabat, to Adam Al Izîl, King of Morocco, and his wife Hafsa Kadmiri, Princess Consort of Morocco. As a newborn, he was greeted by a crowd of thousands, who cheered when they saw him presented over the palace balcony.

In 1953, Massen began cavorting around with Imad Gargach, the child of Palace Staff, and they became friends.

In 1955, Massen received a vaccination. He managed to stay calm while he was injected.

In 1957, Massen was enrolled at a private primary school at Little Doukkali, an all-boys school run by the overly strict but popular 71-year-old Youssef Bashir. He made friends with a classmate named Ayoub Baddour. By then, he loved to read. He then started taking guitar lessons. The same year, his parents bought him an Arabian horse with a white coat named Rusty, whom he started hunting with. That year, he insisted that his subjects refer to him as "your excellency." By then, Massen was stepping into public life, looking into potential laws in Morocco and engaging in public service like reading picture books to students at the National Library.

In 1958, Massen opened a new children's hospital in Agadir under his authority as Sovereign Prince of Jihan.
