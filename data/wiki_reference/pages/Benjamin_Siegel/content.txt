Benjamin "Bugsy" Siegel
was an American mobster, businessman, and casino operator who revolutionized gambling in Las Vegas, Nevada and was the driving force behind the development of the Las Vegas Strip. Known for his magnetic charisma, his gentlemanly demeanor, his extreme wealth and spending, his preference for cigars, and his signature checkered suit, Siegel was a high-profile member of the Italian-American Mafia, eventually taking over as the leader of its Nevada division.
After years of being Godfather and making hundreds of millions of dollars from both it and his casinos, Siegel decided that the mafia had outlived its usefulness and worked with the government to eradicate its presence in the Southwest in exchange for legal immunity and a sizable cut of seized mafia assets and property, using his newfound wealth to become Mayor of Las Vegas and transform it into a shining empire of vice, sin, and power, an everlasting oasis of light in the middle of the desert where the partying was eternal.
