Edward Poirier
(1900-2022) was a Canadian politician, social activist, soldier and supercentenarian. He served as mayor of Saskatoon for 12 years and was the longest-living person in the world.
Early Life
[
]
Edward Poirier was born on March 25, 1900. His father was a paralegal. He grew up in a wealthy family. In 1918 he graduated from the Saaskaton Secondary School.
Military Career
[
]
In 1918, he applied for political science studies and was accepted. In 1922 he graduated and tried to study law but was rejected. In 1924 he joined the Royal Canadian Air Force as a second lieutenant, which was formed that same year. In 1925 he was promoted to lieutenant. In the following years, he earned a pilot's license and was promoted to captain in the army. In 1931 his mother died and Edward inherited a considerable fortune. In 1932 he received an honorable discharge from Royal Canadian Air Force. He then bought a property with 6 rooms near Saaskaton. He spent the next few years resting and organizing charity events.
