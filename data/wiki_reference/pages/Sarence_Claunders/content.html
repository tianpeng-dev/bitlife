Sarence Claunders (January 1, 1914 - October 24, 2021) was a grocer from Amherst County, Virginia. He was a doppelganger of modern retail pioneer Clarence Saunders.

Life and career[]

Claunders was the son and only child (aside from half siblings) of LaSondra Martin (neé Rizzo) (1895 - 1987) and Vincent "Vinnie" Claunders (1855 - 1924). His father was an editor and his mother was a camerawoman.

Claunders became interested in grocery work at a young age. He was fascinated by the process of production and distribution of marketable supplies. In middle school, he was a member of the book, religious, sewing and recycling clubs. He became a secretary of the latter in his last year of middle school. In high school, he joined the theater program, pottery club, Dungeons and Dragons club, and Habitat for Humanity club. Claunders was a talented actor and eventually became the president of the drama club in his senior year. He was also active in Habitat for Humanity, helping fundraisers for homeless people and helping animals as well.

In 1932, Claunders enrolled in university, majoring in marketing. He was considered lucky to do so, given the hardships of the great depression. He was again a member of the Habitat for Humanity club, this time becoming president. He graduated in 1936. Claunders then applied to work for Adagio Supermarket. He was hired, starting as an apprentice. He earned the promotion to grocer in 1938.

Under the company's recommendation, Claunders retired in 1999 after working for Adagio Supermarket for 63 years.

Personal life[]

Claunders' wife was co-worker Lily Montana-Brady. They began dating during Sarence's first year on the job, and they married the next year. Their first child, a son, Dirk (1939 - 2016) was a flight attendant for Requium Air. Their second son, John (1943 - 2010) was a PI for Mr. Aria. Their other four children are daughters Shauna (born 1944), Whitney (born 1946), Cherish (born 1948), and Ana (born 1961). Shauna is a computer programmer for Hardwood Automotive, Whitney is an IT Support for Polar Energy, Cherish is a hairdresser for Abacus Salon, and Ana is a camerawoman for Crystal TV.

Claunders died on October 24, 2021 in his sleep, aged 107. He is survived by his wife, four surviving children (daughters), two grandchildren and half-nephew, Omari.
