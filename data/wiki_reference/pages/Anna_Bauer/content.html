Anna Bauer, Sr. (4397-January 1, 4445) was a German convicted murderer.

Early life (4397-4415)[]

Bauer was born in Stuttgart, Germany. She is the only child of Mattis Bauer and his girlfriend Franziska Schwarz.

In 4403, Bauer enrolled at Little Eggemann Primary School.

In 4407, Bauer enrolled at St. Juna’s Middle School.

In 4412, Bauer enrolled at Epsilon Secondary School.

Prison sentence (4415-4444)[]

In 4415, Bauer graduated from secondary school. She was convicted of murdering her first three husbands and sentenced to 39 years in prison. She was released on parole in 4444.

Death[]

On January 1, 4445, Bauer committed suicide.

Marriages and children[]

In 4408, Bauer started dating a student named Gabriel Keller (4397-4415). They got married in 4415. They have two children, Jannik II (4414-after 4465) and Anna Bauer, Jr. (4415-after 4465). Bauer murdered Keller with a set of plastic vampire teeth after making love to him.

In 4415, Bauer married an unemployed man named Malte Kant (4397-4415). She murdered him with a set of plastic vampire teeth after making love to him.

Less than a year after murdering Kant, Bauer married an unemployed man named Milo Schulte (4397-4415). She murdered him with a set of plastic vampire teeth after making love to him.

In 4444, Bauer married an unemployed man named Lasse Mayer (4426-4444). She murdered him with a set of plastic vampire teeth after making love to him.

Less than a year after murdering Mayer, Bauer married an unemployed man named Julius Bergmann (4426-4444). She murdered him with a set of plastic vampire teeth after making love to him.

Less than a year after murdering Bergmann, Bauer married an unemployed man named Lenny Billman (4426-4444). She murdered him with a set of plastic vampire teeth after making love to him.

Less than a year after murdering Billman, Bauer married an interior designer named Anton Werner (4417-4444). Werner had two children, Jasmin I (4441-after 4465) and Eberhard Werner from a previous marriage to a woman named Jana Debus.

Less than a year after Debus’s death, Bauer was engaged to a student named Jackson Thompson (4423-after January 1, 4445). They were married in 4445 and divorced less than a year later, leaving Bauer with $107 million in debt.
