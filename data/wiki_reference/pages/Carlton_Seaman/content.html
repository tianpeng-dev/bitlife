Carlton Seaman (1911-2021) was a veteran of the US Coast Guard, a cabin crew supervisor, and a famous author. Legally, he was the father of two children, though it is rumored that Layla was another man's child.

Early Life[]

Carlton Seaman was born to Tiffany Black and Gilbert Seaman in Jacksonville, USA. When Carlton was only 1, Gilbert Seaman married Goddess Serena, who became his stepmother. Goddess Serena disliked her stepson, refusing to even spend time with him until he was six. When Carlton was 10, his mother gave birth to his half-brother, Thomas Black. Two years later, Tiffany gave birth to Gabriel Peckerman, another half-brother of Carlton's

School Life[]

When he entered middle school, he joined the cross country team, the skateboarding club, and the chess club. Before graduating high school, Carlton became vice-president of the skateboarding club. When he was a freshman, he enrolled in both the Track team and the cross country team. Before graduating high school, he became the captain of the cross country club. He joined the Beta Theta Pi fraternity when he was 20, and became the captain of the cross country team when he was 21.

Military Career[]

After graduating university at 22, he enlisted in the US Coast Guard as an officer. When he was 42, he was sent on a deployment to Indonesia; he was given an award for his service. He was sent on another deployment to Georgia (the country) when he was 47; he was again rewarded with a medal. He was an Admiral before he was honorably discharged due to reaching the mandatory retirement age, 62.

Post Military Career[]

After his retirement from the Coast Guard, Carlton became a flight attendant; he was one for 23 years, before being forced to retire by Swagger Air. He attempted to sue the company for discrimination, but failed. Afterwards, he became a writer. When he was 93, he became famous for his work.

Personal Life:[]

Carlton was only 19 when his father died when he was 78 of old age.

When he was 28, he met Kayla Lord, a 35-year-old professor. He asked her to elope, but she refused. After Kayla accidently became pregnant, she had an abortion despite him not wanting her to. However, one year later, when the same thing happened again, she elected to keep the child. Carlton asked her to elope again, and she refused this time as well. He tried to propose to her when they were at a circus; she rejected him yet again. She suffered a first-trimester miscarriage the same year. The next year, he proposed to her at a botanical garden, but her answer was the same. A few months later, he asked her to elope, to no avail. Yet again, Kayla became pregnant, however she decided to have an abortion. On his 33rd birthday, he proposed to her in a hot tub, this time with a 2.56-carat sapphire ring. This time, she accepted. A few months later they married in a courthouse; Carlton kept his surname, Kayla hyphenated hers to Lord-Seaman. The same year Kayla had a first-trimester miscarriage. When Carlton was 37 and Kayla was 44, Jackson Seaman was born.

After finding out that his wife cheated on him while he was on his first deployment, Carlton waited until she gave birth to the other man's child. After she gave birth to Layla Lord-Seaman, he purchased a 15-year-old Chrysler Pacifica, which he proceeded to use to kill Kayla via drive-by. When the police came to arrest him, he escaped. Shockingly, the cops never found him, despite him being a member of the Coast Guard. He raised Layla Lord-Seaman as his own, never telling her that he was responsible for her mother's death.

Goddess Serena, Carlton's stepmother, died when she was 78 and Carlton was 67. Although she disliked him in the beginning, in the end she and Carlton loved each other mutually. The next death in his family was his mother, Tiffany Black, who died when she was 91 and he was 78. Eight years later, his half-brother Gabriel Peckerman died from a stroke when he was 74. The same year Carlton became famous, his half-brother Thomas Black died. Sixteen years, his son Jackson died from cancer, with only Jackson's children and Layla to comfort him.
