Matteo Garcia
was a Colombian criminal and mafia godfather, specializing in car theft. He was born on April 23, 1942, and died on January 8, 2016.

Biography
[
]
A difficult childhood
[
]
Matteo Garcia was born on April 23, 1942, in Medellin, Colombia. Born into a relatively well-off family, he lacked nothing but was raised the hard way and received little attention from his parents, who cared very little for him. And so, at just 11 years of age, he commits his first crime by burglarizing a house and stealing almost $10,000 worth of jewelry and various items of equipment, all with great success, which for a first experience is a great thing for him and makes him idealize the world of crime.
As for his social life, he's not the most popular at school, where he does well academically, but still has a small circle of trusted friends, to whom he'll share his most important secrets and his first misdemeanors and petty crimes in his early teens. It was at the age of 14, in 1956, that he made his first car theft. He successfully stole not one or two, but three, and was arrested by the police on the fourth car he tried to steal, subsequently serving 3 years in a juvenile reformatory, again in Medellin.
In 1959, at the age of 17, when he was released from a juvenile reformatory, he continued to steal cars on numerous occasions, and continued to do so regularly over the following years.
Coming of age and active criminal life
[
]
At just 18 years of age, Matteo has already amassed a small fortune of over $200,000 worth of stolen vehicles. He was then given the opportunity to join a criminal group, more precisely a Latin mafia, the Selva Family, in which he was easily recruited thanks to his talent for stealing vehicles. He is quickly noticed within the mafia for the money he brings in, since he has a production rate of over $125,000 in his first year, and although his talents don't allow him to be promoted very quickly, he is at least highly regarded by his mafia bosses.
Nevertheless, in the first few months of coming of age, he has the opportunity to have lots of one-night stands and catches a sexually transmitted infection: genital herpes, which he will never get rid of, although it won't actually destroy him.
As the years went by, Matteo continued to operate, gaining more and more respect from his mafia bosses, but still wasn't a highly respected figure, until he carried out his first murder, that of a poor mafia associate named Ezio Bucca, with whom he didn't get on well and whom, to show his power, he decided to kill by running him over with a stolen car. Although nobody knows the exact figures, it's likely that in the first 4 years after he joined the Selva family, Matteo stole between $700k and $1 million worth of vehicles.
It was at the age of 23 that Matteo was promoted and entered the real estate business. By this time, he owned two houses, with a total value of only a few hundred thousand dollars, and he took advantage of the fact that the Selva family was providing him with free accommodation and that he owned two properties to start renting them out, earning large sums of extra money every year, the equivalent of a whole salary. Two years later, he earns the nickname Purple Matteo, and gets another promotion. He also enlarges his small real estate holdings to ensure that his income grows in a legal way, so as to ensure a proper hedge against what he earns on the side.
A criminal career and a flourishing fortune
[
]
Barely out of his thirties, Purple Matteo is already a multimillionaire with a dozen of properties, all of which he rents out. He's already considered one of the best car thieves in the country, knowing the mechanics of each one as well as he does himself. In 1977, at the age of 35, he became the Mafia Underboss, but in 1979, two years later, his mother was killed by a rival mafia, something that angered Matteo more than anything, who vowed to kill one by one those who had themselves killed his mother. In 1981, he and his wife Shakira Garcia gave birth to their daughter, Catalina Garcia.
As the years went by, Purple Matteo's real estate holdings grew, earning him several million every year in addition to his criminal business. All went well for him, he was never prosecuted and earned a better living than ever before, and gave birth to two new children, a daughter, Gabriela Garcia, in 1984, and a little boy, Diego Garcia, in 1985.
A tragic downfall
[
]
In May 1985, Purple Matteo quietly continued his activities and gradually increased his wealth, but as he extorted a large sum of money from a company director, the latter calls the police on him and Matteo is arrested on July 17, 1985. At the scene of the arrest, Matteo was directly identified, and information about all the crimes committed was recorded. What's more, a large number of police attended the scene - a total of 18 officers, 7 detectives and the Medellin police chief - to celebrate his arrest, as he offered them 500k dollars in exchange for his freedom.
His trial lasted just three months, during which he called in the best lawyers for his defense, but although he faced a life sentence, he decided to plead guilty and was sentenced to just 45 years in a maximum-security prison in Colombia. It was claimed much later that, as soon as he arrived in the prison, he had already bribed the guards for his protection. During his prison sentence, he joined the Bitizen Mafia gang in order to obtain their protection too, and found work in the prison, but still remained one of the most feared and respected people during his sentence, despite the profile of the other prisoners. He also continues to receive some $2 million in rent each year from all his rentals, and is therefore financially secure.
Released from prison and witness protection
[
]
In 1990, Matteo Garcia tried to appeal, having served only 5 years of his 45-year sentence. Nevertheless, he was convincing throughout the appeal process and eventually won it, thus getting out of prison very quickly, with in addition to his rents another $10 million in his pocket, with a total fortune that year estimated at over $35 million. What's more, as soon as he was released from prison, he regained his position as Underboss of his mafia, allowing him to resume his business activities. He also invested his savings in the stock market, more specifically in bonds, in the months following his release.
But on August 9, 1992, he was arrested again on the same charges, as well as murder, after an operation by Colombian special forces on his large estate. Nevertheless, he was offered a deal: given his importance in his mafia, he could become an informer for the police, snitch on the most important members of the mafia and be pardoned for his crimes. To save his own skin, he decides to accept, and begins gathering information. And in 1951, as he continues to gather information, he is contacted by the police department responsible for his information-gathering to report to them, and an operation against his own mafia is carried out, wiping it out for good.
For his part, Matteo Garcia aka Purple Matteo had all charges against him pardoned and was put under a witness protection program, moving to Bangkok, Thailand, with his family, changing his identity to Chaisai Kumluk, although continuing his real estate activities in Colombia.
In Thailand, he is known locally for his large fortune, being the head of a very large estate, for example 100 million dollars in 1998, being the opposite extreme of the local people. Nevertheless, he was much appreciated, but fell into alcoholism in his fifties, probably as a result of all the events that had happened to him in life, and lived alone, surrounded only by his family and private staff such as his cook, butler and personal doctor, during the years following his escape from Colombia. His son Diego recounted some years after his death that Matteo had already been arrested several times by the police for drinking and driving, but that he always got away with it by bribing them.
Death
[
]
On 8 January 2016, Matteo Garcia, under the name of Chaisai Kumluk, died at the age of 74. He left behind a wife, 3 children, a life of hidden crime and over 170 million dollars. After his death, his son, Diego, and his eldest daughter, Catalina, each wrote a book about their father, which became relatively well-known, and which recounted their point of view and many things about their father..
Income and assets
[
]
Total income
[
]
In the early years of his career as a criminal, Matteo Garcia secured a substantial income from vehicle theft, but gradually he detached himself from these criminal financial needs, earning almost all his money through real estate. Around the 1930s, he began to earn several million every year, and in the 1950s, until his death, he earned up to several tens of millions, mainly from the real estate he continued to sell in Colombia, as well as from the stock market, specifically high-risk bonds, which appealed to Matteo Garcia and enabled him to earn a great deal of money.
Assets
[
]
Matteo Garcia's fortune is very substantial, and can be divided into two parts: real estate and material assets, with properties, vehicles and works of art, as well as substantial liquid assets. Before his fiftieth birthday, he had already accumulated a fortune of over 35 million, which doubled in 4 years, reaching 70 million dollars in 1953.
Real estate and assets
[
]
Real estate is a major part of Matteo Garcia's fortune. He owned dozens of properties in Colombia and Thailand, as well as a large number of land, water and air vehicles, including several private planes, 2 large yachts and a dozen cars at the end of his life.
Substantial liquidity
[
]
Throughout his life, Matteo Garcia has chosen to set aside very large sums of money. This decision was beneficial for him, as it enabled him to defend himself in court and to appeal (he spent almost a million dollars in legal fees for these two legal proceedings), or even to start a new life of luxury in Thailand, becoming one of the richest personalities there at the end of his life. It is said that he often kept very large sums of money in cash, such as the 500,000 dollars found in his office on the estate when he was arrested on August 9, 1950, or the more than 10 million dollars found throughout his main estate when he died, as well as several million dollars found in Colombia in hidden areas belonging to him.
