Maria Sandu-Mureșan (1584-1684) was a daughter of Barbu Sandu-Mureșan and Ionela Ilie. She was a fashion icon in the 17th century Wallachia.

Fashion Icon[]

Maria wasn't allowed to have a job, because of her status, so she became a fashion designer, introducing western fashion in Wallachia throughout the first half of the 17th century.

Personal Life[]

Maria married rich nobleman Andi Dumitrescu (1584-1675) in 1604 and got 4 children:

Anca Dumitrescu (1605-1668), who never married but had an illegitmate son, Rolan Ciobanu (1634-?), who married Ramona Rădulescu (1640-?) in 1661

Mihnea Dumitrescu (1608-1696)

Radu Dumitrescu (1613-1690), who married Ramona Popescu (1627-?) in 1660 and had a daughter, Bia (1661-?)

Drăghici Dumitrescu (1618-1682), who married Orsi Enache (1615-?) in 1649 and got three daughters, Carmen (1650-?), Orsi (1653-?) and Emese (1655-?)

In 1646, her cousin died and Maria adopted his son, Alexandru Stan (1644-?), who took the name Sandu-Mureșan. In 1669 he married Laura Badea (1640-?) and got 4 children, Bia (1673-?), Anna (1675-?), Zoltan (1677-?), Andrei (1681-?).

Maria became depresed because of he deaths of her daughter in 1668, her husband in 1675 and of her son in 1682. Maria lived for 100 years and died in 1684.
