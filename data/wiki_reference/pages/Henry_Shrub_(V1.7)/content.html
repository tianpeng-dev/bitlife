Henry Michael Shrub (October 21, 1884 - February 11, 1962), also known by his initials, HMS, was an American lawyer, judge, fighter pilot and politician who served as the President of the United States from his election in 1960 until his death in his second year in office in 1962. Shrub also served as Mayor of New Haven from 1924 to 1932, the Governor of Connecticut from 1932 to 1936, and as School Board Director from 1940 to 1948. As a lawyer, he was most prominent with the firm Fudge & Duffy, at which he worked from 1914 until his mayoral election in 1924. He also worked with Osborne Associates for several years. As a judge, he served as a magistrate for the United States District Court for the District of Connecticut for twelve years until his victorious presidential election.
