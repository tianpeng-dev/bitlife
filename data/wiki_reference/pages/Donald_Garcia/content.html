Donald Cornelius Garcia (May 26, 1908 - September 14, 2022) was a Hawaiian-American musician who specialized in blues music. A supercentenarian, Garcia holds the record for one of the longest music careers in the world, a total of nine decades from 1932 to his death in 2022.

Early life and education[]

Garcia was conceived in a medical hut in Honolulu, Territory of Hawaii. His mother was a grocer named Katelyn Rockefeller (later Reed; 1867 - 1956), allegedly related to John D. Rockefeller. His father was a receptionist named Landon Garcia (1839 - 1909), who retired several years before Donald's birth. Landon died when Donald was a year old, leaving him to be raised by his stepfather, professor Amani Reed (1869 - 1941 or 1942).

Music career[]

After completing graduate school in 1932, Garcia embarked on his music career.

Personal life[]

Garcia met his wife, Shanice Reed (1908 - 1974) in 1925 when they were both seventeen. After five years of dating, they married in 1930. They had two children together, including Brittany (born 1934), a policy analyst, and Travis (1944 - 2020), a museum docent at Sonata Foundation. Shanice died in 1974 when she was hit by a car. Donald cremated her, sprinkling her ashes at her favorite beach. Travis passed away in 2020 from old age, only a couple years before Donald would follow.

In August 1959, following the passage of the Hawaii Admission Act and the admittance of Hawaii as the 50th and final state, Garcia became a naturalized American citizen. After being widowed for over twenty years, Garcia met his second wife, soldier Kiera Santos (1971 - 2072), in 1998. They got married that same year and had five children together:

  • Deandre (born 2001)
  • Jesus (born 2003)
  • Kevin (born 2005)
  • Velma (born 2016)
  • Colt (born 2021)

Death[]

On September 14, 2022, Garcia died at the grand old age of 114 years, 3 months, and 19 days. He is survived by his second wife, Kiera, and six surviving children.

Legacy[]

He is famous for his music and is one of the most famous musicians of Hawaiian History.
