Abby Ortiz (née Butkus) (January 4, 1905 – January 11, 2010) was an American lawyer. She gained recognition in her later years for her remarkably good health and 13-year-long cancer journey, after being diagnosed with pancreatic cancer at the age of 92.

Early Life[]

Abby Ortiz was born as Abby Butkus on January 4, 1905 in Las Vegas, Nevada, USA. Her single mother, Genesis Butkus (formerly Poindexter; March 8, 1880 – August 9, 1966), was impregnated by an anonymous donor. When Ortiz was 1, her mother married Javier Poindexter, a police officer, her stepfather. Through this marriage, Ortiz had 2 stepsiblings; Tara (June 5, 1892 – April 14, 1968) and Duke Poindexter (July 16, 1897 – December 20, 1981). She also had two half-siblings; Lynn Poindexter (March 31, 1909 – January 16, 2003) and Christian Carter (January 27, 1913 – October 4, 1989). Her mother and stepfather divorced in 1912.

Despite her poor relationship with her stepfather, she kept in touch with her ex-stepsiblings after their parents’ divorce. Ortiz was especially fond of her stepsister Tara, who often defended her against bullies in her teen years.

Career[]

When she was 16, Ortiz got a part-time job as a pizza maker at a small Las Vegas pizzeria. After graduating from high school, she applied to university to study criminal justice and fulfill her dream of becoming a lawyer. She graduated in 1927 with an undergraduate degree, after which she applied to graduate school. In 1930, she applied to law school, which she completed in 1933. During her later studies, she worked as a collections specialist and a secretary, respectively.

Ortiz was hired as a junior associate for Bush & Associates in October 1933. After years of hard work, she was promoted to associate in 1937. She was later promoted to junior partner in 1941 and partner in 1945. She continued working at the company for nearly 40 years, until her retirement in 1971.

Personal Life[]

Ortiz began dating her high school classmate, Kendrick Dunn in 1922. The couple were engaged for a brief period of time before eventually breaking up in 1929.

She bought an apartment in downtown Las Vegas in 1934, as she got fed up with her mom constantly bugging her to move out. Due to the student loans she had to pay back, she was forced to sell her apartment the following year and buy a smaller one.

She began dating Jeremy Vazquez in 1934. They moved in together in early 1937 and had a daughter, Camille Vazquez (August 10, 1937 – December 31, 2020). The pair split up in December of the same year.

Ortiz met her future husband, Elmer Ortiz (October 14, 1892 – March 26, 1964) in late 1937. They had a daughter together; Candida Ortiz (September 14, 1939 – May 16, 1978). They later adopted a girl, Miley Ortiz (né Bruno; April 12, 1952 – May 9, 2024), whose parents were killed in a plane crash. The couple were married on August 2, 1942 at a fancy Las Vegas restaurant. They moved to a quiet suburb of Las Vegas to raise their children. In 1956, the family bought a bigger house in the same neighborhood.

Later Life[]

Ortiz was widowed in 1964, after her husband suffered a stroke. Later that year, her first grandchild, Alonzo Peckerman, was born. In 1982, she sold her contemporary home and moved in with her daughter Camille.

Health and Longevity[]

Ortiz adapted to the Mediterranean diet when she was 36. She had generally good health throughout her life, up until her 80s. She was diagnosed with arthritis in 1988, at the age of 83.

In 1997, Ortiz was diagnosed with pancreatic cancer. Her daughter Camille, who was also diagnosed with cancer when she was just 2 years old, but made a full recovery shortly after, said she had very low hopes for her mother’s survival, as did most relatives and friends. Despite Ortiz’s advanced age and already existing health issues, after years of continuous treatment, her condition began to improve.

In January 2005, she celebrated her 100th birthday. On this occasion, she was visited by then-mayor of Las Vegas, Oscar Goodman.

Death[]

Ortiz succumbed to her illness at her daughter’s home in Las Vegas, Nevada, USA on January 11, 2010, a week after celebrating her 105th birthday. She was survived by two of her three children, her two grandchildren, and many nieces and nephews. A public funeral service was held on January 23, 2010.
