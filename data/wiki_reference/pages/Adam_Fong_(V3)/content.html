Azura Fong (born Adam Fong; April 6, 1954 - September 10, 2022) was a Malaysian royal family member who served as Queen of Malaysia from 2000 until her conviction of murder in 2016. A transgender woman, she is considered to be the first LGBT person in Malaysian history to ascend the royal family throne.

Early life[]

Born a male, Adam Fong was the Prince of Suhalia in Kuala Lumpur, the son of Queen Natasha (Natasha Wang; 1930 - 2000) and Prince Consort Ishtiqal Fong (1910 - 1987). Ishtiqual had been a royal military officer in the Malaysian Army during the Second World War.
