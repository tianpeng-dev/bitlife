Henry Michael Shrub (October 21, 1914 - February 11, 1992), also known by his initials, HMS, was an American lawyer, judge, fighter pilot and politician who served as the President of the United States from his election in 1990 until his death in his second year in office in 1992. Shrub also served as Mayor of New Haven from 1954 to 1962, the Governor of Connecticut from 1962 to 1966, and as School Board Director from 1970 to 1978. As a lawyer, he was most prominent with the firm Fudge & Duffy, at which he worked from 1944 until his mayoral election in 1954. He also worked with Osborne Associates for several years. As a judge, he served as a magistrate for the United States District Court for the District of Connecticut for twelve years until his victorious presidential election. Shrub was also a distinguished military officer during World War II.
