Theodore Frederick Bunker
(March 30, 1943 - September 13, 2022) was an American politician and United States Navy sailor who served as the Mayor of Kingsville from 1993 to 2001.

Early life and education
[
]
Teddy was born in Kingsville, Texas to Darlene (1914 - 1990) and Jacob Bunker (1911 - 1979). His mother was an insurance agent and his father was a librarian who served in the Army during World War II. He had an older sister named Olivia (born 1941).
Naval career
[
]
Bunker enlisted in the Navy in 1961 after graduating from high school.
...
After twenty years of service, Bunker retired from the Navy in 1981 at the age of 38.
Politics
[
]
After retiring from the Navy, Bunker enrolled at Texas A&I University-Kingsville, Class of 1985, majoring in political science. After graduating in 1985, he decided to run for the office of School Board Director. His opponent was Daisy Colon-Espinoza (born 1944).
Personal life
[
]
Bunker met fellow sailor Natalie Davis (born 1939) in 1964. After a few years of friendship, they began dating in 1967, and they heartily stole each others' virginities a year later during the peak of the Vietnam War. Five years later, their first child, a son named
Cornelius
, was born in 1973. Bunker and Davis' second child, a daughter named Pamela, was born in 1978. A year after that, they finally got married after Natalie proposed to proposed to Teddy on a farm, to which he accepted; this was a moment of irony, because Teddy had proposed to Natalie in the years prior while they dated, and she kept rejecting him. Natalie gave birth to their third child, a daughter named Maddie, in 1982. Cornelius, inspired by his father's military service, joined the Marines in the early 1990s after finishing high school. Pamela works as an Internal Auditor at Everest Insurance, and Maddie is a psychiatrist.
Cornelius died in a car accident in 2004. Shortly afterwards, Teddy and his family established the Cornelius Bunker Foundation to support road safety for all drivers, even veterans.
Death
[
]
Bunker died on September 13, 2022 at the age of 79 in a freak accident involving a crocodile. He is survived by his four siblings, wife, two daughters, and any potential grandchildren.
