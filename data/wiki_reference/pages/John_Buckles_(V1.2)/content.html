John Buckles (September 11, 1923 - June 6, 1944) was an American soldier who participated in the Normandy invasion of D-Day in World War II. He was killed in action while storming Omaha Beach. Buckles was posthumously awarded and honored for his heroism.

Early life[]

John was born in Sacramento, California to Cassandra Buckles (1894 - 1969), a real estate agent. From his recollection, he never met his biological father, who abandoned him at birth.

Military career[]

Buckles enlisted in the Army in 1941, close to the time of the Japanese attack on Pearl Harbor. His platoon members included Robert "Bob" Eastwood (early 1920s - ????), Buster Brian (1904 - 1950), Charles Little (mid-1920s - ????), Julius Snatch (late 1910s - ????), Luther Wallace (early 1920s - ????), Maxwell "Max" Hole (early 1920s - ????), and Matthew "Matt" Ryder (late 1910s - ????).

D-Day and death[]

In June of 1944, Buckles was among the many soldiers who were to be deployed on Omaha Beach as part of the invasion of Normandy, France. In the days leading up to the attack, he would spend time mingling with his fellow soldiers on his ship on the English Channel, and also write letters to his friends and family back home. On June 6, Buckles stormed the beaches with his platoon, dodged as many bullets as possible, and took down several Nazis along the way. Although he initially managed to clear the beach, he was shot by an enemy soldier while scaling the cliffs. Buckles eventually succumbed to his mortal wounds.

Personal life[]

Buckles met Farah Groper (born 1923) at the age of seven in 1930. They started going out as a couple in 1932 and became engaged in 1944. They planned to be married whenever Buckles would be placed on leave, but the wedding ultimately never came to fruition. Groper received the bad news after putting out a fire.

Legacy and honors[]

For his heroic actions on D-Day, Buckles posthumously received several medals and honors, including a Purple Heart and Bronze Star, both of which are the primary markings of his gravesite.
