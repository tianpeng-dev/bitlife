Joseph Kristof Simmons (May 15, 1930 - May 21, 2021) was an American author.

Early life[]

Joe was born in Atlanta, Georgia. His mother, Monica Simmons, was a travel agent. His father, Norville "Shaggy" Simmons, was an Army officer, which accounted for the family having to move every once in a while. He had an older sister, Rebel, who worked as a college professor. His grandfather, Kristof Simmons, originally grew up in Norway before he and his family immigrated to America in the mid-1880s.

Simmons had a deep connection with his friends and family, especially with the family mongrel, Penelope, the namesake of one of Joe's daughters. He showed a huge interest in reading and writing as a child and teen. Whenever he wasn't feeling his smartest, he would find a good book to read to sharpen his mind.

Career[]

In 1948, at 18 years old, Simmons was hired by the Boanno Press for their Writer opening. He started off with short stories before transitioning to feature-length novels. In the early 1950s, Simmons was supposedly drafted to serve in the Korean War, but there are no records of his name in the lottery.

In 1955, Simmons achieved fame with his novel, (TBA).

Personal life[]

Simmons met his future wife, Carson Bennet, an architect, in 1949. After two years of dating, they married in 1951. Their first child, son Jaylen, was born a year later.

Death[]

From complications of old age, Simmons died peacefully in his sleep on May 21, 2021, at the ripe old age of 91, six days after his birthday. He is survived by his wife, seven children and ten grandchildren. His net worth was $71,588,770.
