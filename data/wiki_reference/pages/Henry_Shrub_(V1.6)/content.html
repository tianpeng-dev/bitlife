Henry Michael Shrub (October 21, 1894 - February 11, 1972), also known by his initials, HMS, was an American lawyer, judge, fighter pilot and politician who served as the President of the United States from his election in 1970 until his death in his second year in office in 1972. Shrub also served as Mayor of New Haven from 1934 to 1942, the Governor of Connecticut from 1942 to 1946, and as School Board Director from 1950 to 1958. As a lawyer, he was most prominent with the firm Fudge & Duffy, at which he worked from 1924 until his mayoral election in 1934. He also worked with Osborne Associates for several years. As a judge, he served as a magistrate for the United States District Court for the District of Connecticut for twelve years until his victorious presidential election.
