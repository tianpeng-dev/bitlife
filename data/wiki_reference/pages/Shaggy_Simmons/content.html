Norville "Shaggy" Simmons (1900 - 1979) was a US Army officer who served during both world wars, the Korean War, and even the Vietnam War. He was the father of famous author Joe Simmons.

Early life[]

Simmons was born in Georgia in 1900. His father, Kristof Simmons, was born in Norway and spent part of his childhood there before immigrating to America in the 1880s.

Career[]

Shaggy had supposedly begun his military career in the mid to late 1910s during World War I. He recieved three promotions during Joe's childhood, young adult and adult years:

  • Major (1933)
  • Lieutenant Colonel (1939)
  • Colonel (1945)
  • Brigadier General (alleged posthumous honorary promotion)

He retired from the Army in 1962, at age 62. He recieved a medal of honor or two for his courageous fighting in both world wars, Korea and Vietnam.

Personal life[]

Shaggy and his wife, Monica, a travel agent, had their first child, Rebel in 1926. Their son, Joe, was born four years later.

Shaggy died in 1979, aged 79 years. Monica followed ten years later.
