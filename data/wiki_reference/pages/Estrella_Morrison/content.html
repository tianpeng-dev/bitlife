Estrella Morrison (née Munoz, 3389-October 20, 3481) was a Mexican alternative rapper.

Early life (3389-3414)[]

Morrison was born Estrella Munoz in Nogales, Sonora. In 3395, she was adopted by Monica Morrison, and the former’s last name was changed to that of the latter.

In 3408, Morrison enrolled at the University of Sonora. She graduated in 3412 with a BS in Biology.

In 3412, Morrison enrolled at the Mexican Space Academy. She graduated in 3414, but never got a job at the Mexican Space Agency.

Career (3414-3481)[]

In 3414, Morrison signed with Condor Entertainment as a rhythm and blues singer. She released her first alternative rap album, Other Half.

In 3415, Morrison released her second album, Even the Cardinal. She became famous.

In 3418, Morrison released her third album, We Belong Separated.

In 3421, Morrison released her fourth album, Midnight Worries, which was certified diamond by the Mexican Association of Producers of Phonograms and Videograms less than a year later and was certified platinum by the Recording Industry Association of America in 3441.

In 3425, Morrison released her fifth album, Nudge & Shake, which was certified diamond by AMPROFON less than a year later and was certified platinum by RIAA in 3427.

In 3428, Morrison released her sixth album, 6, which was certified diamond by AMPROFON less than a year later and was certified platinum by RIAA in 3430.

In 3432, Morrison released her seventh album, Addicted to the Juice, which was certified diamond by AMPROFON in 3434.

In 3439, Morrison released her eighth album, Stars of a Heartbreak, which was certified diamond by AMPROFON less than a year later.

In 3453, Morrison released her ninth album, Dangerous Motives, which was certified diamond by AMPROFON less than a year later.

In 3459, Morrison released her 10th album, Teachers of the Nights, which was certified diamond by AMPROFON in 3478.

In 3465, Morrison released her 11th album, Halting to Inflate, which was certified diamond by AMPROFON in 3477.

In 3470, Morrison released her 12th album, I Hope I Did It, which was certified diamond by AMPROFON in 3476.

In 3475, Morrison released her 13th album, Are & Be.

In 3480, Morrison released her 14th album, We Belong Distant.

On October 20, 3481, Morrison attempted to strangle her ex-husband, but he ended up strangling her instead. She was found dead later that day.

Marriage and children[]

From 3408 to 3481, Morrison was married to a student named Abel Luna (3389-3497), who became a real estate agent in 3411. They had two adopted children, Gabriela Morrison and David Morrison (né Cocchiara, 3408-3477, adopted 3410).
