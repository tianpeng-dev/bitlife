Ivy Sangeloa
(February 26, 1930 - April 16, 1984) was an American actress, model, and soldier. After serving in the United States Air Force for twenty years, she embarked on a career of acting and modeling that spanned the last fifteen years of her life.

Early life
[
]
Ivy was born in San Angelo, Texas to Mikayla (1911 - 1990 or 1991) and Henry Sangeloa (1883 - 1970). Her mother worked as a trucker, while her father was a graphic designer and World War I veteran. She had three brothers, Gaylord (born 1929), Winston (born 1932), and Latrell (1950 - 1976). Gaylord and Winston are receptionists for Royalty Mills and Lilac Corp, respectively. Latrell was a magician for Abacus Circus; he died in 1976 when he was struck in the head with a golf ball. Ivy had a pet rabbit named Bun (1930 - 1936), whom she had a close bond as a toddler. Bun died when Ivy was six years old, having succumbed to intestinal issues after eating walnuts. Her best friend was Martha Grace (born 1930), whom she met when she was two.
Sangeloa was interested in both acting and modeling from a young age. She began taking voice lessons and acting lessons at the age of eight, and modeling lessons at age ten.
Air Force service
[
]
Sangeloa was also interested in the military, having grown up in the same city of an Air Force base. After graduating high school, Class of 1948, she enlisted in the Air Force.

Acting and Modeling
[
]
Following her retirement from the Air Force, Sangeloa embarked on her acting career in 1968. The timing of her retirement was perfect, as it took place during the peak of the Vietnam War.
Personal life
[
]
Ivy married her childhood friend and high school sweetheart, Anthony Worthy (born 1930) in 1952. Worthy is a cameraman at Prominence Broadcasting Service. Together, they had two daughters, Avery (born 1954) and Camille (born 1955), and two sons, Marquis (born 1958) and Kevin (1968 - 1979). Avery is a receptionist for Aurora Data, Camille is a real estate agent for Manhole Realty, and Marquis is a mailman. Kevin died in 1979 at the age of 11 from sickle cell disease.
Death
[
]
During her service, Sangeloa had developed hay fever. Later, during her acting and modeling career, she developed bunions. In 1980, during the filming of a scene of a film adaptation of
Frankenstein
, she consumed heroin as a method of getting into character. It was at that point that she, at the age of 50, became a heroin addict. Rather than get treated for it, she decided to continue focusing on her career and see how long she could survive, which was not very long. Ultimately, she died from an overdose on April 16, 1984 at the age of 54. Her family, including her still-living mother (at the time), her husband, her children, and her two brothers, attended her funeral, which also included military honors due to her service in the Air Force.
Despite the official manner of Ivy's death being listed as accidental, personal reports from family and friends suggest that she overdosed on purpose as a method of suicide. Members of both her immediate and extended family, and most of her closest friends, including her best friend, Martha Grace, claim that she was suicidal due to the losses she suffered throughout her life. The only piece of evidence against the theory of suicide is that Ivy was never officially diagnosed with suffering from depression.
Mikayla passed away between 1990 and 1991, aged 79 or 80.
