Lola Cahill-Jordan
(born
Princess Lola of Philipps
; July 2, 1986–November 12, 2076, fictional
bitlife
character) was a British royal, actress, author, cult leader, and convicted murderer known for her widely reported departure from the
monarchy
and subsequent career in film and television and criminal trajectory in the later years of her life.
Born  into the House of Philipps at Blenheim Palace, Cahill's adolescence was adorned by media fascination and scrutiny. As a teenager she became notorious for her anti-royal antics and relationship with commoner Arthur Thompson, which resulted in a high profile royal teenage pregnancy. In 2005, aged 18, she renounced her royal title and relocated to the United States to pursue an acting career.
Cahill made her acting debut in the 2006 musical film
The Phantasm of the Opera
. Her breakthrough came the following year with the thriller
Slashed
, a commercial and critical success that earned a
Golden Globe
nomination for Best Thriller Film. Cahill's acting career was extremely successful with early commercial success and award acclaim in
The Lunch Lady
(2007) and her transition into television with the ABC sitcom
Meet Me at the Funeral Home
(2008–2013) being both profitable and critically recognised, the latter receiving multiple
Primetime
Emmy
nominations. Cahill won the award for
Outstanding Lead Actress in a Comedy Series
on multiple occasions in the late 2010s and early 2020s and was one of the highest paid actresses in the industry.
Cahill's later years saw her engage in criminal activity, including murders of multiple family members, most notably a manslaughter conviction in 2025 that led to the end of her acting career. She quickly became involved in organised crime with the Gigante crime family and famously escaped prison multiple times. Cahill also led the global cult
Way of Lola
for multiple decades, which became a multi-million dollar enterprise.
Throughout her life, Cahill published five different books detailing her life, with the most notable being
Entitled
(2017) which sold near to 8 million copies and detailed her famous wild child days and shift to traditional celebrity status and her 'legendary'

Confessions of a Teenage Princess
(2075) which detailed her crimes and secrets throughout her 8 decades of life, which became one of the best selling books in history with over 60 million copies sold.

Early life and education
[
]
Princess Lola was born on July 2, 1986, in Oxford, United Kingdom at the historic
Blenheim Palace
, the ancestral residence of the House of Philipps. An estimated 6,823 people gathered in Oxford, including dignitaries, foreign royals, and esteemed guests. Public festivities, parades, and receptions were held across the country, marking the birth of the princess as a significant event in the royal family’s history. Lola was reportedly born into a fortune of £13.9 million in her trust fund.
Lola was enrolled at the private Little Canterbury Primary School in September 1991, where school life was initially hard for her as her classmates struggled to socialise with her due to her huge public status, however by age nine she had become popular amongst her classmates, befriending a large portion of the school and garnering a reputation for being kind. In September 1997, Lola enrolled at the private Selhurst Secondary School. Lola enrolled in acting classes in 1998, aged twelve. The murder of her classmate Oliver Adams made global news in 1999, with Lola being constantly questioned by the press at just 12 years old about the impact of the murder. Lola joined her schools basketball team in 2000, and began posing for different press outlets, gaining media backlash for being dressed "too provocatively", with which she argued back with saying she was dressing the same way as the rest of her 14-year old age mates. Around this time, she also began what the media would deem "acting out" against the royal family standards. It reached headline news in May 2002 when it was leaked to the press, widely believed to be by her fellow classmates, that she lost her virginity to her boyfriend Arthur Thompson at age 16, which the relationship already faced media scrutiny due to Thompson's commoner status. The rumours were confirmed when Lola gave birth to her first child at age 17.
Royal life and career
[
]
1992–1998: Early royal duties
[
]
From age six, Lola participated in public royal duties such as clean-ups as pushed by her parents. In 1995, a plaque honouring a then 9-year old Princess Lola and her royal duties was unveiled in Oxford. The following year, she gained large media praise for her 1996 fundraiser and Gamwystle Castle for a dog charity. Once Lola began secondary school in 1997, the media attention on her became stronger, with a widespread interest on how her behaviour would change in comparison to her widely known kind hearted child personality.
1999–2004: Press backlash and scrutiny, teen pregnancy, public disservice and abdicating royal status
[
]
On August 5, 1999, a series of photos were leaked to numerous press outlets including unseen royal family photos from Lola's personal camera, as well as private photos of a then 13-year old Lola. In May 2000, Lola received backlash from the press for paying large amounts of money from her trust fund to sway the votes of competition television show "
British Idol
". She also began committing public disservice that the press labelled as 'childish', such as attempting to rename historical sites after herself & offering "park and ride" zones on her lap in summer 2001. She also began dating her commoner boyfriend Arthur Thompson that November, being pictured together at football games and other public events.
Arthur Thompson
, Lola's long-term boyfriend (2001–2014)
The rise of
paparazzi
in the 2000s coincided with Lola's final years of royal life and subsequent media scandals. Throughout 2002, pictures of Lola & Arthur were amongst the most in-demand topics of paparazzi, selling for high amounts. The public reception differed in the United States and the United Kingdom, where in the U.K they received constant backlash for Thompsons commoner status, however in the U.S. the teen romance was extremely popular and adored, especially by fellow teens. Lola revealed in a mid-August 2002 interview with The Guardian that she had became pregnant with Arthur's baby earlier that month, with the news reaching global headlines less than 24 hours later due to Lola being freshly 16. The royal family quickly followed with a campaign to salvage their reputation, having Lola present checks for near to £50 million to a charity. Lola was pictured everywhere with her baby bump, marking the death of what was left of her royal reputation. Lola constantly egged on the media storm, wearing tees with provoking messages, such as "Guess Who’s Not a Virgin?" and "Future Monarch in Here". Around this time, a sub positive public opinion started growing amongst anti-monarchs of Lola for her 'humane-like' behaviour and life despite her status, like the best-selling early 2003
Entertainment Weekly
magazine that showcased Arthur's acne problems. Amber Cahill-Thompson was born on May 15, 2003, with another media storm taking place surrounding the 'royal bastard'.
In July 2004, Lola began being pictured at high-profile nightclubs in London, just days following her 18th birthday and completion of school. That same summer, the media began speculating around Lola's facial appearance, with rumours of plastic surgery floating around. Lola made her non-royal television debut in January 2005 when she appeared on
The Late Late Show with Millie Davis
. Public response to the episode was surprisingly positive, with many praising her personality and believing her 'wild teen years' were blown out of proportion by the media. In March 2005, Lola announced her second pregnancy with Thompson, giving birth to Isabelle Cahill-Thompson on December 12, 2005. Just a week before she became pregnant in March 2005, aged 18, Lola infamously appeared as the nude centrefold in Flesh Magazine. The magazine shoot made a point to the public that Lola no longer cared for her royal status. She also appeared in a commercial for
Mason Airways
, both of these ventures earning her an estimated £4.5 million alone, and established Lola's aim to transition into a Hollywood figure. She was also reportedly offered £34,000 to write a book by
Kelly Books
, capitalising on her widely speculated teenage years. Lola also continued to be photographed at nightclubs, with an
US Weekly
magazine cover showing her taking xanax. Lola appeared at premieres and other celebrity events throughout 2005.
On July 20, 2005, Lola Cahill officially abdicated as Princess of Philipps, surrendering Gamwystle Castle. She relocated to the United States with Thompson and her two children the following week to pursue her long awaited acting career.
Acting career
[
]
2005–2012: Early acting successes,
Meet Me at the Funeral Home
[
]
On August 3, 2005, it was announced that Princess Lola had signed with acting talent agent Selena Zuckerberg, under her full birth name Lola Cahill. Cahill was soon cast in the 2006 musical film
The Phantasm of the Opera
, playing a small role as an opera singer. According to Cahill, the role was the first time she had truly experienced "a humble life", not being the constant centre of attention. The film released in March 2006, and despite its small budget, was a commercial flop grossing $500,000 against a $930,000 budget, however it received immense critical acclaim, aiding Cahill's attempt to be taken seriously as an actor against public belief that she only was able to land the role because of her pre-existing fame. Cahill was reportedly paid under $20,000 for the role.
In April 2006, Cahill was cast in the thriller film
Slashed
as Aurora Roberts. She released her first book, "Let Me Explain!" in May, being well received, however critics noted that it was very early for Cahill to be writing a book about her life.
Slashed
was released on October 6, 2006, grossing $12 million against its $2.4 million budget. Besides its commercial success, the film received critical praise and a nomination for Best Thriller Film at the
2007 Golden Globes
. On January 8, 2007, director Rebel Stone-Khan announced Cahill was to join the cast of the horror film
The Lunch Lady
. The set environment was reportedly sour between Cahill and 57-year old lead Jenny Delgado-Frost, with the two feuding. Cahill claimed that she tried to get on a good foot with the senior actress, who treated her unkindly in response, and a feud took place. Reports that there was only a $10,000 pay gap between Cahill and Delgado-Frost's salary despite the latter's lead credit and Cahill's closeness to director Stone-Khan helped aid Delgado-Frost's disdain for the younger actress. The Lunch Lady was released in October to critical and commercial success, grossing $30.73 million against its $5.9 million budget and received a nomination for Best Picture at the
2008 Golden Globes
.
In early 2008, Cahill and Thompson officially engaged after seven years of dating. Cahill joined the cast of the third season of
ABC
sitcom
Meet Me at the Funeral Home
as Sophie Stanley, becoming her first ever role she would be paid over $1 million for. Cahill gained a positive reputation for treating extras kindly on set, constantly buying gifts for them. Season four received an
Emmy
nomination for Best Sitcom. Cahill gave birth to her first son, and third child Gavin in 2010.
2013–2019: Massive television success and
"Entitled"
[
]
In March 2013,
Meet Me At The Funeral
was renewed for an eighth season on ABC. Cahill announced that she would not be returning to the show after 120 episodes across five seasons. Her departure was seen as a big loss for a show already on life support, that had already declined significantly in viewership and popularity, dropping off hugely after its fourth season. The series continued to receive critical acclaim and renewals however, with Cahill being deemed the standout factor of the show. Just three weeks later, Cahill was cast in the new ensemble sitcom
The Genius Effect
on
NBC
as Patty. She was reportedly offered $560,000 an episode upon signing. Upon the series premiere in September 2014, Cahill's public status immediately boomed in the United States. She was paid over $11.6 million for the first season and earned a nomination for
Best Supporting Actress
at the Emmys, her first ever award nomination.
The Genius Effect
was immediately renewed for a second season. Cahill earned the award for Best Supporting Actress, two years in a row for her work on season two and three.
The Genius Effect
was not renewed for a third season due to low ratings, despite its acclaim and praise.
In November 2014, Cahill and Thompson broke up after 13 years together. Cahill later revealed that the relationship wasn't as "sweet" as it was presented to the public, stating that it was a relationship filled with pressure. She later revealed that the infamous media spectacle of her losing her virginity to him in 2002 was a result of him pressuring her to do so, as well as the teen pregnancies being a result of his irresponsibility. She also revealed that he would argue with her when she would do provocative shoots, and that he was in debt for many years until she paid off the debt herself.
In June 2016, Cahill joined the cast of
Metropolitan Affairs
' seventh season as the new lead of the series, Mary Branning. She made headline news as she joined the list of highest paid actresses and actors, receiving $1.3 million per episode. Cahill released her second book, "Entitled" on August 12, 2017, titled after her attitude as a teenager. The book, diving into her royal days and her rise in the acting industry became a quick bestseller, selling 7.7 million copies in its first year of release and received praise for its self reflective nature. Cahill's addition to
Metropolitan Affairs
helped revive the series, recording a large jump in viewership following her join to the cast. At the 2018
Met Gala
, Cahill met famous Panama born soccer player
Mickey Jordan
, and the pair began dating shortly after. The couple welcomed their first child, Isaac Cahill-Jordan on June 17, 2019. Cahill won the coveted
Best Actress
award twice in a row for her work on season 9 and 10 of
Metropolitan Affairs
at the
2019
and
2020 Primetime Emmy Awards
.
2020–2025: Marriage, humanitarian work, failed transition to film and arrest
[
]
In summer 2020, Cahill married her boyfriend of two years, Mickey Jordan, reportedly giving him a 20-carat diamond cocktail ring worth near to $10 million. Their wedding ceremony was held in Peru, and Cahill legally became
Lola Cahill-Jordan
. Following their marriage, Cahill's net worth reportedly reached over $640 million, making her one of the world's richest actresses at just 34-years old. Following their marriage, Cahill stepped back from public activities temporarily, besides promotion and filming for the eleventh season of
Metropolitan Affairs
. In 2021, it was revealed by the Firefighters Against Arsonists charity organisation that an unnamed actress had donated $14.36 million that year, which they later revealed to be Cahill.
The final season of
Metropolis Affairs
concluded on NBC in May 2022. Earlier that month, Cahill had joined the cast of the new NBC sitcom,
Brightfield
, reportedly earning $1 million per episode. Brightfield was an immediate smash success with both audiences and critics, ranking amongst the Top 10 shows in its debut season while Cahill won the award for Best Supporting Actress at the at the
2023 Primetime Emmy Awards
. Cahill remained on the show for its second season, receiving another award nod, however decided to leave ahead of the third season in 2024 in hopes of transitioning into film roles over television. She was offered a role in
Riding The Lion
(2025), however declined due to low pay. Cahill gave birth to her fourth child Esme in 2024.
In late 2024, Cahill had been casted in the horror film
A Fake Blade
and began filming, and also continued to appear in commercials and magazines. Around this time Cahill had an increased fondness for travelling and visited Japan with husband Jordan, where she gave him a
red baneberry
which she claimed she didn't know was poisonous, resulting in his death and her subsequent arrest and conviction for manslaughter, sentenced to six years in medium security prison. She was dropped from the cast of
A Fake Blade
quickly.
Crime career and life
[
]
2025–2049: Rebuilding career, criminal activity and prison stints
[
]
Majority of crimes come from the confessions of Cahill prior to her death, from her book "
Confessions of a Teenage Princess
".
Whilst in prison in 2027, Cahill's mother Queen Lily passed after suffering a stroke, with Cahill unable to attend her funeral due to her incarceration despite their closeness. After her release in 2030, Cahill joined the
list of female billionaires
after her father King Lee passed away and she inherited over $460 million. Selena Zuckerburg, Cahill's agent of 25 years had refused contact with her once she was released from prison, resulting in Cahill firing her. She also found that she had been blackballed from acting, and her sole public activity that year was a magazine shoot, however the following year she was able to book public appearances and released her second book, "Times of Cahill" which continued her successful line of memoirs with over 5 million copies sold. After accepting the end of her acting career, Cahill opted to start her own business with "Cahill Coffee" in 2031, however the business never turned a profit in its 9 years of operation, with Cahill even making more money through editorials and commercials in 2031. Cahill released a third memoir in 2034 titled "Me", another commercial success selling near to 3 million copies. Cahill sold
Cahill Coffee
in 2040.
Cahill's descent to madness began in 2039 after she murdered three of her own children and got away with it. The following year she hired a hitman on former friends who her relationship had soured with following her incarceration and murdered three more of her former friends with the same motive. Following a failed attempt to murder another former friend and a successful murder of another, Cahill was caught and arrested and reportedly attempted to bribe police officers with $1 million. She was charged and convicted with six counts of murder and bribery and sentenced to life, where she began serving in maximum security in 2041. Cahill reportedly attempted to escape multiple times over multiple years and was caught, and also orchestrated a
prison riot
. Cahill's crimes and prison stories quickly became one of the most talked about events in history due to her prior level of fame and recognisability.
Cahill successfully escaped from prison in 2044 and not long after did a photoshoot with a photographer with no intent of publicly releasing the pictures. Three years later, the photographer released the pictures to media outlets and notified authorities of her whereabouts, resulting in her capture and return to prison. After extending her life sentence by 14 years in two years from two failed escapes, in 2049 she managed to escape once again and moved to
Puerto Vallarta, Mexico
. Also in 2049, Cahill established a cult titled "Way of Lola". Whilst attempting to recruit in public, she was convicted for disturbing public peace where she served in Puerto Vallarta Correctional Institution for one year.
2050–2076: Organised crime, cult and death
[
]
In 2050, Cahill had joined the
Gigante
organised crime family as an associate after growing relationships with the family members. Early in her joining, she was offered an opportunity to become an informant against the family but declined and was thus arrested for racketeering and murder during her time in the family, as well as running from the police after she escaped from prison in the United States, being sentenced to life. Using her cult to her advantage, Cahill urged her followers to raise donations to her lawyers to appeal her sentence, which they did multiple times raising millions of dollars and she subsequently won the appeal in 2051 being released, also thanks to her connections with the powerful Gigante family. She was arrested once again in 2052 for extortion and racketeering however her ties to the family helped her get out of the arrest. Cahill would be arrested many more times following whilst doing duties for the family but would be let go after arrests again due to her connections. In 2057, Cahill was sentenced to life in prison for murdering her boyfriend Alfonso Acosta, however she managed to appeal her sentence using her connections to the mob and her cult followers.
Cahill continued to lead her cult with members growing significantly in 2054 onwards. The Gigante family was investigated in 2056 due to a confidential informant providing information to authorities, however they managed to get away with their crimes after paying a large sum. The cult expanded internationally in 2060 with outreach missions in different countries, marking the first time Cahill was in the headlines again since she had escaped to Mexico. Cahill had become important to the Gigante family and thus was extremely protected against law enforcement despite the global awareness of Cahill's crimes and whereabouts. The cult had secured its place as a point of global fascination by 2062 when it was reported that Cahill brought in over $2 million per year through membership fees alone. She officially disbanded the cult in 2071, after making an estimated $40 million.
In 2075, Cahill found out she was likely to pass soon due to her old age. She decided to write a book titled
Confessions of a Teenage Princess
detailing her entire life from her fame, royal days and criminal activities including at the time unknown details. The book released on September 13, 2075 and marks the best selling memoir of all time and one of the best selling books in history with over 60 million copies sold.
Lola Cahill passed away from a stroke on November 12, 2076. Her funeral was broadcasted on numerous networks worldwide and was one of the most talked about events of the 2070s, whilst her life story has become one of the most legendary in history.
