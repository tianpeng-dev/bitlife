Elise Nilsson
(b. January 17, 1904 — d. ???) is the former Swedian baroness, born to
Lars Nilsson
and Molly Nilsson, the legal heir to a fifth of her father's estate and the secondary successor of the Nilsson branch. At birth she was identified as the Baroness of Lerum.
The Former Baroness of Lerum, inheritrix of the Counts of Rosenqvist.
Childhood
[
]
Infant Elise
Elise was born in Gothenburg, Sweden, in 1904, identified as the Baroness of Lerum. In 1911 she was enrolled in Jensen Primary School with her big sister, Ella. Unlike her sister, Elise preferred to earn high grades through her charisma rather than her diligence. In 1920, she started Hvidfeldtska Secondary School after her sister.
Adult Life
[
]
Having graduated from secondary school at the age of 18, she enrolled at the university in the direction of religious studies. Four years later she moved out in Lerum graduated from univesity with an undergraduate degree in religious studies. In 1936, she give birth to a baby girl named Elin Nilsson. In 1939, she left Sweden with her daughter Elin and immigrated to Helsinki, Finland, where she began a new life. She got a job at the hairdresser in Aristokraattinen yksilö salon, located next to her home. She wanted her children and future generations to have a calm and stable life, filled with pre-prepared knowledge, so Elise began to teach her daughter various sciences early.
Government
[
]
In 1927, Elise took office as the Baroness of Lerum. She did not express her main goals in the board, and in her personal diary she often mentioned that she did not want to rule anything at all.
1928:
Elise de facto transfers control of the city to her assistant Karl Lirveau.
1936:
Management of the city is transferred to Ella Nilsson.
1939:
Officially ends his baronial activities, renouncing the title.
