Buster Brian (1904 - 1950) was an American soldier who served in World War II and briefly in the Korean War. He was a platoon member of fellow soldier John Buckles. He was killed in 1950 as a bystander in a bank robbery.

Early life[]

Buster was born in California.

Army career[]

Brian enlisted in the army in 1922 at age 18.

World War II[]

Dates of rank[]

  • Private (1922-1923)
  • Private First Class (1923-1924)
  • Specialist/Lance Corporal (1924-1925)
  • Corporal (1925-1926)
  • Sergeant (1926-1931)
  • Sergeant First Class (1931-1936)
  • Master Sergeant/First Sergeant (1939-1943)
  • Sergeant Major (1943-1950)

Personal life and death[]

Brian had a wife, whom he married long before the war. He also had at least one child, who was in their teens at the time of Brian's death.

In September of 1950, Brian was placed on leave to spend time with his family. On September 11, he was on his way to attend Buckles' 27th birthday party, when he witnessed a bank robbery. He tried to escape, but one of the robbers shot him. Brian died almost instantly.
