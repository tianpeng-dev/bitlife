Buster Brian
(1904 - 1950) was an American soldier who served in World War II and briefly in the Korean War. He was a platoon member of fellow soldier
John Buckles
. He was killed in 1950 as a bystander in a bank robbery.

Personal life and death
[
]
Brian had a wife, whom he married long before the war. He also had at least one child, who was in their teens at the time of Brian's death.
In September of 1950, Brian was placed on leave to spend time with his family. On September 11, he was on his way to attend Buckles' 27th birthday party, when he witnessed a bank robbery. He tried to escape, but one of the robbers shot him. Brian died almost instantly.
