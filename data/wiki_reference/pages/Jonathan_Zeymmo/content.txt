Jonathan "Jon Boy"  Zeymmo
(December 19th, 1941 - March 7, 2004), later known as
Heath John Davids
, was an American ex-soldier turned mafia Godfather who was an active made man, and eventually leader, of the Floridian division of the Italian-American mafia in Miami for 23 years until 1998 when a rat snitched on him and the entire crime syndicate, forcing him to illegally immigrate to
Papua New Guinea
and go into hiding until 2003, when authorities discovered his location and extradited him to the
United States
where he underwent trial and was imprisoned for 5 life sentences and 941 years, but only served an quarter of his first year due to him having committed suicide to escape his punishment.
Decades after his death, Zeymmo's illegitimate son Smithsonian would adopt his father's surname and reform the Italian-American mafia.

Early Life
[
]
Jonathan was born to Vito and Francesca Zeymmo, two Italian-Americans, in Newport, Oregon on December 19th, 1941. Francesca was an chef and Vito was an bartender. Jon strived to be a good student and to live up to his rather abusive father's expectations, but continuously failed. In 1951, when Jon was 9, Vito, in a drunken state, killed Jon's dog, Godfather, resulting in Francesca being fed up with Vito's antics and divorcing him. Traumatized by the experience, Jon became more reckless and violent.
Independent Years
[
]
Zeymmo joined the army after graduating from high school, sending his mother checks. After narrowly clearing a battlefield packed with landmines, Zeymmo was given a medal and a promotion. Later that year, Zeymmo requested a discharge due to being uncomfortable near a bisexual fellow soldier, and moved him and his mother to Miami, looking for a job. After being rejected by every business in the area, Zeymmo began committing crimes to keep him and his ailing mother on their feet. Eventually, Zeymmo caught the attention of the local mafia. After spending a few months doing favors for them, Zeymmo was asked to join, to which he accepted.
Rising Through The Ranks
[
]
Zeymmo started out as an made man, however he slowly but surely climbed his way up the hierarchy of the crime syndicate. Throughout 1975-1980 during his first years in the family, he burglarized homes and contributed the shares to the mafia, making a living for himself, and briefly his mother, who peacefully passed away from natural causes in 1985. Zeymmo was promoted to Caporegime in 1990. He committed hits for local drug cartels associated with the family, stole vehicles and other goods for the syndicate, coerced enemies of the family into submission, and murdered police officers and government officials close to discovering the family's activities.
Death Of The Family
[
]
In 1994, Zeymmo was promoted to Godfather of the family after working for them for 19 years. Suspicions of a Caporegime being a DEA informant and Zeymmo's right-hand-man
Toni
being murdered by a Yakuza member named "Murakamai" ravaged the family until 1998 when the suspected member revealed himself, arresting most of the members except Zeymmo himself and a few others. Zeymmo fled to
Papua New Guinea
the following year, where he took on the alias Heath John Davids and began working as an security guard for a local museum. This ran until 2003 when authorities conducting a sting operation around an sex trade ring recognized Zeymmo and arrested him. Zeymmo was deported back to the United States where he stood trial, being forced to apply for a public defender since the American government seized all of his remaining assets. Zeymmo lost the case and was sentenced to 5 consecutive life sentences and 941 years at St. Baxter Maximum Security Containment Facility in Colorado. Realizing he had run his laps, Zeymmo accepted defeat and hung himself in his prison cell on March 7, 2004.
Legacy
[
]
In 2007, it was discovered Zeymmo had an illegitimate daughter and son, both of whom were born in 1985. As of 2010, she was a teacher living in New York, and he was an mobster and drug dealer.
Zeymmo's father, who had been divorced and gone into the shadows in 1951, attended Zeymmo's funeral at the age of 86, having finally felt remorse for the actions he had committed against his son and wife.
Due to his fame and notoriety as the final leader of one of the most powerful criminal syndicates in the world, his fellow prisoners and some corrupt guards held a secret memorial for him before they reported the body.
"Heath John Davids" vanishing was, despite the public being aware of the truth, filed off as natural causes.
