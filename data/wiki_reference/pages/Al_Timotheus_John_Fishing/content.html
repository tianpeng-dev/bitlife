John Fishing (March 16, 1922 - April 9, 2022) was an American pilot and microbiologist. Fishing's aviation career spans over five decades, flying twenty-five years for Accent Airlines, twenty years with Licker Air and two years with Walsh Airlines, plus six years of service in the Air Force. After his full retirement from flying, he worked as a microbiologist for Gray Engineering.

Early life, education and USAF[]

Fishing was born and raised in Bangor, Maine. His father, Lil (1860 - 1929), was a Navy enlistee who had served as a leader during the World War I and had retired the year John was born. His mother, Viviane (1888 - 1968) was a firefighter for the Bangor Fire Department. John had three siblings, sister Dixie (1919 - 2010), brother Kendrick (1925 - 1932) and sister Jamila (1927 - 2010). Kendrick died at seven years old from rectum cancer. Dixie was a sales associate for Evelyn Jewlers and Jamila was an operations analyst for Rainer Development.

Fishing recieved his pilot's license after completing high school at age 18. He studied biology at university.

After completing university, Fishing applied to the US Army Air Forces (later US Air Force) in 1944. After being promoted to Captain in 1949, he practiced that position for his commercial debut for one year before leaving the Air Force in 1950.

Commercial flying[]

Fishing applied to Accent Airlines in 1950 the day after his big welcome home party from the Air Force, which had been arranged by his family and friends. After impressing his instructors with his steady flying skills he learned in the Air Force, he was hired as a pilot trainee.

Scientific career[]

Fishing worked as a microbiologist at Gray Engineering.

Death[]

Fishing passed away peacefully in his sleep on April 9, 2022 at age 100. He is survived by his three children and grandchildren. John is remembered as a conscientious man. Friends smile when remembering how he once called a granddaughter a closer.
