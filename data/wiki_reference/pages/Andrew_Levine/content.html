Andrew Mark Levine (born January 26th, 1988 - October 15th, 2082) was a lawyer and politician who was elected as President of the United States from 2056 till 2064. He is well known for his time spent focusing on the economy, and had incredibly high approval ratings at over 95%. A member of the Democratic Party, he previously ran a law firm until running for President.

Early life[]

Andrew was born in Nashville, Tennessee, to his parents Sierra and Martin Levine. Both of his parents were real estate agents. Andrew had an interest in sports, and was a member of his school's soccer team in middle and high school. While in high school, he discovered his passion for law and politics and joined his school's speech and debate team, where he was the Vice President his senior year.

After graduating from high school, Andrew was offered an academic scholarship to Vanderbilt, which he gladly accepted. He continued his sports career as a soccer player, and joined Alpha Delta Phi, a well known fraternity. Upon graduating from Vanderbilt, Andrew was offered a three-year contract with Manchester, but turned it down to attend law school, where he graduated top of his class. When asked why he didn't play in the Premier League, Andrew said he didn't have much potential anyways and would've probably ended up as a journeyman.

Law career[]

Andrew applied for a job with Marcello & Associates, where he was hired on the spot by a fraternity brother. He started as a junior associate, and worked his way up to Partner in less than a decade. However, Andrew was interested in starting his own law firm, and resigned from his position immediately.

Andrew launched his business by purchasing a health food store and renovating it into a law firm. He named it, Levine & Associates, and invested almost all of his earnings into his business. Over a few fiscal years, the business took few clients and was on the verge of bankruptcy, but by year ten the company had skyrocketed into hundreds of millions and had thousands of clients. Levine & Associates would continue to operate until Andrew sold the company in interests of pursuing the presidential office. When Andrew sold the company, it was worth a stunning twenty billion dollars and had over seventy locations throughout the country, in addition to hundreds of employees.

Political career[]

Activism[]

Throughout Andrew's life on social media, Andrew frequently posted political thoughts and shared politicians on his account, leading to much criticism. He was a moderate Democrat and had millions of followers, but was often criticized for involving politics in business. Furthermore, he worked two years as an activist, but quit the job when he formed his company. Levine wanted to actually act on his beliefs and decided to run for President of the United States after his wife was the Mayor of New York City for four years and the Governor of New York for another four years.

Presidency[]

Levine invested upwards of a billion dollars towards his presidential campaign, the majority coming from either his former law firm or from donors. He won the primaries and faced Alejandro Garcia, a well-known Republican politician who had spent his life in the government. Alejandro criticized Levine for his mistakes in the past and even had a blimp interrupt Levine's rallies. The elections came down to an incredibly close vote, with Levine winning with 52% of the country's popular vote. He was sworn in as President and had a crackdown on laws focused on crime. He was known for spending lavishly on energetic rallies. He re-won the election in 2060. Upon exiting office, he had an approval rating of 75%.

Personal life[]

Andrew met the love of his life, Hannah on a date at a coffee shop. Hannah was in the military as an army officer, and the two married at a banquet hall. They had three children together: Daniel, named after Hannah's father, who went on to follow in his parent's footsteps by serving in the military and then as a politician who was elected President, Jackson, who ran a major hedge fund and is an investor, and Sierra, who went on to become a runway model and actress. The Levine family is considered one of the most powerful and influential families in today's modern world.

Andrew passed away at the age of 94, and was mourned accross the nation. He was survived by his wife of over sixty years and his three children, as well as several grandchildren. Free copies of "Superstar", Andrew's autobiography, was handed out at his memorial. Friends and family remember him as a funny guy.
