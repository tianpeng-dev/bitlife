Kashm Alakozai
(August 11, 1942 - September 20, 2022) was an Afghan psychiatrist.

Early life and education
[
]
Kashm was born in Kabul, Afghanistan to Asal Alakozai (1911 - 1948) and an unknown father. Asal was a naval sailor during World War II who disguised herself as a man to gain entry, since female military personnel were still frowned upon at the time. She had an older half-sister named Mahwash Moon (1932 - 1959), a saleswoman for Polar Jeweler. Asal was killed in a car bomb in 1948 when Kashm was six years old, forcing her to live in Mohammadzai Orphans Home for the remainder of her childhood. Mahwash would be a loving mother figure to Kashm, even serving as her mentor until she was killed by a landmine in 1959.

Personal life
[
]
At ten years old, Alakozai met her first love, Musa Khan (1942 - 2017), who would later become a hairdresser for Central Salon & Spa. When they both reached the age of eighteen, they got married. They had three children together:
Nawaskhan (1962 - 2022) - Trucker for Unity Trucking Company
Jabroot (1967 - 1983) - Died in a shark accident
Isfandiyar (1970 - 1995) - Paralegal at Anwarzai & Associates; died in a suicide bombing
Additionally, Alakozai was the legal guardian of a nephew, Anousheh Niazai (born 2006), the child of her cousin who passed away from a pulmonary embolism. Before Khan's death in 2017, Alakozai came out as a lesbian, and he still accepted her, supporting her during his dying days. At the time of her death, she was engaged to Damsa Farooq.
Death
[
]
Alakozai died on September 20, 2022. Predeceased by her husband and three biological children, she was survived by her adoptive daughter and her grandchildren.
