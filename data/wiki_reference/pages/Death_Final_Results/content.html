After the player character dies, they will get a grave and a ribbon. The former is a record of their performance, though players may choose to bulldoze graves if they don't want to keep them. The latter is a symbol of what they did during their life and what the final rank is.

The player's gravestone is actually from the point of an obituary because it recounts the player's actions and what they did in their life. If the player went to jail, the gravestone will recount the warden's point of view because sometimes it says that they spent "most of their time here in jail".

After listing the player's final stats (net worth, children, grandchildren, murders, etc.), the gravestone will say how and when the character died, as well as the people who have attended their funeral. A character's funeral is attended by (if appliable) their parent(s), spouses, siblings, children, grandchildren, nephews and nieces, but only if they have good relationships by the time of the character's death, otherwise they'll not attend their funeral. If a character is in Royalty at the time of their death, there will also be a number of 'loyal followers', which depends on the character's respect stats, ranging from 'a handful' to 'tens of thousands' of loyal followers.

At the bottom of the grave is a message from the player's anonymous "friends" who recount an event during the player's life, the event can be very minor; such as whether or not the player had their child circumsised or not, to major events such as the player saving a person's life.
