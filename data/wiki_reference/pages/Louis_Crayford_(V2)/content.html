Sir Louis Crayford OBE (January 12, 1935 - March 10, 2022), also known by the pen name L. L. Crayford, was a British author and monk.

Early life and education[]

Louis was born in Staines to paralegal Finnigan "Finn" Crayford (1892 - 1970) and restaurateur Regina Crayford (1895 - 1977). A World War I veteran, Finn often worked with military clients. The family had a pet cat named Nala (c. 1934 - 1946).

Personal life[]

Despite five romances, Crayford never managed to father any of his own children, at least according to official DNA scanning. His first relationship was with Elizabeth Hawkins, a fling he had met for a one-night stand in 1961. After two years of dating, Crayford left Hawkins in 1963 for his co-worker Anastasia "Anna" Cumberbatch, a fellow writer he had met the previous year. Elizabeth grew jealous and resorted to stalking Louis. He caught Hawkins peeping on him in 1965 and filed a restraining order against her.

Crayford and Cumberbatch got married in 1967. Before, during, and long after their wedding, they made love many times but failed to produce any children. They remained married for over fifty years until Anna's death in 2019 from heart disease. Shortly afterwards, Crayford began dating fellow writer named Gracie Buckingham, who he had met on a dating app, and they got married a year before Louis' death.

Death[]

Crayford died on March 10, 2022. He is survived by his second wife. During his funeral and subsequent tribute services, many younger fans of Crayford's works came forward, claiming to be his grandchildren, whose parents themselves claimed to be his children. The accuracy of said claims and speculation remains uncertain.
