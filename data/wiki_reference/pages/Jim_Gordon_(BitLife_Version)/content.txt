James Walker Gordon
(March 20, 1981 - September 17, 2022) was a police officer for the Gotham City Police Department. During his eleven years on the force, he was commonly associated with the vigilante figure
Batman
(Bruce Wayne).

Early life
[
]
Jim was born in Gotham as the newest member of the prominent Gordon family. His mother, Jennifer (born 1943), is a retired jeweler, and his father, D'Brickshaw (1945 - 1993), was a travel agent and Vietnam draft dodger. He had an older sister named Alyssa (1976 - 1987), who died at the age of 11 from scarlet fever. The family had an Akita dog named Gilbert (d. 1988). D'Brickshaw died in 1993 from spleen cancer when Jim was twelve years old. Jennifer would remarry a few years later to John Duffy (1925 - 1997), a retired flight attendant and World War II combat pilot, though the marriage only lasted a year until John's death in 1997. From Jennifer's marriage to Duffy, Jim would gain two step-siblings, Perry (born 1945) and Kiara.
Marines
[
]
Upon graduating high school, Gordon joined the United States Marine Corps. He made acquaintances with his fellow Marines, including Cooper Reed, Gabriel Moorehead, Jake Huang, Joey Mountjoy, Sydney Phan, Whitney Robinson, and William "Will" Bone.

Personal life
[
]
Gordon began dating his childhood friend Martha Grace (born 1981) when they were both ten years old. After maintaining their loving relationship for eleven years, Martha proposed to Jim in 2002 at a circus. Just as Gordon was accepting her proposal, the circus encountered a raid by a mobster, which caused the death of all but one member of the circus family Flying Graysons, with Richard "Dick" Grayson being the sole survivor. A while after the incident, Jim and Martha got married, and Martha later became an internal auditor for Lifeblood Labs. They had six children together:
Barbara (born 2004)
Jim Jr. (born 2006)
Dirk (born 2012)
Walter (born 2014)
Elmer (born 2016)
Woody (born 2020)
Death
[
]
On September 17, 2022, Gordon suffered a massive heart attack while on shift. He was rushed to the hospital, where he later died. Gordon is survived by his wife and six children.
