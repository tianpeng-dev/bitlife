Romouald Mifsud
(June 20, 1922 - September 14, 2022) was an Egyptian military officer and politician who served as Mayor of Alexandria from 1996 to 2008. Prior to his mayoral career, he served as Alexandria's school board director from 1984 to 1996. Mifsud was also well-known for his attempts to become the President of Egypt. Before his career as a politician, he served in the Egyptian Army for over four decades, rising from the rank of private all the way up to lieutenant colonel, serving in various conflicts such as World War II.

Early life
[
]
Romouald was born in Alexandria to Sadiki Mifsud (1857 - 1930), a business analyst who retired before his son's birth, and Noha Ashour (1892 - 1969), a truck driver. He had an older half-brother, Bebti Ashour (1921 - 1930), who died at the age of nine from scarlet fever. He had two other half-siblings after he was born, Hossam (1925 - 1989), a graphic designer for Gaber Media, and Jehan (1932 - 2004), an accountant for Meridian Consulting. Sadiki passed away from old age when Romouald was eight years old, resulting in an inheritence of 28,845,496.05 Egyptian pounds ($586,887 in 1930; $11,041,031.33 in 2024 money).
Mifsud was an outgoing child. He showed a keen interest in exercise and learning about topics that were advanced for his age. At St. Osahar's Secondary School, he was a member of the fencing club, becoming its vice-president in 1938. He also joined the speech and debate club as a way to practice for serious debates that he would have as a politician. Mifsud graduated from St. Osahar's at the age of 17 in the summer of 1939, several months before the invasion of Poland by Nazi Germany, the Soviet Union, and the Slovak Republic.

Personal life
[
]
Mifsud was married twice and was a father to five children. He met his future first wife, Bassant Amari (1922 - 1981), when they were both five years old. They began dating in 1940. In 1945, Amari proposed to him in a movie theater, and he accepted. They got married two years later and had three children, Kasto (1947 - 2015), Gahji (1949 - 2014), and Norhan (born 1952). Amari died in 1981 after being bit on the spine by a venomous snake. Kasto and Gahji both studied art history at university, but they ended up going down different career paths, with Kasto working as a grocer for Cocksure Grocery and Gahji being a travel agent for Valley Travel Agency. Norhan, partially inspired by her father's political career, worked as a school administrator.
A year after completing his second term, Mifsud met his second wife, grocer Mi-soon Kassab (born approx. early-mid 1970s) in 2009. They married a year later, the same year their daughter, Sohalia, was born. Mifsud became the stepfather of Mi-soon's daughter, Noha Salib (born 1995), from a previous relationship. Noha is a real estate agent for Osman Bata Realty with a degree in architecture.
Death
[
]
Mifsud died on September 14, 2022 at the age of 100. He is survived by his second wife, surviving children and grandchildren.
