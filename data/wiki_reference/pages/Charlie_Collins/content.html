Charles George Collins (June 12, 1922 - February 12, 2022) was an American accountant. He spent fifty years working for Cherry Global, one of the most prominent businesses in the Boston area.

Early life and education[]

Charlie was born in Boston to writer Bobbie Sue Gobble (1891 - 1980) and pilot George Collins (1879 - 1955). He had a stepfather, a lawyer named Marshall Holyfield (1887 - 1958). From the marriage of his mother and stepfather, Charlie had two half-siblings, Junior (1924 - 1999), a firefighter with military experience from World War II, and Ava (born 1931), a receptionist.

Collins was a star football player in high school and had the potential to go pro. However, living a life of fame did not align with the kind of person he wanted to be. Ultimately, he left football after high school. He attended Boston University, Class of 1944, with a bachelor's in accounting. Although Collins' college career made him exempt from the World War II draft, he was enrolled in various military programs as a precaution for the possibility of service after graduating.

Career[]

In 1944, after graduating from BU, Collins was hired by Cherry Global as an accountant. In just three years, he was promoted to Sr. Accountant in 1947 at the age of 25.

Collins retired from Cherry Global in 1994, shortly after celebrating his 50th anniversary at the company.

Personal life[]

Collins met his first wife, Tara Allen (c. 1925 - 1973), in 1947. After dating for nine years, Tara proposed to him in 1956, to which he accepted, and they wed shortly after. They had five children and seven grandchildren together:

  • Aisha (born 1948) - Retired Marine; Vietnam veteran
  • Daniel (born 1952) - Retired trucker for Redwood Trucking
  • Buck (born 1953) - Retired hairdresser for Paddock Salon & Organics
  • Ethan (born 1961) - Restaurateur at Kiana's Diner
  • Dick (born 1962) - Agent for Boston Travel

Tara died in a car accident in 1973. That same year, Collins started dating his second wife, childhood friend Aliyah Montgomery (1922 - 2013). They married in 1984 and remained so until her death in 2013, around the fortieth anniversary of Tara's death.

Collins died on February 12, 2022, exactly four months short of his 100th birthday. He is survived by his five children, six grandchildren, and his half-sister.
