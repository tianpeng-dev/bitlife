Henry Sangeloa (1923 - 2010) was an American graphic designer and the father of Air Force pilot, actress and model Ivy Sangeloa.

Early life and education[]

Henry was born in 1923 in Texas. His father was a veteran of World War I, and his mother was a multi-tasking jack-of-all trades, working as a waitress, seamstress, secretary, and many other odd-jobs that helped to keep the family financially stable. He had a good childhood but struggled socially in his teen hood and early adulthood. Although he had many great friends growing up, he found it difficult to make romantic connections with his peers. Because of this, he preferred younger girls, but not too much younger, because he never saw himself as a pervert or pedophile.

From a young age, Henry was fascinated with art and wanted to make a living out of it. He would involve himself in many creative extracurricular activities throughout school and would either win or at least be nominated for various academic artistic competitions, building a unique resume that he could use to secure a successful career. After graduating high school, he started to attend university, majoring in graphic design, and expected to make his full four years without issue. However, with America's entry into the war in 1941, Henry's college career would get rough, as he would end up being called to serve. Luckily, Henry's father, who allegedly developed premonitions of his son's predicament from own experiences, planned ahead for a solution by signing him up for an ROTC program that would assist his academic tenures while he served.

Military career[]

When hearing about the Japanese attacking Pearl Harbor on December 7, 1941, Sangeloa immediately began preparing himself, physically and mentally, so that less aspects of joining the military would come as a negative surprise. Shortly after America's official entry into the war, he enlisted in the Army.

Graphic design[]

After coming home from the war in 1945, Henry finally managed to complete college and earn his degree. Shortly afterwards, his career in graphic design took off.

Personal life[]

Sangeloa met his future wife, Mikayla (born 1951), in 1968 when she was 17, and he was 45; the relationship was consensual, and Mikayla and said that they preferred this age gap because they struggled to socialize romantically with their peers, and her friends and family supported her. In 1969, a year after they first met and started dating, they got married, and Mikayla gave birth to their first child, Gaylord, within the same year. Another year later, they would have their second child, Ivy. Their next two children would be born much later, with Ivy's younger brother Winston being born in 1982 when she was 12 (Mikayla was 31), and her youngest brother Latrell being born in 1990 when she was 20 (Mikayla was 39).

Death[]

Henry was diagnosed with dementia in 1999 and was given at least a decade left to live. During that time, Ivy occasionally took him to the doctor to seek treatment. Despite the family's best efforts, there was little to nothing that could be done. Henry died peacefully in his sleep in 2010 at the age of 87. Because of his service in World War II, he received military honors at his funeral. Additionally, attendees of his funeral included his fellow partners in the graphic design industries, ranging from his students, the much younger artists of the next generation, to any of his surviving co-workers from his early days in the field.
