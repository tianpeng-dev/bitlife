Bradley Wilson (November 9, 1844-1915) was an American-Spanish soccer player, receptionist, and founder of the Wilson Legacy.

Early life[]

Wilson was born in Houston, Texas. His parents were a policeman named Jeffrey Wilson (1803-1862) and a mail carrier named Elektra Wilson (1800-1881). He had an older brother named Owen Wilson (1838-after 1893). He was on the General Middle School basketball team from 1856 to 1859. He went to Houston High School and was a member of its soccer team from 1859 to 1863.

Soccer career[]

In 1863, Wilson emigrated to Seville, Spain. He joined Betis as a forward.

In 1866, Wilson was traded to Español de Fútbol. He won four Golden Boots while on the team.

In 1876, Wilson was traded to Barcelona. He won six championships, a Golden Boot, and a Ballon d’Or while on the team.

In 1885, Wilson was released by Barcelona and joined La Sociedad. He was released in 1886.

Front desk career[]

In 1894, Wilson became a receptionist at Indica Technologies. He resigned in 1910.

Death[]

In 1915, Wilson died of natural causes. His funeral was held in Seville and his body was donated to science.

Relationship and children[]

In 1859, Wilson started dating a student named Genesis Felt (1845-1937), who later became a flight attendant. They had two children, Daniella (1862-1940) and Paul Wilson.

Pets[]

Wilson had nine pets:

  1. Pebbles (1842-1851), a Basset Hound owned by Jeffrey and Elektra
  2. Dixie (1851-1861), a mutt adopted in 1856
  3. Prada (1859-1875), a cat purchased in 1861
  4. Xena (1851-1866), a leopard purchased in 1863
  5. Julia (1861-1910), a gorilla purchased in 1866 and given to Paul in 1893
  6. Dash (1862-1883), a chimpanzee purchased in 1867
  7. Joan (1881-1895), a Siberian tiger purchased in 1886 and given to Paul in 1893
  8. Lolo (1881-1891), a squirrel monkey purchased in 1888
  9. Arnau (1874-1900), a Bengal tiger purchased and given to Paul in 1893
