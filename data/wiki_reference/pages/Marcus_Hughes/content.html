Marcus Stanley Hughes (April 11, 1893 - April 11th, 1939) was a convicted French-American serial killer and death row inmate known for his manipulative personality and his alleged high emotional intelligence and high IQ. Officially Hughes killed 5 people from 1908 - 1937. Additionally, he is the father of famous actor David Hughes.

Early Life[]

Childhood[]

Marcus Hughes was born in Houston, Texas. Hughes had no siblings. He was born to Oprah Judge, née Carter, a makeup artist, and archaeologist Stanley Hughes. The two were unmarried and hadn't meant to concieve Hughes. Marcus Hughes grew up primarily with his mother and stepfather, Jotham Judge. Reportedly, while Hughes attended Aardvark Elementary and St. Beavis's Middle School, he was severely bullied by his classmates. When Hughes was 12 his cat Zuzu died to which, according to his stepfather Jotham Judge, he had no grief for. Not much else is known about Hughes' early childhood.

First Murder[]

In 1908, when Hughes was 15 and attending Brad Feingold High School, a classmate named Elmore Smith had acted out in a class. Mr. Gallo, the teacher, stated that Hughes instantly stood up and repeatedly kicked the student in his groin and punched his head before anybody could step in. Shortly after, the student Smith passed away from his injuries. Marcus Hughes was apprehended by the police and convicted of manslaughter. He was sent to Houston Juvenile Detention Facility for 3 years. Hughes was supposedly a model inmate and the police claimed that he was "...an intelligent well-behaved kid." Hughes was released from Houston Juvenile Detention Facility in 1911 at the age of 18. Shortly after, he got a job as a newspaper delivery person for Houston Chronicle. He would stay at this job for a year, quitting when his father died from choking on a hot dog.

Marcus's Father's Death[]

In 1912, Hughes arrived at his father's house. His father Stanley Hughes was a retired archaeologist who had 58k dollars to his name, which adjusted to inflation is approximately 2 million dollars as of 2026. While the two were eating dinner, S. Hughes began to choke on a hotdog. According to Marcus Hughes' diary, Marcus Hughes calmly sat there and smiled at his dying father. Detectives found out after Hughes' execution so Hughes had not been charged for his father's death. Hughes inherited 30k dollars (1 million dollars) and he went on to quit his job at Houston Chronicles and buy a his house at 189 Aries St. 189 Aries St. has been referenced in popular culture and many believe it to be haunted.

Enlistment In WW1[]

In 1916, when Hughes was 23, he volunteered for the US army. Hughes had learnt French nearly fluently and was excited to arrive in France and considered it as a free trip. Not much is known about his service but peers stated that he acted like any other soldier. After WW1 ended in 1918, Hughes decided to stay in the army for a few more years. Hughes recieved an honourable discharge at his request and returned home in 1921 at the age of 28.

Killings[]

Houston[]

In early 1923, while on a walk back home from a general store, Hughes saw an apparent enemy (unidentified) of his in a dark alley. Hughes went into a fury and pushed her/him over before grabbing lighter fluid and quickly dousing her/him in it. Then, Hughes grabbed a lighter and set her/him on fire. Police were not sure of any of the details of the murder as the corpse was found as ash. They knew this occured due to a witness, Edward Renett. Hughes saw Renett right before he ran away and threatened Renett that he "...would kill him if he told anybody." Renett told the police in 1937 but it was too late as Hughes was already on death row.

Hughes claimed that after the murder: "I lost myself... I wanted to experience the rush of murder again, and again, and again..." Most pyschologists currently believe that Hughes was a high-functioning psycopath and potentially had Bipolar Disorder in addition.

An unidentified dismembered male body was found in Hughes' backyard in 1937. The police believe he was killed shortly after the previous murder; approximately 2-6 months into 1923, meaning this would be his 3rd charged victim. Not much is known about this victim but police believed the victim was strangled to death, Hughes' key killing style.

Hughes was nearly imprisoned in late 1923 for attempted murder, but the case was soon dropped.

In 1924, Hughes entered a romantic relationship with Alison O'Reilly, an 22 year-old Irish immigrant from Dublin who worked as a hairdresser at Swank Salon Houston. One night after she decided she didn't want to have any children with Hughes, Hughes randomly killed her by strangling her. After this murder, Hughes knew that the police would want to question him. Before they could, with his father's money, he sailed to Paris, France and got French citizenship, successfully immigrating there.

Paris[]

As Hughes could speak French fluently, he was doing well in Paris by his lonesome. He bought a house in Paris and decided to lay low for a bit. Back in Houston, the police declared that Hughes and O'Reilly were both missing.

From 1925-1928, it is unknown what Hughes got up to. Many experts hypothesize that he could have killed even more people during this time. No important diary entries or anything of the sort have been found from this time period and Hughes denied anything happened.

In 1929, at the age of 36, Hughes met Margot Francois, a well-off 20 year-old Parisian woman. Shortly after, they began dating. Two years later, in 1931, Margot Francois gave birth to David Hughes, Marcus Hughes' son. The same year, the two couple married eachother.

Apprehension and Death[]

Arrival To Houston[]

After another period of silence, Hughes decided to plan a temporary stay in America too see his sickly mother, thinking the police would not care about the case anymore, and potentially not even recognize him anymore. So, in 1935, 42 year-old David Hughes, 26 year-old Margot Francois and 4 year-old David Hughes arrived in Houston, Texas. As soon as they got off the cruise ship, Marcus Hughes was apprehended by the police for murder, facing the death penalty. Shockingly, after the extremely long court case, Hughes was found not guilty, of course, with the help of his wealth.

Death[]

In 1937, Hughes killed his last victim, a beggar of the name Frank Renett, by strangulation. The police discovered him fleeing from the scene of the crime with a rope in his hand and they arrested him. The beggar, Frank Renett, was Edward Renett's cousin, the same Edward Renett who witnessed Hughes kill his second victim. After hearing that Frank Renett was murdered, Edward Renett called the police. Although, by the time he called the police, Hughes had already been found guilty of murdering Frank Renett and they'd found the bodies in Hughes' backyard. Hughes was sentenced to be hanged and he was schedueled to be executed on his 46th birthday: The 11th of April, 1939. Throughout his stay in Houston Maximum Security, Hughes sent letters to his mother. wife and son apologizing for his actions and asking how they were.

Hughes was hanged on the 11th of April, 1939, no family attended his funeral.

People Involved[]

Elmore Smith[]

Elmore Smith (July 26, 1893 - 1908) was an American High School Student and murder victim born on the 26th of July, 1893 in Houston, Texas. He attended Brad Feingold High School and was known as a troublemaker but also as a kind and caring friend. In 1908, while in a class taught by Mr. Gallo, Elmore Smith was brutally and fatally attacked by Marcus Hughes, a serial killer. Elmore succumbed to his injuries in 1908, at the age of 15.

Stanley Hughes[]

Stanley Hughes (1833 - 1912) was a renowned American Archaeologist. Stanley Hughes was an extremely private figure so not much is known about his life and works except for his death via choking on a hotdog in 1912 at the age of 79 next to his son, Marcus Hughes, who sat and watch. Some people attribute his death to Marcus and some say Marcus is technically not liable.
