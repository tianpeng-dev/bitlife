Soan Francois III (3404-3506) was a French porn star, Air Force officer, politician, and lawyer who became the President of France.

Early life (3404-3429)[]

Francois was born in Lyon, France. He was the younger of two brothers born to Tiago Francois and the only one born to his second girlfriend, Camille Rose.

Francois went to Sciences Po Lyon and graduated with a Licence in Political Science in 3427 and a Diplôme National in 3429.

Porn career (3432-3439)[]

In 3432, Francois became a porn actor for Atmosphere Pictures.

In 3439, Francois became famous. He resigned.

Military career (3439-3463)[]

In 3439, Francois joined the French Air and Space Force as a sous-lieutenant.

In 3440, Francois was promoted to lieutenant.

In 3443, Francois was promoted to capitaine.

In 3449, Francois was promoted to commandant.

In 3455, Francois was promoted to lieutenant-colonel.

In 3463, Francois was promoted to colonel. He retired and enrolled at Sciences Po Lyon, from which he graduated with an LLM in 3466.

First political career (3466-3486)[]

In 3466, Francois ran for his arrondissement’s school board and beat Ahsan Hussein.

In 3471, Francois ran for reelection and beat Eliott Thomas.

In 3476, Francois ran for Mayor of Lyon as a member of La France Insoumise and beat Renaissance candidate Lyna Guillot.

In 3481, Francois ran for reelection and beat Renaissance candidate Ines Dubois.

In 3486, Francois ran for Governor of Auvergne-Rhône-Alpes, but lost to Renaissance candidate Nina Roche.

Legal career (3486-3491)[]

In 3486, Francois became a junior associate for Louis and Associates.

In 3490, Francois was promoted to associate.

In 3491, Francois resigned.

Second political career and death (3491-3506)[]

In 3491, Francois ran for Governor of Auvergne-Rhône-Alpes and beat Renaissance candidate Emma de Gaulle.

In 3496, Francois ran for reelection and beat Ecologist candidate Antoine Marie.

In 3501, Francois ran for President of France and beat Ecologist candidate Adam Lavigne.

In 3506, Francois did not run for reelection. He died of a stroke less than a year after leaving office.
