Dr. Benjamin Robert Phelps Bsc Honors, MD (July 6, 1923 - March 14, 2024) was an American family physician, Director of the Nashville Board of Education from 1993 to 2001, and Mayor of Nashville from 2001 to 2009. Phelps was known for his kind, loving, and supportive nature, and was well known for his wit. He worked as a family physician at the Nashville General Hospital for thirty-one years, before retiring. After spending ten years in retirement, he entered into a career in politics and was elected as the Forty-Ninth Director of the Nashville Board of Education, serving for two terms. Following this, he successfully campaigned in the 2002 Nashville Mayoral Election, and served as Mayor of Nashville for two terms, before then retiring again. His service as a school board director was seen as modest, and as Mayor of Nashville, he was seen as an effective mayor. Historians rank him highly in historical rankings of Nashville Mayors.

Early life

Benjamin Robert Phelps was born on July 6, 1923, to Noah Phelps (1864-1934), an insurance agent; and Kelly Phelps (1898-1937), a trucker. Phelps' parents bought him a cat named Tabitha when he was three, and he befriended Guy Mendoza-Hicks in 1925, and Elwin Clooney in 1927. Phelps began attending Serena Hole Elementary School, an all-boys school in 1928. His father Noah retired a year later in 1929, the same year Phelps' younger brother Jeremy (1929-1935) was born.

Phelps' early life was beset by tragedy, both his father and his friend Elwin died in 1934, his father Noah dying while sleeping peacefully, and Elwin dying from cancer of the anus. A year later, his little brother Jeremy died from whooping cough. That same year, Phelps' mother married Dennis Handy (1911-1987), a receptionist and changed her name to Kelly Phelps-Handy. In 1937, Phelps' mother died after a tree fell on their vacation home. Phelps was then raised by his stepfather Dennis, who he referred to as "Dad". Phelps had a younger half-brother named Cody Handy (1936-2018), the son of his mother and his stepfather. Phelps had an ambivalent relationship with his stepfather, he didn't hate him, but they weren't very close. Phelps would later describe his childhood as heartbreaking, which led him to pursue greater things.

In 1941, Phelps graduated from Empire High School, an all-boys school, at the top of his class. Because of his good grades, he was awarded a scholarship to university, which he accepted and started a program in biology. His college career made him exempt from military service during World War II. Phelps graduated in 1945 with a Bachelor of Science in Biology, and because of his good grades, he was the valedictorian. He then was awarded a scholarship to medical school, which he accepted. He graduated from medical school in 1952.

Medical career

In 1952, Phelps began working at the Nashville General Hospital as a family physician. He would work there for thirty-one years. During his career, he was renowned for his practical approach to medicine, and his humor and wit that he would share with his patients. He believed that the phrase "Laughter is the Greatest Medicine" was true and would often try to cheer up sick patients with dry humor. While Phelps was beloved by his patients, his superiors didn't think very highly of him and often overworked him. When he retired in 1983, Phelps was working 65 hours a week, and as a result was suffering from high blood pressure. After retiring, however, Phelps recovered from high blood pressure.

Personal life

Phelps began dating his first girlfriend Katherine Lopez in 1939. A year later, Katherine broke up with him. In 1952, shortly after graduating from medical school, Phelps met Wendy Borchlet (b. 1929), and started dating her. Phelps proposed to Wendy a year later and she accepted. The two of them married in 1954 at a beach and went to Milan for their honeymoon. In 1958, Wendy came out as a transgender man and changed his name to Woody. Phelps, originally surprised, later accepted it and offered Woody his unconditional love and support. The two were a very close couple and remained married to each other until Phelps' death in 2024.

Phelps and Woody had six children: Neil (b. 1955), Brendon (b. 1958), Kyle (b. 1960), Juan (b. 1961), Jessica (1963-2015), and Irene (1964-1980). Phelps was very loving and supporting towards each of his children. When his son Brendon came out as gay in 1983, he offered his unconditional love and support. He did the same with his son Kyle, who came out as bisexual in 1991. In 1980, his daughter Irene snuck into a bar with friends despite being underaged, and they were caught in a bar fight. Irene was killed in the fight at the age of 16. Phelps was devastated about his daughter's death. In 2015, Phelps' daughter Jessica traveled to Pamplona, Spain, and was killed during the Running of the Bulls at the age of 52. Phelps was devastated even more.

While Phelps wasn't particularly close to his half-brother Cody, he paid his respects at Cody's funeral in 2018. Similarly, he paid his respects at his stepfather Dennis' funeral in 1987.

Political career

After ten years of retirement, Phelps decided that he had to make a difference in the world. He began his political career by running for Director of the Nashville School Board in the 1993 election. His campaign had a budget of $48,000. Phelps defeated his opponent, 63-year old Regai Ndoro, and won the election. He was sworn in as the Forty-Ninth Director of the Nashville School Board.

Director of the Nashville School Board (1993-2001)

Following being sworn in, Phelps gave a speech on focusing on education. The speech was described to scare the public into believing that education was important, however, it was ineffective and received mixed reviews. During his first term, Phelps focused mainly on improving education and working to protect the environment as he was an environmentalist. After completing his first term in 1997, Phelps announced his campaign for reelection on a $58,000 budget. The 1997 election season was quite intense, as Phelps' opponent, 47-year old Mia Ruiz, did not treat Phelps with respect and was quite vocal about her opinions about her. Ruiz even told a blatant lie about Phelps during a debate. Phelps however, kept a calm and level head and corrected her and cited scientific research. Phelps defeated Ruiz and was reelected Director of the Nashville School Board for a second term.

During his second term, Phelps shortened statistics classes for students, and extended the school day to 8 hours a day, Monday to Friday. He possessed an indomitable willpower, and even reported the caterers he'd hired for an event to the police for dumping food into a pond. Although, it is rumored that he accepted a bribe of $386,000 but didn't go through with what he was bribed. In 2001, Phelps finished his second term and announced he was running for Mayor of Nashville.

Mayor of Nashville (2001-2009)

Phelps budgeted $227,000 towards his campaign and ran as a Democrat against 64-year-old Dwayne Light, a Republican. Despite his campaign manager betraying him to Light, Phelps managed to win the 2001 election and was sworn in as Mayor of Nashville. During his first term, he focused on improving the economy, and fighting crime. His administration was notorious for implementing a new luxury tax to cover a budget deficit. Phelps also created more low-income housing to fight homelessness. In 2005, Phelps announced his run for reelection, and faced off against the Republican candidate, 49-year-old Ace Chapman.

The 2005 Nashville Mayoral Election was beset with controversies. Chapman commissioned a billboard portraying Phelps' head on a snake's body. Phelps tried to get it removed, but the police said that the billboard didn't violate any laws. Despite this, Phelps defeated Chapman and won a second term. During his campaign, he promised to lower taxes and decrease spending. During his second term, Phelps focused on improving the environment and protecting privacy rights, though he passed a law to initiate a public surveillance system. In 2008, during a policy-making session on economics, an angry member of the opposition took off his show and through it at Phelps' head. Luckily, Phelps was able to dodge it. Phelps then took off his own shoe, and threw it at the assailant, hitting him directly in the face. The assailant refused to press charges against Phelps, saying that he probably deserved a shoe to his face. After completing his second term in 2009, Phelps announced his retirement from politics as since he was 86, he believed that he was too old to continue in politics.

Later life

Phelps spent the rest of his life in seclusion with his husband Woody. He attended the funerals of his stepfather and half-brother in 1987 and 2018, respectively. Phelps also planned the funerals of his daughter Irene in 1980, and his daughter Jessica in 2015. He also attended the weddings of his sons Neil, Brendon, and Juan. In 2023, Usher surprisingly began following Phelps on Instagram, for reasons unknown.

Death

Phelps died on March 14, 2024 in his sleep while he was taking his afternoon nap. He was 100 years old, and is survived by his husband Woody; and his sons Neil, Brendon, Kyle, and Juan. They all also attended his funeral. At the time of his death, he was worth $4,724,380.
