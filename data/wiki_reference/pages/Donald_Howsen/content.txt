Donald Howsen
(1934 - 2019), also known by the stage name "
Don Howzer
," was an American dancer. He was the older brother of lawyer and USMC veteran
Derek Howsen
.
Early life
[
]
Donald was born in 1934 in Arlington County, Virginia to magician
Camille Howsen
(1906 - 1994) and writer
Dennis Howsen
(1903 - 1976). In addition to his younger brother Derek, born a year after him, he had another younger brother named
Vernon
(born 1942), a receptionist and Vietnam draft dodger. Growing up during the later years of the Great Depression, as well as World War II, Donald was influenced by the world around him in many ways. When turning ten years old around the time of D-Day in 1944, he pitched the idea for a propaganda film screenplay to his father, who delightfully accepted inspiration to write the script.
Dance career
[
]
After completing high school in 1952, Howsen tried to look for a job as a professional dancer as quickly as possible, having become subject to the Korean War draft. Initially unable to find employment, he ultimately gave in to conscription and enlisted in the military. While stationed in South Korea, he collaborated with the United Services Organization (USO) in providing entertainment by dancing for his fellow troops.
After returning home at the end of the war in 1953, Howsen finally found a job as a dancer for Blart Productions.
...
Howsen retired from professional dancing between 1999 and 2000.
Personal life
[
]
Donald married insurance agent
Lila Campbell
(born 1936) in 1955, having met her the previous year when he was 20 and she was 18. They had four children together before divorcing in 1964:
Tyreese (born 1958) - Wedding planner for Wood & Sons
Gabby (born 1959) - Hairdresser for Atmosphere Salon Arlington County
DJ (born 1960) - Sales associate for Long-Rojas Jeweler
Isabella (born 1962) - Marine biologist for Bush & Sons
Howsen died in 2019, at the age of 85. Survived by his children and grandchildren, his little brother, Derek, would follow five years later in 2024.
