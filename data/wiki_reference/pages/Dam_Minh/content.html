Dam Minh (June 20, 1925 - July TBD, 2025) was a Vietnamese actor, artist, and military officer. He began his service during World War II and eventually rose through the ranks to become an officer. His career in the People's Army peaked during the Vietnam War, during which he served in four ranks, as a captain, major, lieutenant colonel, and colonel. After retiring from the Army as a major general, Ming embarked on an acting career beginning in the late 1980s and rose to prominence during the 1990s and 2000s. He would go on to become one of the most iconic soldiers-turned-actors, often being compared to American actor Dale Dye, another decorated officer and fellow Vietnam veteran.

Early life and education[]

Minh was born in Hanoi to Buu Chau (1901 - 1985) and Dam Phuc (1898 - 1967). His father was a flight attendant and alleged World War I veteran, and his mother was a writer. A year after Minh was born, Chau married Dam Quyen (1908 - 1977), who became Minh's stepfather.
