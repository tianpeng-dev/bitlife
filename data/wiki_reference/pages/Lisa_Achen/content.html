Lisa Achen (2491-after 2559) was a German politician and writer.

Early life (2491-2509)[]

Achen was born in Berlin, Germany. Her parents were Tilda Fernandez and her ex-boyfriend Carlo Achen. She graduated secondary school in 2509.

First writing career (2509-2512)[]

In 2509, Achen became a writer for Bronze Books. She resigned in 2513.

Political career (2512-2532)[]

In 2512, Achen ran for election to the Berlin GEB. She defeated Jannik Baumann and was inaugurated the same year.

In 2516, Achen ran for a second term and beat Julian Wagner.

In 2520, Achen ran for a third term and beat Benjamin Bank.

In 2524, Achen ran for a fourth term and beat Elina Pohl.

In 2528, Achen ran for a fifth term and beat Mila Horn.

In 2532, Achen announced that she would not be running for a fifth term.

Second writing career (2533-2550)[]

In 2533, Achen became a writer for Bronze Books, 20 years after resigning.

Achen was alive in 2550, but her death date is unknown.

Relationship and child[]

From 2512 to 2513, Achen dated a financial analyst named Dieter Schumacher (2490-after 2550). They had a daughter, Amy Schumacher.
