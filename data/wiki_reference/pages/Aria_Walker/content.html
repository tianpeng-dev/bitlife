Aria Walker (née Russell) (February 20, 1904 – April 15, 2024) was a Canadian supercentenarian who, at the age of 120 years, 55 days, is the second-oldest person in history whose age is validated by the Gerontology Research Group (GRG), behind only 122-year-old Jeanne Calment of France.

Early Life[]

Aria Walker was born as Aria Russell on February 20, 1904 in Edmonton, Alberta, Canada. She never knew who her father was as her mother, Julianna Russell (October 9, 1880 – February 21, 1957) had many affairs throughout her early years and never got married. Russell had 3 half-siblings; Nicolas Stroker (January 19, 1910 – March 25, 1985), Victor Russell (September 20, 1917 – March 10, 1992), and Claudia Russell (May 8, 1919 – February 28, 2014).

Career[]

Walker studied marketing in university, graduating in 1925 with an undergraduate degree. In 1926, she was hired as a lobbyist at Paramount Systems in her hometown of Edmonton. She worked at the company for 30 years, until her retirement in 1956.

Personal Life[]

Following in her mom’s footsteps, Walker had numerous one-night stands throughout her late teens. In 1924, she gave birth to an illegitimate son, Vincent Lavoie (March 6, 1924 – December 7, 2013). After Walker’s graduation in 1925, they moved to a trailer park in Edmonton, and 5 years later they relocated to Kildare, a residential neighborhood in the northeast part of the city.

After several years of dating, Walker married her longtime boyfriend, Dick Walker (August 13, 1897 – May 4, 1956), on August 1, 1937 at her aunt’s house. The couple had no children together, but Walker’s husband already had 3 daughters from a previous marriage; Kim (September 11, 1926 – April 26, 2009), Claudia (March 14, 1928 – April 3, 2015), and Caroline Walker (April 21, 1931 – July 18, 2011). The family moved to Jasper Place, Edmonton, Alberta, Canada, which was called West Jasper Place at the time and didn’t amalgamate into Edmonton until 1964.

In the summer of 1947, in celebration of their 10th wedding anniversary, Walker and her family went on a trip to Gold Coast, Australia. After all their children moved out, the couple decided to sell their house in West Jasper Place and moved to Westmount, Edmonton, Alberta, Canada.

Later Life[]

Walker was widowed in 1956. She decided to retire from her job as her mother’s health had been deteriorating and she needed to be looked after. Walker temporarily moved in with her mother until her death less than a year later.

In 1960, Walker bought a condo in downtown Edmonton to be closer to her son who lived there at the time. She continued to live there for the next 55 years, being taken care of by her children and nephews, until she moved into a nursing home at the age of 111 in 2015.

Health and Longevity[]

Walker’s relatives said she was extremely generous, always gifting her used cars to family members instead of selling them. She still drove at the age of 100.

When asked about her secret to longevity, Walker attributed it to her diet and staying active. She had been living on a Mediterranean diet since the age of 24. Her family also had a history of long lives. Her grandmother died at the age of 99 and one of her great-great-grandfathers allegedly lived to be 93.

Following the death of 113-year-old Cora Hansen on April 18, 2012, Walker became the oldest living person in Alberta.

Walker celebrated her 110th birthday in February 2014. She was visited by then-Prime Minister Stephen Harper who congratulated her for her longevity.

Following the death of 111-year-old Marguerite Wabano on November 13, 2015, Walker became the oldest living person in Canada, a title she held for nearly 10 years.

She became the oldest living person in North America on September 27, 2018, following the death of 114-year-old Ophelia Burks of the United States.

Walker celebrated her 117th birthday in February 2021, becoming only the 12th validated person in history to reach this age.

On October 9, 2021, Walker became the oldest validated Canadian person ever after surpassing the final age of Marie-Louise Meilleur.

On May 29, 2023, she surpassed the final age of American Sarah Knauss, becoming the oldest validated person in North America ever.

Following the death of 118-year-old Lucile Randon of France on January 17, 2023, Walker became the oldest living person in the world and the last surviving validated person born before 1907.

In February 2024, Walker celebrated her 120th birthday, becoming only the second validated person to reach this age. This was the first such birthday since February 1995.

Death[]

Walker passed away in Edmonton, Alberta, Canada on April 15, 2024, at the age of 120 years, 55 days. She is the first validated person on record to die at the age of 120. She outlived all of her children, her niece and one of her nephews. At the time of her death, she had 2 (step) grandchildren, 4 (step) great-grandchildren and 6 (step) great-great grandchildren. She was also survived by 2 of her nephews.

Following her death, 117-year-old Maria Branyas Morera of Spain became the oldest validated living person in the world, 114-year-old Elizabeth Francis became the oldest validated living person in North America, 112-year-old Helen Doan became the oldest validated living person in Canada, and 110-year-old Margaret Cameron became the oldest validated living person in Alberta.
