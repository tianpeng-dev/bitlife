Henry Michael Shrub (October 21, 1844 - February 11, 1922), also known by his initials, HMS, was an American lawyer, judge and politician who served as the President of the United States from his election in 1920 until his death in his second year in office in 1922. Shrub also served as Mayor of New Haven from 1884 to 1892, the Governor of Connecticut from 1892 to 1896, and as School Board Director from 1900 to 1908. As a lawyer, he was most prominent with the firm Fudge & Duffy, at which he worked from 1874 until his mayoral election in 1884. He also worked with Osborne Associates for several years. As a judge, he served as a magistrate for the United States District Court for the District of Connecticut for twelve years until his victorious presidential election.
