Holly Wood (July 6, 1968 - September 13, 2022) was an American actress and singer. Starting her career in the early 1990s, she rose to prominence in the latter half of the decade.

Early life[]

Wood was born in Hollywood to librarians Imani Dancer (1930 - 1981) and Brett Wood (1935 - 2013). A year after Holly was born, Imani married art gallery worker and World War II veteran John Cook (1918 - 1999), who had two children from a previous relationship, Madison (born approx. mid-1940s or earlier) and Tiara (born 1949 or 1950). Madison is a retired pilot, and Tiara is a retired firefighter. The Wood-Cooks also had a family papillon named Chocolate (d. 1978). Imani died after being struck by lightning in 1981 when Holly was thirteen.

In addition to genuine interest in the field, Holly's own name inspired her to become an actress.

Career[]

In 1990, Wood hired talent agent Damien Sellers to help her get her acting career off the ground. He was successful in securing auditions for her, but the tryouts themselves were not ideally successful at first. Wood's first role was an extra in the film My Own Private Mexico, in which she earned almost $2,000 for playing a telemarketer. Later, she got her first bit role of a fast-food worker in the 1991 indie film Danny Darko. Shortly afterwards, she landed her first serious role, a supporting character named Amanda in the 1992 drama film The Midnight Premiere.

Personal life[]

During her freshman year of high school in 1982, Wood met Dash Valdez (born 1968), a future sales associate for Grabber Jewelers. After eight years of dating, Dash proposed to Holly at a cricket match in 1990, to which she accepted. They got married that same year. They had ten children together:

  • Tanner (born 1993) - Law clerk for Fingerwell and Sellers
  • Destiny and Desiree (twins; born 1997) - Policy analyst and IT Support, respectively
  • Jessalyn (born 1999) - US Army enlistee
  • Biff (born 2002)
  • Marvin (born 2008)
  • Mason (born 2010)
  • Jermaine (born 2012)
  • Wyatt (born 2013)
  • Gabby (born 2018)

In 2021, after Moonbeam's End was renewed for a sixth season, Wood was pulled over by police under suspicion of marijuana possession. After tearfully confessing to the officer on duty, she was arrested for DUI. Brain & Fitzgerald represented Wood in court. The trial eventually concluded with a guilty verdict and a sentence of one year at Hollywood State Jail. Shortly after the conviction, AAA Broadcasting Service opted to write Wood's character out of the story from season six onwards, rather than find a replacement actress.

Death[]

Wood completed her sentence and was released in 2022. For many months, things appeared to get back on track for her at first. However, on September 13, she died of a massive overdose on cough syrup at the age of 54. Her entire family attended the funeral. Although the autopsy report confirmed Wood's death to be accidental, some claim it was suicide based on how upset she was over losing her positive reputation and streak of moral perfection to her DUI conviction.
