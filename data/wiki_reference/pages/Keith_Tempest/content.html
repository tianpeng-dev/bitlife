Keith Tempest (1932-2023) was an American actor and baseball player. He played for the New York Galleries before trading to the Kansas City Regals, and later moving to Los Angeles to pursue a career in acting.

Early Life (1932-1949)[]

Tempest was born Keith Zapata Tempest, to Caleb Lee Fisher (1906-1982) and Sandra Tempest (1907-1986) in Los Angeles. Fisher was a sperm doner who donated to Sandra Tempest due to her being lesbian but still wanting to raise a child. Tempest was raised by Sandra and her at the time secret girlfriend Nora Zapata (1905-1997). Tempest's family moved to South Dakota in 1936, and would move again to Kansas City, Missouri three years later in 1938. While in Kansas City, Tempest went to Little Dalton Elementary and later enrolled in the middle school. As a child, Tempest was interested in sports and acting, and participated in several high school and middle school plays. He appeared as Ebeneezer Scrooge in his high school's production of "A Christmas Carol". He also began playing baseball in his freshman year, and he would eventually become team captain in his senior year.

The New York Galleries and the Kansas City Regals (1950-1956)[]

Upon graduating high school, Tempest moved to New York and was drafted into the New York Galleries as an outfielder. His first seasons were not successful, and Tempest was often viewed as the laughing stock of the team. After a year of playing, Tempest requested a trade, and joined the Kansas City Regals in 1951, moving back to Kansas City. He played for six seasons and was far more successful, but he was not offered another contract after his current one expired. Tempest was offered to join the Cincinnati Scarletts, but he declined, later stating that he "disliked the mood and community that he was around during his time in sports". He enrolled in college in 1956, majoring in Journalism.

Career[]

In 1960, Tempest moved to Los Angeles to pursue an acting career. He soon met director Lorenzo McNaughton (b. c. 1920s), who gave him his first role in the 1961 comedy film Homies, in which he played a mall cop named Karl. However Tempest and McNaughton's friendship did not last long and the two ended on bad terms. He appeared as an extra in many 1960s films after, but found it hard to get any big roles. In 1964 Tempest portrayed Mr. William Collins in the film Pride And Prejudice. In 1965 he portrayed Calvin Leonard in the comedy film Night By Night.
