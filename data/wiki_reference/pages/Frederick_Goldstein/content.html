Frederick Myles Goldstein Sr. was the 39th President of the United States, the 50th Governor of the State of Louisiana, and a member of the Cambridgeshire Council Chair. He was also a former actor, starring in The Smell of Cinema (1962), which won a Silver Globe for the Best Children's Film, and television shows such as Boned (1951-1960) and Yes, Chef (1963-1967). Goldstein has been a noticeable figure in both the acting and the political industry, and brought the idea to Americans that an entertainer could achieve being the President of the United States. The work that he has produced and written are commonly known as "Goldsteinian Republicanism".

Early Life[]

Fred was born as the eldest son and second child of Albert Goldstein and Victoria McCormick-Goldstein on September 30, 1904. His father was a famous singer, as he is dubbed the "Father of Modern Pop Music" by many. Through his mother, Fred is also related to the wealthy McCormick family. When he was 8, Fred decided to audition as the lead role in his school rendition of L. Frank Baum's The Wizard of Oz. Although surprising and horrifying that a boy was playing the role of Dorothy Gale, Fred seemed to have successfully operated his role, despite his gender. He soon graduated middle and high school as the President of the Drama Club and the Treasurer of the Student Council.

At first, Fred wanted to pursue a career in music, just like his father did. However, he instead focused on getting a degree in law to become a Magistrate. While studying in law school, Fred took up acting classes and found his true talent. With the encouragement of both his parents, Fred started taking up roles as an extra throughout different pieces of media, including both film and television.

Entertainment Career[]

Goldstein first found work as Gavin Nelson, a reoccurring character in the TV show, Silicon Valley (1946-1950). Although the first season of the show was highly regarded by many critics, and even won a Bitty nomination for Best Sitcom, the show slowly died off. After that, he played Vance Sweets, appearing as a regular on the show Boned (1951-1960). During his tenure, he got the Bitty Award for the Best Supporting Actor 5 times, and also got nominated 3 times for the award. After the end of Boned, Goldstein went on to play the character of Dr. I. C. Wiener in the cult classic musical film The Stocky Horror Picture Show (1961), where he got a nomination for the Best Supporting Actor. In 1962, Goldstein portrayed the main protagonist in The Smell of Cinema, portraying Ellis Thomas. The film became a smash hit and was well received among general audiences, and it eventually won the Silver Globe Award for Best Children's Film. Lastly, Goldstein portrayed the chef Corbin Ramsay in the docudrama Yes Chef (1963-1967), which led him to receive an award for Best Actor, 2 nominations for the award, and leading to a nomination of Best Show. After the ending of Yes Chef, Goldstein momentarily halted his acting career.

Political Activites[]

Governor of Louisiana[]

In 1967, after the cancelation of Yes Chef, Goldstein ran as the Republican nominee for the Louisiana guberntorial election that was going to happen on February 4, 1967. His opponent was Caleb McQueen, a 42-year-old Democrat. During his campaign, Goldstein ran out of funds, which eventually resorted him to use his own wealth, and also used McQueen extramarital affair against him. Although the candidates ended up in a tie-breaker, the outrage caused by McQueen's scandal helped Goldstein win the tie-breaker. He was officially the Governor-Elect of Louisiana. He was officially sworn in as the 50th Governor of Louisiana months later, in 1968. During Goldstein's tenure as Governor, he was well-known for fiercely campaigning for the increase of Louisiana's economy and mostly focused on Louisiana's economic issues. He is also known for hosting many rallies each year. In 1971, he decided to seek a second term as Governor and went against Diana James, a 48-year-old Democrat. Goldstein won in a landslide. In 1975, Goldstein's second term expired.

President of the United States[]

In 1975, Goldstein poured $154,487,008 of his own money to run for President of the United States. He won the Republican primary, and ran against Lillian Ortiz, a 69-year-old who was the Democratic nominee for President. During the campaign, Goldstein decided to focus on California, which contained 55 electoral votes if won. This decision proved to be risky but rewarding, as Goldstein won and was sworn in as President of the United States. During his tenure, Goldstein was known to expose corrupt businesses and was a core member of building more low-income homes to combat the growing issue of homelessness. In 1979, Goldstein sought a second term and fought against 58-year-old Jose Reagan of the Libertarian Party. During the campaign, Goldstein was featured on Time Magazine, and promised to address the nation weekly for transparency. In 1983, his term ended.

Cambridgeshire Council Chair[]

In 1983, Goldstein, along with his wife and youngest children, emigrated to the United Kingdom. Goldstein poured £10,000,000 to run as the Cambridgeshire Council Chair, and faced off against 64-year-old Amelia Cook of the Libertarian Party. Surprisingly, Goldstein won in a landslide victory, becoming one of the only American-born politicians to serve in British government. During his tenure, a son, Ernest, was born to Goldstein and his wife. However, in 1984, Goldstein resigned his post and emigrated back to the United States.

Later Years[]

Goldstein spent his later years at his home in New Orleans, Louisiana, while also taking care of his children and grandchildren. He began preparing his eldest son, Benjamin Goldstein, on handling the family fortune. In 2002, at age 98, Frederick got a job as a Magistrate for the City of New Orleans, working along with his son, Henry Goldstein. He died on November 2, 2004, aged 100.

Trivia[]

  • Frederick was named after Holy Roman Emperor Frederick Barbarossa.
  • Frederick's middle name is in honour of his paternal grandfather, Myles Goldstein.
  • Frederick's son, Frederick Myles Goldstein Jr., almost went to college in the United Kingdom, but shortly emigrated.
  • Frederick's son, Benjamin, was named after Frederick's younger brother, Benjamin Allen Goldstein.
  • Frederick's daughter, Remilia Goldstein, married into the wealthy Rockefeller family, marrying James H. "Jimmy" Rockefeller. They had issue.
