Marcus Ethan Reed (October 15, 1928 - March 9, 2022) was an American dance instructor. His career at Los Angeles Dance Troupe spanned over six decades, and he worked with many of the greatest dancers of all time.

Early life[]

Mark was born in Los Angeles to Ethan (1903 - 1968) and Emma Reed (1905 - 1981). His father was a jeweler, and his mother was a makeup artist. He had a cat named Henry (c. 1926 - 1935). Mark was the oldest of six other siblings, including Shanice (1933 - 1938), Elena (born 1935), Brendon (1936 - 1997), Hareem (born 1941), Lauren (born 1944), and JB (1946 - 2018). Elena is a retired police officer from the LAPD. Brendon was a restaurateur for Anytime Restaurant. Hareem is a retired restaurateur from Chez Lilac. Lauren is a retired flight attendant for Baker Air. JB was a mail carrier. Shanice died at the age of five from whooping cough. Brendon was stabbed to death in an alleyway mugging in 1997.

Reed's passion for dance stemmed from a young age when his parents took him to see shows of various genres, such as vaudeville, Broadway, and many others. He became a student actor in middle school, earning the treasury to the drama club in eighth grade. He was the president of the film club in his high school. He attended UCLA Class of 1950, majoring in a dance program, but was unable to participate in extracurriculars due to "poor grades." Nonetheless, Reed would still be seen dancing around campus, expressing his skills, his passion, and his dreams to make a career out of his favorite hobby.

Career[]

After graduating from UCLA, Reed applied for a job at Los Angeles Dance Troup as an assistant instructor. He was hired with a starting salary of over $20,000 per year (over $250,000 pre year today).

Personal life[]

In 1962, Reed met Viviane Hurt (born 1930), a soldier in the United States Army as a veteran of the Korean and Vietnam Wars. They married a year later and had two children together, including Cruella (born 1965) and Robert (born 1968). They divorced in 2018.

Reed died on March 9, 2022 at the age of 93. He is survived by his two children, four grandchildren, and his two siblings.
