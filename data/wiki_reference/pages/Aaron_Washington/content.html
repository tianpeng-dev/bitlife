Aaron Washington (1927 - 2007) was a United States Air Force veteran who served during World War II, the Korean War, and the Vietnam War. He was the father of reggae singer Toby Washington.

Early life[]

Aaron was born in New York to a proud family of veterans. His father fought in World War I at the age of sixteen (falsifying his age to get in), his grandfather fought in the Spanish-American War, his great-grandfather in the Civil War, and he even had an ancestor that fought in the Revolutionary War during colonial times.

Air Force career[]

World War II[]

When he graduated high school in 1945, the European campaign had come to a close, but the Pacific was still in full swing. He enlisted in the United States Army Air Forces, the predecessor to the Air Force.

Post-military career[]

Following his retirement from active duty, Washington opted to continue his affiliation with the military via reserves and other programs. He remained active in the aviation industry, spending much of the 1990s as a flying instructor for both military and civilian pilot trainees.

Personal life[]

Washington had met his wife, Brooklyn, during the last days of World War II. They married sometime between the late 1940s and the early 1950s. Their son, Toby, was born on June 30, 1952. Aaron and Brooklyn remained married until her death in 2005. Aaron died two years later in 2007. Toby died on July 4, 2022. Aaron is survived by his four grandchildren:

  • DJ (born 1982) - Graphic designer for Hunter & Sons
  • Serinity (born 1984) - Mailwoman
  • Honey (born 1991) - Legal secretary for Gump & Reed
