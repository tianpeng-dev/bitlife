Tetanus 😬
[
]
Chance of cure after visiting doctor: 70% (higher if vaccinated or treated early)
Likelihood of death: Moderate to High
Notes: Contracted via deep cuts or wounds; causes muscle spasms
Average quickness of death: Days to weeks if untreated
Cystic Fibrosis 😷
[
]
Chance of cure: 5–10% (mostly managed with medication/lung therapy)
Likelihood of death: High
Notes: Chronic genetic disease affecting lungs and digestion; requires lifelong care
Average quickness of death: Usually decades, gradual decline
Anthrax 😷
[
]
Chance of cure: 50%
Likelihood of death: High
Notes: Caused by contaminated meat
Cholera 🤢
[
]
Chance of cure: 75%
Likelihood of death: High
Notes: An acute bacterial infection
Fatal Familial Insomnia 😫
[
]
Chance of cure: 1%
Likelihood of death: High
Notes: Chronic prion disease affecting ability to sleep
Average quickness of death: 1-3 years
Parkinson’s Disease 😬
[
]
Chance of cure: 0% (can only manage symptoms)
Likelihood of death: Moderate in late stages
Notes: Progressive neurological disorder; affects movement
Average quickness of death: 10–20 years after onset
Dengue (Severe / Hemorrhagic) 😷
[
]
Chance of cure: 60% with hospitalization
Likelihood of death: Moderate
Notes: Tropical viral infection; severe forms can cause bleeding and organ failure
Average quickness of death: Days to 2 weeks in severe cases
vCJD (Variant Creutzfeldt-Jakob Disease)🧠
[
]
Chance of cure: 0%
Likelihood of death: Very High
Notes: Fatal prion disease causing neurological decline
Average quickness of death: Months to 1–2 years after onset
Brain eating amoeba 😬
[
]
Chance of cure: 0%
Likelihood of death: Extremely high
Notes: Caused by dirty freshwater going up your nose
Average quickness of death: Hours to months
Huntington’s Disease 😷
[
]
Chance of cure: 0%
Likelihood of death: Very High
Notes: Late-onset genetic disorder; gradual brain degeneration
Average quickness of death: 10–25 years after first symptoms
Cirrhosis 😷
[
]
Chance of cure: 10–20% if detected early and alcohol intake stopped
Likelihood of death: High
Notes: Severe liver damage; can result from alcohol abuse or hepatitis
Average quickness of death: Months to years depending on severity
Polio 😩
[
]
Chance of cure: 0% (supportive care only)
Likelihood of death: Moderate
Notes: Rare today; can cause paralysis, especially in children
Average quickness of death: Weeks to months in severe cases
Monkeypox 😷
[
]
Chance of cure: 50–70% with medical care
Likelihood of death: Low to Moderate
Notes: Usually mild but dangerous for immunocompromised patients
Average quickness of death: 1–3 weeks
Paralytic Shellfish Poisoning 🦐
[
]
Chance of cure: 50% with hospital treatment
Likelihood of death: High
Notes: Caused by eating toxic shellfish; leads to paralysis and respiratory failure
Average quickness of death: Hours to days
Salmonella 🤢
[
]
Chance of cure: 70–90% with fluids and antibiotics
Likelihood of death: Very low
Notes: Foodborne illness; severe cases can cause dehydration or sepsis
Average quickness of death: Days to 1–2 weeks
Zika (Pregnancy-related)🦠
[
]
Chance of cure: N/A (treatment is supportive)
Likelihood of death: Indirect (mainly affects fetus)
Notes: Can cause fetal death or severe birth defects; usually mild for adults
Average quickness of death: Not usually fatal to adult
