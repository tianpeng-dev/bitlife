Uma Dingleberry
(January 28, 1929 - May 12, 2024) was an Amerian exotic dancer, choreographer, real estate agent, and actress. Her stripping career spanned nearly three decades from the late 1940s to the mid-1970s, starting from when she was 18 years old. Dingelberry could also be seen partaking in minor acting credits as a side-hustle. After retiring from stripping, she would go on to spend a total of forty years as a choreographic dancer and as a real estate agent, from the late 1970s to the late 2010s.

Early life
[
]
Uma was born in Charleston to stay-at-home parents Destiny (1891 - 1965) and Trayvon Dingleberry (1874 - 1950). She had an older brother named Benjamin (1926 - 1953), a makeup artist who started out as a beauty assistant for military personnel during World War II, then later working for the city's official film studio. She had two pets growing up, including a pussycat named Snow (1927 - 1931) and a dalmatian named Coby (1930 - 1944). She became best friends with Anita Juarez (1929 - 2012), at the age of five. Juarez would grow up to become a police officer. Benjamin died at the age of 27 from skin cancer.
Stripping career
[
]
Dingelberry embarked on her dancing career after graduating from high school in 1947.
...
Uma originally planned to work as a stripper for exactly thirty years, retire at the age of 48, and then attend community college so she could graduate and start her secondary career specifically at age 50. However, these plans backfired when she was forced to retire in 1974, three years earlier than intended. Ultimately, she spent a year working as garbage collector before enrolling in a 4-year college program in 1975. Dingleberry majored in dance, intending to use her 27 years of experience to teach the next generation(s) of dancers.
Choreography
[
]
After graduating from college in 1979, Dingleberry browsed jobs on the market and eventually found work as a choreographer.
...
Dingleberry retired from her dance teaching career in 1999 at the age of 70.
Real estate
[

Following her second retirement, Dingleberry decided to enter the business world, finding a new job as a real estate agent.

Uma retired for the third and final time in 2019, at 90 years old.
Acting
[
]
On the side of her other occupations, Dingleberry would work on the side as an actress. She started off as an extra in her early dancing career but would later be known for supporting roles in notable films.
Personal life
[
]
Dingelberry met her first husband,
Anthony Smart
(1929 - 1998), a policy analyst, during childhood. They started going out at the age of ten and married in 1947. Smart was killed in a gas station explosion in 1998. There is debate on whether the incident was an accident or an act of terrorism; the original report found no signs of arson or foul play. Uma and Anthony had ten children together:
Natalie (1946 - 2016) - Operations analyst for Essence Automotive
Jayla (born 1949) - School administrator
Anita (born 1951) - Insurance agent at Dynamic Insurance
Vinnie (born 1954) - Policy analyst
Marilyn (born 1955) - Marine biologist for Mr. Bosom
Buddy (born 1957) - Insurance agent at Dynamic Insurance
Jay (born 1961) - School administrator
Daevon (1969 - 1990) - University student (Engineering major); killed in a railroad accident at age 21
Amy (born 1974) - Enlisted sailor in the US Navy
Juan (born 1979) - Financial advisor for Charleston Credit Union
After Smart's death, Dingleberry married her second husband, Casey (born 1958), with whom she had an affair years prior. They remained married until Dingleberry's death.
Death
[
]
Dingleberry died in her sleep on Mother's Day of 2024 at the age of 95. She is survived by her second husband, eight surviving children, and 21 grandchildren. At the time of her death, she had a net worth of over $16 million.
