Eagle Hawkson (February 9, 1940 - May 17, 2024), also known by the codename "Hawkman," was an American spymaster, lobbyist and ex-soldier in the United States Army. He led many missions throughout his career, some being relatively successful. As a lobbyist, he spent three decades working for Begonia Mills.

Early life[]

Eagle was born in Washington to grocer Breanna Delgado (1918 - 2009) and photographer Yusef Hawkson (1871 - 1955). He had a cat named Ursula (d. 1954). Yusef worked as a war photographer during World War I. When Eagle was a year old, Delgado married Edward Nigma (1923 - 2009), a puzzle master from Gotham, New Jersey. He had four half-sisters, including Ciara (born 1945), Oprah (1947 - 2022), Becky (born 1948), and Alyssa (born 1955). Ciara is a retired jewelry saleswoman for Bohner Brothers Jewelry, Oprah was a receptionist for Begonia Mills, Becky is an author and retired writer for Lawless Press, and Alyssa is a retired photographer from The Washington Tribune.

From a young age, Hawkson became fascinated with the art of espionage. Growing up with various popular World War II-era spy comics, he realized he wanted to become a spy himself when he grew up. At Kylie Wallace High School, he was a seasoned member of the weightlifting club, becoming its president during his junior-to-senior year. Hawkson graduated from Kylie Wallace High in 1958.

Military service and spy career[]

After graduating high school, Hawskon started his agency in 1958. That same year, he enlisted in the Army ahead of his conscription.

...

In 1962, just as Hawkson was about to request an honorable discharge, his commanding officer informed him that he would be recieving a discharge that was "other than honorable." Hawkson insulted his commanding officer before reluctantly accepting the situation. The exact reason for the Army's decision has yet to be determined, though it is likely that they had found out about Hawkson's espionage activity and suspected him of being involved with the communist party.

Following his discharge, Hawkson enrolled at Washington State University, Class of 1966, majoring in communications. He was a member of the military club and the "brainy kids" clique. He became the president of the military club in 1965. Hawkson graduated from WSU in May of 1966. With the Vietnam War still in full swing, he attempted to rejoin the Army as an officer, but was rejected due to his dishonorable discharge. Hawkson would spend the next four years unemployed until he began his lobbying career.

Lobbying and finance[]

In 1970, after four years of unemployment, Hawkson found work as a lobbyist for Begonia Mills. When asked by his interviewer about the gap in his resume, he told numerous half-truths, refraining from revealing his agency.

Personal life[]

During his freshman year of high school, Hawkson met Ellie Bautista, who would later become a real estate agent for Grace & Ortega. Death[]

Hawkson died on May 17, 2024. He is survived by his wife, six surviving children, three sisters, and six granddaughters.
