Daniel "Dan" Dickinson (May 5, 1931 - June 4, 2021) was an American Navy enlistee and fisherman. He dedicated over five decades of his life to Avalon Fish Company.

Early Life[]

Dan was born on May 5, 1931 to Paisley Dickinson, a flight attendant. His father was never revealed. He had a half brother, Travis. Dickinson joined the outdoors club in high school.

Service in the Navy[]

Dickinson graduated high school in 1949. He elisted in the Navy and served his country for twenty years with eight positions:

- Seaman Apprentice (1950)

- Seaman (1952)

- Petty Officer Third Class (1956)

- Petty Officer Second Class (1957)

- Petty Officer First Class (1958)

- Chief Petty Officer (1960)

- Senior Chiet Petty Officer (1962)

- Master Chief Petty Officer (1966)

Life as a Fisherman[]

Following his retirement from the Navy, Dickinson was hired as a Fisherman for Avalon Fish Company in 1969. His fifty-two years at the company never went unnoticed. He shared many amazing fishing stories with his children and grandchildren.

Personal Life[]

Dickinson began dating his classmate, Briona Ryder-Cyrus, who would later become a mail carrier, in middle school. They broke up for a time, but got back together in 1952. They married that same year. They had three children and seven grandchildren.

Death[]

Dickinson suffered a stroke and died on June 4, 2021. He is survived by his wife, children, grandchildren, nieces and nephews.
