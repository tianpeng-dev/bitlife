Frank Kaiser
(December 2nd, 1920 - September 1st, 2011) was a Captain in the United States Army, renowned Hollywood actor, and highly praised politician who eventually served as the 40th President of the United States.

Early Life & Education
[
]
Frank was born on December 2nd, 1920, in Los Angeles, California. His mother was 26-year-old mail carrier, Ophelia Kaiser. Frank never knew his father and would be raised all alone by his mother.
As a young boy, Frank would go to the theater with his mother and watch films. This sparked an interest in Frank which would leave a long-lasting impact in his life. By only 5 years old, Frank already knew that he wanted to become an actor in films.
Throughout primary school, Frank would come out on top in all of his classes and would become Valedictorian during his senior year in high school. After he graduated, Frank would move to Boston, Massachusetts, after being awarded a scholarship at Harvard University. Once again, Frank would graduate at the top of his class in 1942.
Military Service (WW2)
[
]
Frank would feel a sense of patriotism due to the ongoing war and would head back to Los Angeles after graduation, becoming an Officer in the United States Army. Leading troops into battle oversees while managing to cheat death several times. He'd be promoted 2 times during the war and would be sent back home in 1945, later being honorably discharged in 1948.
Acting Career
[
]
While strolling through Los Angeles, Frank would bump into a man named Salvador Ramos, an experienced but unknown talent agent. Salvador would see Frank's talent in acting and would offer to become his very own talent agent. Frank would eagerly accept, wanting to fulfill his childhood dream of becoming a Hollywood star.
Frank would be met with the reality that it was difficult to land a role in a film and become a star in Hollywood. He would nearly give up and become a Junior Associate for Doubtfire & Witherspoon. In a final attempt to become known by movie producers so he could get a big role in a film, he would take minor roles in movies as an extra.
A year later, Salvador would manage to secure a bit role for Frank in a fantasy film named Scott Pioneer vs. The Universe, as a character named Wallace. The film would be a commercial success, making millions in the box office. Frank would be paid 1.3 million dollars for his role and would quickly rise to stardom in Hollywood.
Throughout Frank's acting career he would earn an astounding 14 Bitcademy awards and 3 Silver Globe awards, cementing his legacy as one of the most influential actors of his generation. At age 53, Frank decided to hang up his hat and retire from acting, setting his sights on politics by running for mayor of Los Angeles as a Republican.
Political Career
[
]
Mayor Of Los Angeles
[
]
Frank would budget $1 million dollars for his campaign in 1973 and would run against Democratic candidate Samuel McCain. Frank would sweep the competition and be elected as Mayor of Los Angeles. As soon as he was in office, he began to focus on crime throughout the city in an attempt to bring back the glory days of Los Angeles. He'd leave office with an impressively high approval rating of 84% and would not seek a second term.
Governor of California
[
]
In 1977, Frank would run for Governor of California and would budget $10 million dollars for his campaign. He'd be up against Green Party candidate Manuel Rossi. The election would be close with both candidates being strong competitors, Kaiser being a well renowned Hollywood actor & war veteran, while Rossi was a well-respected entrepreneur based out of San Francisco. After many debates and campaigning, Kaiser would come out on top winning 54 percent of the vote, Rossi falling right behind him with 44 percent with 2 percent going to other minor candidates.
As governor, Frank would work with lawmakers to get the state budget for education increased by approximately 30% in order to heal the still open wounds of the 60's. Even though it was a smart move that would benefit generations to come, many entrepreneurs throughout the state would feel otherwise about the new changes due to the effect it could have on the economy. Frank was aware of this and would quickly get to finding solutions that would balance back what was lost. After just a year, his approval economically wise would skyrocket making the state incredible amounts of money.
Once again, Frank would not seek a second term and would leave office with an impressively high approval rating of 92% after starting his term with a weak approval rating of 39%.
President of The United States (First Term)
[
]
In 1981 at the age of 61, Frank would announce his run for president with a budget of a little over 100 million dollars. He would be up against 38-year old Libertarian Party candidate Charlotte Lopez, an underdog in the political world. Frank would appear on CNN, a popular news station that openly did not support him but would crush every question thrown at him. During a rally, Frank would take questions from an angry protester, responding to every single one leaving her in shock. All of this media attention would secure Frank's win for the presidency even though the odds were still in his favor. Frank would win the election in a landslide victory, securing 60% of the vote.
In 1982, Frank would be informed by citizens of Singapore about how unfair their minimum wages were. Frank would take it upon himself to speak with government officials of Singapore to bump up their pay, some rumoring that he used intimidation tactics to get what he thought was right for the people of Singapore. Over in the states, Frank would pass a new law to increase funding for financial education in schools. Also in response to an increased rate of homelessness in the nation, he'd start a program to create more mental health centers in the United States.
In 1983, a political party watch group would accuse Frank's office of spying on an opposing party's election headquarters. He'd respond by authorizing a thorough internal investigation into the matter as he had nothing to hide. During a press conference in the Oval Office, Frank would accidentally use a curse word while describing a political opponent. He'd carry on as if it never happened.
He'd end his first term on a very strong approval rating of 93%.
President of The United States (Second Term)
[
]
Frank would run for re-election in 1985 and would be up against Socialist Party candidate Atticus Cunningham. During a debate, a college student would ask Frank if he were re-elected would he make changes to the White House and what would they be. Frank would respond by saying he'd install a state-of-the-art sound system. One day during his campaign, he would make a trip to Detroit, Michigan, and attend a rap concert in order to get more voters with the youth. This would almost backfire on Frank as several photos of him at the concert with what appeared to be red eyes.
He would win the election by a landslide once more, being re-elected as president. During his second term, he would sometimes go to colleges across the country and speak with students on campuses in an effort of getting more positive publicity. In 1987, an alien lifeform would land in the Nevada desert. Frank would give Area 51 researchers full responsibility for handling the discovery.
Frank would finish his second term in 1989 with an all time high approval rating of 95%. Frank would be regarded as one of the most influential Republican presidents in history.
Post Presidency
[
]
Frank would head back to Los Angeles at his Bel Air estate, spending the rest of his days visiting colleges speaking with students, and traveling across the world with his wife Serena. He'd live a relatively quiet life until his peaceful passing on September 1st, 2011, at the age of 91 years.
