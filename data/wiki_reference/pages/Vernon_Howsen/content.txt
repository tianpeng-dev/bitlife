Vernon Howsen
(born 1942) is an American advocate former receptionist. He is the younger brother of USMC veteran and lawyer
Derek Howsen
.
Early life
[
]
Vernon was born in Arlington County, Virginia as the younger brother of
Donald Howsen
(1934 - 2019) and Derek Howsen (April 1, 1935 - May 14, 2024). His mother,
Camille Howsen
(1906 - 1994) was a magician, and his father,
Dennis Howsen
(1903 - 1976) was a writer. His oldest brother, Donald, was a dancer. At the time of Vernon's birth, Dennis was busy with contributions to the war effort, writing letters and books for the troops and creating the screenplays for numerous Allied propaganda movies.
Career
[
]
Howsen graduated from high school in 1960, thus becoming subject to the draft. Coincidently, his big brother, Derek, had enlisted in the Marines after finishing law school. Not wanting to become another statistic of young Americans who had their lives thrown away fighting a war that the country had nothing to do with, he burned his draft card and spent the next year looking for a job. It is speculated that he served time in jail at one point for openly expressing his decision to dodge the draft. However, it is also believed that Vernon actually did end up serving in Vietnam, and even received an award or two for being wounded in action. Howsen has neither confirmed nor denied these claims, leaving the story to be a mystery.
In 1961, Howsen had found work as a receptionist for Jupiter Mills. It is unclear if he had gotten the job at that time with a yearlong gap on his resume or if he got the job the previous year after graduating high school.
