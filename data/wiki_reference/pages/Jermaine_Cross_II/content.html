Jermaine Cross II (3041-after 3116) was a French bluegrass singer and business analyst.

Early life (3041-3066)[]

Cross was born in Lyon, France. He was the youngest of four children born to Sonny Cross II and zir second partner Agathe Guillot. He was named for his 17x great-uncle.

Cross went to ENSL and graduated with a Licence in English Studies in 3064. He went to Emlyon Business School and graduated with an MBA in 3066.

Music career (3072-3089)[]

In 3072, Cross signed with Oasis Studios as a country singer. He released his debut bluegrass album, Country Steel, which went gold in less than a year.

In 3073, Cross released his second album, The Limeade Stand, which went gold in less than a year.

In 3074, Cross released his fourth album, Itty Bitty Bluebird, which went gold in 3076.

In 3079, Cross released his fifth album, Dumplin’, I’m a Lone Canine, which went gold in less than a year and platinum in 3089.

In 3084, Cross released his sixth album, Champagneville, which went platinum in less than a year and double platinum in 3088.

In 3089, Cross was released by Oasis.

Business analysis career (3090-3103)[]

In 3090, Cross became a junior business analyst for Fingerwell Corp.

In 3100, Cross was promoted to business analyst.

In 3103, Cross retired.

Later life[]

Cross was alive in 3116, but his death date is unknown.
