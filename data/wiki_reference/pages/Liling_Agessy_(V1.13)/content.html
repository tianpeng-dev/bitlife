Liling Agessy (née Zedong; July 10, 1877 - July 29, 1964) was a Canadian and American professional volleyball player who spent most of her career playing for the Vancouver Vans from 1895 (the year the sport was invented) to 1909. She also played for the American teams Boulder Brownies from 1909 to 1914 and the Detroit Material Girls from 1914 to 1918. She won many awards throughout her career in both her home country and the United States.

After retiring from volleyball, Agessy spent twenty years working as a marriage counselor from the early 1920s to the early 1940s. She then embarked on a political career spanning the last two decades of her life, whereas she served as a school board director and the mayor of her city.
