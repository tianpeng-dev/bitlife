Napoleon Antionette
(1890 - c. 1960s) was a French Foreign Legion soldier who served as an enlistee during World War I and as an officer during World War II and the Korean War. He is widely known as the stepfather of French politician and Navy sailor
Kylian Morin

Napoleon was born in France in 1890 to a caring and prominent family of French nobles. According to most sources, his parents named him after Napoleon Bonaparte.

After four decades of service, Antionette retired from the Army in 1952 at the age of 62.
Personal life
[
]
Upon returning home from the war in 1918, Antionette married Sarah Molin, the widowed mother of Kylian Morin. They had their own daughter, Julia (1927 - 2010). A year after Julia's birth, they divorced.
