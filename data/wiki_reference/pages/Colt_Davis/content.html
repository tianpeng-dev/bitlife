William Colter "Colt" Davis, better known by his stage name Colt Davis was an American musician, singer songwriter, and astronaut.

Born on July 13th, 1944, in San Francisco, California, he moved with his family to Atlanta, Georgia in 1946, and later Memphis, Tennessee in 1949. Davis grew up listening to classic rock and roll and blues artists, and also became interested in the country music scene in Nashville. Inspired by the musicians there, he started practicing guitar and singing and played country music briefly during the mid 50s. Davis gradually grew tired of the music however, and turned his attention to the pop bands emerging during the early sixties. In 1962, upon graduation of high school, Davis and his classmate Mark Patton formed the band Delusional Dolphins with 2 other musicians. Throughout 1962 and 1963 the band released multiple singles until they got to recording their self-titled debut album in 1964, simply titled Delusional Dolphins. The release was a success and the band started touring the US and attracting many fans. By 1965 they had a gold (later diamond) record titled I've Got The Hour. Despite all the success, Davis began to grow tired of the simple pop style. He suggested many new albums, wanting to go in a new direction but his band refused. Tensions between Davis and the rest of the band led to the end in 1966. Throughout the 60s, Davis was in numerous bands but his biggest success post-1965 was 1971's self titled Opaque Sabbath, released by the band Opaque Sabbath. He was in the band The Mosquitoes at one point, but final managed to sign a deal to go solo in 1973. During the 70s he became an alcoholic and drug abuser and never was able to garner any success. In 1978 he released his last album, and moved to France 2 years later. Davis' whole life, he'd been very interested in space, especially since he had watched the moon landing years before. Davis decided to pursue this dream and went back to college to get a degree in engineering. From 1980-1984 he studied at a university in Paris and applied for astronaut school that same year.
