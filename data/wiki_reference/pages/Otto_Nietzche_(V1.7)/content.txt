Otto Nietzche
(20 November 1959 - 24 April 2014) was a German politician, museum worker and soldier who served as the director of the Cologne School Board from 1985 to 1993. He is best known for his ambitious but unsuccessful attempts to run for high political offices, such as the governorship of his home state and the Chancellorship of the country.

Early life
[
]
Otto was born in Cologne, Germany to jeweler Lara Klum (1925 - 2013) and hairdresser Nicholas "Nick" Nietzche (1924 - 2006). He had two siblings, Friedrich (born 1954), a hairdresser like his father, and Hannah (born 1964), a flight attendant. He also had a stepbrother named Liam from his mother's brief marriage to retired restaurant worker Kilian Becker (1934 - 2008). He had a pet mongrel named Marlon (1955 - 1969).

Personal life
[
]
ietzche met his future wife, grocer Pauline Zimmer (born 1969) at age ten. They dated for ten years throughout their school years and got married in 1979 during Otto's third year in the military. They had four children and five grandchildren together:
Sofia (born 1979) - Navy sailor
Lothar (born 1984) - Receptionist for Swift Counseling
Annabell (born 1986) - Firefighter
Lara (born 2001)
Death
[
]
Otto died of a heart attack on 24 April 2014 at the age of 54. He is survived by his wife, four children, five grandchildren and three siblings. His gravestone is marked with his medal of honor.
