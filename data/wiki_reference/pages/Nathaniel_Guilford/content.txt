Nathaniel Marcus Guilford
(December 25th, 1988 - May 19th, 2074) was a famous actor, model, and writer. He made his acting debut at the age of 18 and was one of the richest actors, having a net worth just over a billion dollars. He played lead roles in many top franchises, including the Revengers, Waking Dead, and Packed Residence, among other shows.

Early life and education
[
]
Nathaniel was born to parents Ruby and Jasper Guilford in Melbourne, Australia. He had an older sister named Georgia (who went on to be a supermodel) and was close with her for his entire life. Unfortunately, Ruby and Jasper divorced when Nathan was just five. However, both of his parents were supportive of his taste in acting and allowed him to take acting lessons. He also joined his school's soccer team, but never had much of an interest in it. On the other hand, he continued his acting lessons and became the President of his school's drama club.
After graduating from secondary school, he decided not to go to university and instead chose to head into the acting world. While he didn't immediately receive his degree, he went on to enroll in university in his fifties during a break from acting and received a degree in finance, before spending another two years in business school.

Early work and critical acclaim
[
]
Post-graduation, Nathan was hired as an extra for nearly a dozen movies before he was discovered by executives and offered a contract in the movie "The Revengers", landing him his first special role. Starring in a movie with over $200M spent in budget required him to hire his first agent, Xavier Williams. While on the set, Nathan met fellow co-star Eleanor Lincoln and started dating her. The two would go on to be a lifelong couple. Starring as Arachnidman, Nathan was nominated for a Bitty Award for his role as the lead, but unfortunately didn't win. On the bright side, however, he would have his own self-titled film "Arachnid-Man", and would perform under the same director, Joseph Smith, The movie won a Silver Globe. He would continue to star in another Revengers movie, where he won a Bitty Award for his performance as a supporting character.
Rise to international fame
[
]
After his first few movies, Nathan decided to move to America, more specifically Los Angeles to pursue a major acting career in Hollywood. Unfortunately, not many auditions worked out, resulting in Nathan firing his agent and agreeing to a deal with Eli Ramirez. Eli found a job for Nathan in the popular TV show "Shook", but the pay was merely a few thousand compared to the millions he previously earned. Still, Nathan agreed. The series went on for four seasons, and Nathan won two Bitcademy Awards as the lead of the series, as well as a third nomination, and the series itself won a Bitcademy award, but ultimately the show was cancelled for declining popularity.
