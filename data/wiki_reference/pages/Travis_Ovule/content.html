Colonel Travis Ovule (July 29, 1927 - November 26, 2023) was an American cult leader and military officer.

Early life and philisophical developments[]

Ovule was born in San Fransisco to makeup artist Irene Ovule (1885 - 1968) and an unknown father. He had a half-sister, Hannah Pousa (1923 - 2005), a sailor in the Navy, and an unemployed stepfather, Charles "Chuck" Mattel (1887 - 1966). Travis had a decent childhood, making a good number of friends in school. He also had a stint as a pickpocket, stealing money from corrupt people, such as gangsters, thugs, and others. He took karate lessons and earned all belts, including the top red, at the age of eight. He also took voice lessons and became a good singer. He joined multiple clubs in middle and high school, and was a member of the hipsters clique.

Cult and military career[]

Upon graduating high school in 1945, Ovule enlisted in the Army. He served as an enlistee for eight years, his fighting experience being in the last days of World War II (Pacific Theatre), and in Korea. In 1953, at the end of the Korean War, Ovule was discharged so he could go to college. He enrolled in the University of California, class of 1957, majoring in philosophy. Ovule was a member of the student council, book club, cancer awareness club, yearbook club, baking club, hipsters clique, and the Sigma Alpha Epsilon fraternity.

Upon graduating UCSF, Ovule was subject to the draft. He managed to avoid conscription by applying as an officer.

Personal life[]

Ovule married twice. His first wife, Nickie Cruz (born 1926), whom he married two years into his Army service in 1947, worked as a stay-at-home mother. They had five children together and remained married until 2013 when Travis found out she cheated on him, after which they divorced. He married his second wife, firefighter Cassandra Palermo (born 1990) a year later. He had one son with Palermo named Jude, but he died at the age of five when he was accidently struck by a foul ball at a baseball game.

Death[]

Ovule died peacefully in his sleep on November 26, 2023 at the age of 96. His grandchildren attended his funeral, while his children skipped it for a baking class. Aside from his children and grandchildren, he is also survived by his second wife.
