Freya Gray
(1927 - 2007) was a British chief petty officer in Her Majesty's Coastguard, serving from 1945 to 1989. She was the first wife of famed rugby player, actor, and later royal consort
Louis Crayford
.

Early life
[
]
Freya was born in Staines to a middle-class family. Her father was a World War I veteran who later worked in multiple fields, such as carpentry, art, medicine, and various others. Her mother worked in other industries, such as entertainment, being an actress, director, producer, and writer for screen and stage, and even worked as a playwright.
Military career
[
]
In 1945, as she was finishing secondary school, Gray enlisted in His Majesty's Coastguard.
...
After forty-four years of service, Gray retired from the Coastguard in 1989.
Personal life
[
]
Gray met Crayford in 1956 while she was off-duty. After a year of dating, they got engaged in 1957 and married within the same year, after Louis had finished his twelfth season in the RFL. They had two children, Ada (born 1960), a professor at Krypton University District, and David (born 1962), a reporter for Syngergy Broadcasting Service. In an interview, Louis stated that one of his few regrets was not having children sooner for the sake of his parents getting to be grandparents to his children for longer than they did; his father Finn had died the year David was born (Ada was 2 years old at the time), and his mother Regina died when Ada was 9 and David was 7.
Crayford and Gray remained married for fifty years. She died in 2007, shortly after their golden wedding anniversary and shortly before Crayford's retirement from acting.
