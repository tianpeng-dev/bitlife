Miley Krueger (2668-2746) was an American financial advisor, writer, and church singer.

Early life (2668-2692)[]

Krueger was born in Jacksonville, Florida. Her parents are Stanley Krueger and his girlfriend Wendy Chen. She graduated from the University of North Florida in 2692 with a BS in Accounting.

First financial advisory career (2692-2697)[]

In 2692, Krueger became a junior financial advisor for Spreadeagle Savings and Loan, ironically the same bank her grandfather had robbed over 40 years prior.

In 2695, Krueger was promoted to financial advisor.

Writing career (2697-2702)[]

In 2697, Krueger became a writer for Compass Publishing.

Music career (2702-2718)[]

In 2702, Krueger signed with Binary Beats as a worship singer. She released her first church album, Shattered Sockets.

In 2703, Krueger released her second album, Crown of Horns.

In 2704, Krueger released her third album, Crown of Flowers.

In 2708, Krueger released her fourth album, Man of Remorse.

In 2711, Krueger released her fifth album, Flatbush Ballet.

In 2714, Krueger released her sixth album, The Profit, which went gold in 2716.

In 2717, Krueger released her final album, Flophouse on a Molehill, which went gold in 2718.

In 2718, Krueger walked out on her contract.

Secondary financial advisory career (2719-2734)[]

In 2719, Krueger became a junior financial advisor for Spreadeagle Savings and Loan again.

In 2734, Krueger resigned.

Death[]

In 2746, Krueger died of natural causes. Her ashes were rolled into a blunt and smoked by her son Sammy.

Relationship and children[]

In 2697, Krueger started dating a Danish-American CEO named Cesar Roma (2679-after 2753). He was the CEO of Triton Foods. They had two adopted sons, Amani Krueger (né Zapata, 2691-after 2753, adopted 2697) and Sammy Krueger (né Slayer, b. 2713, adopted 2714).
