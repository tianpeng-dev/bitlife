Ivanna Mikayla Sangeloa
(February 26, 1970 - April 16, 2024) was an American actress, model, and fighter pilot. After serving in the United States Air Force for twenty years, she embarked on a career of acting and modeling that spanned nearly a couple of decades.

Early life
[
]
Ivy was born in San Angelo, Texas to Mikayla (born 1951) and
Henry Sangeloa
(1923 - 2010). Her mother worked as a trucker, while her father was a graphic designer at Vivacious Inc. and World War II veteran. She had three brothers, Gaylord (born 1969), Winston (born 1982), and
Latrell
(1990 - 2016). Gaylord and Winston are receptionists for Royalty Mills and Lilac Corp, respectively. Latrell was a magician for Abacus Circus; he died in 2016 when he was struck in the head with a golf ball. Ivy had a pet rabbit named Bun (1970 - 1976), whom she had a close bond as a toddler. Bun died when Ivy was six years old, having succumbed to intestinal issues after eating walnuts. Ivy's realtionship with Bun would later become the basis for a family adventure story
Ivy and Bun
, first as a children's picture book, and later adapted into several short and feature films, animated and live-action. Her best friend was Martha Grace (born 1970), who she met when she was two.
Sangeloa was interested in both acting and modeling from a young age. She began taking voice lessons and acting lessons at the age of eight, and modeling lessons at age ten. She sung chorus in middle school, played lacrosse in high school, and she was in the drama club throughout middle and high school.

Acting and modeling
[
]
Following her retirement from the Air Force, Sangeloa embarked on her acting career in 2008. After some searching, she was offered the role of Aliyah Crump in the new series
Dead Case
, which began in 2007. Two years, later, she submitted her headshots for her first choice of modeling clients, Nurse Feelgood's Meds.
Personal life
[
]
Ivy married her childhood friend and high school sweetheart, Anthony Worthy (born 1970) in 1992. Worthy is a cameraman at Prominence Broadcasting Service, and he often worked with Ivy on her modeling gigs. Together, they had two daughters, Avery (born 1994) and Camille (born 1995), and two sons, Marquis (born 1998) and Kevin (2008 - 2019). Avery is a receptionist for Aurora Data, Camille is a real estate agent for Manhole Realty, and Marquis is a mailman. Kevin died in 2019 at the age of 11 from sickle cell disease. Additionally, she had many nieces and nephews from her brother Gaylord.
One of her nephews, Malcolm (born 2000), inspired by his aunt's military career, joined the Air Force himself between 2018 and 2019. At the time of Ivy's death, he had reached the rank of Senior Airman. Ivy was also ever-present on social media, having accounts for every platform, including YouTube and OnlyFans.
Death
[
]
During her service, Sangeloa had developed hay fever. Later, during her acting and modeling career, she developed bunions. In 2020, during the filming of a scene of a film adaptation of
Frankenstein
, she consumed heroin as a method of getting into character. It was at that point that she, at the age of 50, became a heroin addict. Rather than get treated for it, she decided to continue focusing on her career and see how long she could survive, which was not very long. Ultimately, she died from an overdose on April 16, 2024 at the age of 54. Her family, including her still-living mother, her husband, her children, and her two brothers, attended her funeral, which also included military honors due to her service in the Air Force.
Despite the official manner of Ivy's death being listed as accidental, personal reports from family and friends suggest that she overdosed on purpose as a method of suicide. Members of both her immediate and extended family, and most of her closest friends, including her best friend, Martha Grace, claim that she was suicidal due to the losses she suffered throughout her life. The only piece of evidence against the theory of suicide is that Ivy was never officially diagnosed with suffering from depression.
