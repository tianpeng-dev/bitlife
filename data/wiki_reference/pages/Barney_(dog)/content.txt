Puppyhood and early years with first and second owners
[
]
Born in Boston, Attila was conceived in an animal birthing room by his mother. He was put up for sale at Celestial Pet Boutique for $425 ($817.90 in 2025). While waiting to be adopted, he spent quality time with the other store animals, as well as the employees. At six months old, Attila met customer Anna Hux, a retired jeweler. Flattered by his big puppy dog eyes, Hux purchased and adopted Attila and took him to her home, where she lived with her other cat, Lee. Hux also changed his name to Barney, naming him after the children's show, which her grandchildren enjoyed.  Barney and Anna became good friends in the four years they spent together. He only got to know Lee for a few months before he escaped from the house one day and was found dead. When considering tricks to teach himself, Barney practiced fetching tennis balls, among other things.
In 2000, Anna adopted Lee's "successor," who was named Shadow, who got along with Barney fairly well. In 2001, Anna took him on his first trip to the vet. In the same year, he helped to comfort her during the impact of the September 11 attacks. In 2002, Anna suffered a stroke and passed away. Barney was subsequently adopted by his new owners, Vincent "Vinnie" Affleck (formerly Vivian Affleck; born 1964), a transgender travel agent at Dash Fuchs, and Cleveland Affleck (1971 - 2018), a BPD officer. Vinnie and Cleveland had four children, including Rebel (born 2002), Bart (born 2005), Carson (born 2007), and Tony (born 2011). Barney also made fast friends with the Affleck's other dogs, a golden retriever named Gumdrop (d. 2015), and a cocker spaniel named Patata (d. 2013).
