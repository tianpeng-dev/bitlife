Zachary Daniel Edwards (July 4, 1925 - May 5, 2022) was an American politician, dancer, photographer, soldier and football player. After playing football for eight years, he took on several years of professional dancing and choreographing before pursuing a thirty year service in the United States Army, maintaining the rank of second lieutenant. He later became the school administrator and mayor of his hometown.

Childhood and football career[]

Zack was born in Biloxi, Mississippi to the then-newer generation of the Edwards family. His father, Orenthal James "OJ" Edwards (1888 - 1959), was a multi-purpose restaurant worker who cooked and waited tables. His mother, Katherine (1888 - 1969) was a wedding planner. OJ was also a World War I draft dodger who almost served jail time, but instead served as a non-inmate prison cook for POWs that were transported from France, Germany, and other enemy territories.

As a child, one of Zack's aspirations was serving in the military, since his birthday was the 4th of July. He was close to receiving his draft notice after graduating high school in 1943. Edwards didn't want to fight in World War II, having heard many stories of soldiers who went to fight in the war and never came back, so he made himself exempt from the draft by enrolling in the University of Mississippi, Class of 1947. While there, he would partake in military-related extracurriculars and undergo ROTC program training for officer service eligibility.

After finishing college and obtaining his bachelor's in dance, Edwards made himself eligible for the 1947 NFL draft. He accepted a three-year, $1.4 million contract to play as a quarterback for the Kansas City Colonizers.

Dancing and US Army[]

Following his early retirement from football in 1955, Edwards found another job as an assistant dance instructor for Eden Dance Studio. He worked there for one year, before getting another dancing job in 1956, this time as a Jr. Choreographer. In 1957, Edwards joined the Army.

Personal life[]

Edwards met Aiesha Reynolds at ten years old when she asked him out. Reynolds went on to become a flight attendant for Iron Air. They married in 1948. They had two sons, Larry and Lee (twins; born 1954), and a daughter, Brigette (born 1959). Larry is a restaurateur at Diversity Diner, Lee is a trucker for Silicon Transportation, and Brigette is an agent for Sativa Insurance.

Athletic controversey[]

During a game in 1950, a player for the opposing team, the Los Angeles Goats, insulted Edwards. Provoked, Edwards started to attack him, attempting to slap him. The player stormed back at Edwards, inverting his throat. Afterwards, the player and their family sued Edwards, while he countered in the event he would lose. Edwards and the player's family eventually reached a settlement.

Death[]

Edwards passed peacefully on May 5, 2022, less than one month shy of his 97th birthday. He happened to be celebrating Cinco De Mayo with his family. He is survived by his wife, three children and three grandchildren.
