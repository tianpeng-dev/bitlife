Ronald Ali, Sr. (April 1, 1925-April 13, 2025) was a King of England, drug dealer, and founder of the Ali Legacy.

Prince of Harris and King of England (1925-1943)[]

Ali was born the Prince of Harris in Southampton, England. He is the younger of two children born to King Arlo (1880-1943) and Queen Annabelle (1895-1985). His sister was Lara, Princess of Ilchester (1920-1943).

Ali went to St. Emma’s Secondary School and graduated in 1943.

In 1943, Ali assassinated his father and sister. He was briefly the King of England before abdicating.

Drug dealing career (1943-2025)[]

In 1943, Ali joined the Button Pushers and became a runner in less than a year.

Between 1943 and 1945, Ali sold over $50,000 ($888,000 in 2025) worth of coyote and glitter.

In 1948, Ali was promoted to manufacturer.

In 1990, Ali was promoted to lieutenant.

In 2025, Ali died of a stroke.

Relationship and child[]

From 1943 to 1944, Ali dated a lieutenant from his cartel named Spun Amy (1923-1970). They had a son, Ronald, Marquess of Cockwallace.
