Henry Michael Shrub
(October 21, 1934 - February 11, 2012), also known by his initials,
HMS
, was an American lawyer, judge, fighter pilot and politician who served as the President of the United States from his election in 2010 until his death in his second year in office in 2012. Shrub also served as Mayor of New Haven from 1974 to 1982, the Governor of Connecticut from 1982 to 1986, and as School Board Director from 1990 to 1998. As a lawyer, he was most prominent with the firm Fudge & Duffy, at which he worked from 1964 until his mayoral election in 1974. He also worked with Osborne Associates for several years. As a judge, he served as a magistrate for the United States District Court for the District of Connecticut for twelve years until his victorious presidential election. Shrub was also a distinguished U.S. Air Force officer during the Vietnam War.
