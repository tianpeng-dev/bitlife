Jenna Maria Grace Burger (neé Long, December 16, 1969 - July 27, 2022) was an American photographer. Her career spanning over three decades is labeled as a significant timeline in the history of the photography and journalism industries.

Early life[]

Burger was born in Buffalo, New York, United States. She was of Italian descent on her mother's side, and also of English and Welsh descent on her father's side. Her parents were Hailey Long (1931-2020) and Arthur Long (1904-1977). She has an older brother, Brendon Long (1966-). She and her brother both attended private schools. Her father passed away when she was eight years old. Jenna and Brendon's mother married Christopher Fuchs (1938-2004) in 1978, when Jenna was 9 and Brendon was 12.

Career[]

After high school, she was given a volleyball scholarship, but she turned down the opportunity and became a photographer instead. She was known for her photography work with the Buffalo Globe. She was fired from her job in 2021 when she got arrested (more information below).

Personal life[]

She formed a romantic relationship with coworker and fellow photographer, Montgomery Burger (1950-2020), in 1987, whom she would later marry in 1992. They remained together until Montgomery's death in 2020. They had five daughters: Kelly Olivia (born 2001), Violet Nicole (born 2003), Ava Heather (born 2005), Daisy Mae (born 2007), and Tiffany Elaine (born 2009). Jenna had a close relationship with her husband and daughters.

In 2020, Jenna's mother died. Jenna was emotionally impacted by the loss of two important people in her life, and sought comfort from her big brother, Brandon, and her daughter, Tiffany, who had told her, "I miss Dad and Grandma, too!"

Legal issues[]

In 2021, Jenna decided to bribe college officials to ensure that her daughter, Violet, was accepted to college. Unfortunately, the bribery backfired when the college officials called the police on her. She was arrested for bribery and fired from her job. She wrote letters to each of her daughters while in jail. She was released from jail one month before her death, having believed that she made the right decision for Violet.

Death and legacy[]

On July 27, 2022, Jenna became so angry about what happened to her that she committed suicide. She is survived by her five daughters and her older brother, Brendon. Jenna's family members recall that she struggled with the loss of her father, a tragedy that she would later tell her daughters about. Jenna's daughters have lamented about never being able to meet their maternal grandfather, who died before any of them were born (more information above).

Jenna's photographs have been selected for preservation by the Buffalo Globe, and are currently stored in the newspaper's archives.
