Mark Pavlovna
(March 13, 1983 - November 23 2020) was a Russian grocer and military officer known to be the son of
Nikita Pavlovna
and for his heroic act in Syria and Cuba.

Early life
[
]
Born in March 13 1983, he spent most of his childhood in St. Petersburg, Russia (at the time known has the Soviet Union, or USSR for short), with his mother, Kristina Pavlovna, his father,
Nikita Pavlovna
, and his sister,
Lilova Pavlovna
. He didn't  go to University, but worked as a Grocer for a now abandoned store at 17 years old.
Career And Military Service
[
]
Mark started to work as a Grocer in 1991 and worked there for 8 years, untill he went to University to study Communications. After receiving his degree, he went to serve in the military at 31 years old in 2014. he was deployed in Syria in 2017 and in Cuba in 2020, where he died.
Military Medals and Nobel Prize
[
]
Mark Pavlovna was a well respected Military Officer after earning a medal for his bravery in Syria, where he was deployed for 7 months to clear a terrorist minefield. He was also awarded a Nobel Prize in 2018 for saving a thousand of lives in Syria from terrorism.
Personal Life
[
]
Mark ordered and married a bride from Vietnam called Nguyen Linh, and had 2 children, Maria Nguyen and Pogrebnov Pavlovna.
Support for Socialism
[
]
Mark heavily supported socialism since the fall of the Soviet Union (or USSR for short). After his death, 6 books written by him about communism were found in his home, where he supported it and explained how it was good. He also supported other communist states like China or Vietnam.
Conspiracy
[
]
A conspiracy was born after the finding of the socialist books in his home that he tried to convert Syria into communism. It was later debunked.
Death
[
]
In November 23 2020, Mark unfortunately died in battlefield after being blown to smithereens by stepping on a landmine while on battlefield in Cuba. His body wasn't buried at the time of his death, but placed in a Cuban morgue in Morón for 1 week, before getting buried at the Novodevichy Cemetery in St. Petersburg, Russia.
