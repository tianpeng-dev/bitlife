Arthur Hudson Buckley (January 2, 1899 - October 31, 2017) was a American archaeologist and supercentenarian.

Early life[]

He was born in Philadelphia, Pennsylvania, USA on January 2, 1899 to Dirk Buckley (Oct 20, 1855 - Oct 31, 1934) and Nora Buckley (Nov 20, 1877 - Apr 15, 1956) he was their first child. In 1905 he began attending Little Palermo Elementary School, in 1910 he began attending Ciara Taylor Middle School, in 1913 he began attending Joshua Black High School, in 1917 he went to university for archaeology. On December 21, 1907 his sister Serena Buckley (Dec 21, 1907 - Sep 16, 2001) was born, on December 26, 1908 his brother Juan Buckley (Dec 26, 1908 - Feb 27, 1988) was born, on October 29, 1917 his sister Nicole Buckley (Oct 29, 1917 - Jun 25, 2002) was born.

Adult life[]

In 1917 he wanted to join the army for World War I but his bad eyesight caused him to be denied.

In 1923 at the age of 24 he got an archaeologist job for the Philadelphia University System.

In 1923 he began dating Hillary Parker (Nov 23, 1893 - Jul 31, 2004) they would be married on May 13, 1929.

On March 23, 1931 he had his first child Mary Buckley (Mar 23, 1931 - Jul 23, 2008), on January 6, 1934 he had his second child Virginia Buckley (born Jan 6, 1934), on December 14, 1935 he had his third child Betsy Buckley (born Dec 14, 1935).

On October 31, 1934 just 11 days after his 79th birthday his father Dirk Buckley died in his sleep.

On July 5, 1937 his mother was remarried to Jeremiah Davis (born Mar 14, 1881 - Oct 5, 1957) giving him a step-brother Jorge Davis (Nov 17, 1909 - May 29, 1987).

On April 15, 1956 his mother Nora Buckley-Davis died at the age of 78 from old age.

On October 5, 1957 his step-father Jeremiah Davis died at the age of 76 from a stroke

Later life[]

On June, 4 1962 he had his first grandchild Damian Turner-Pitt (born Jun 4, 1962) (son of Betsy Buckley), on May 27, 1968 he had his second grandchild Penelope Figueroa (born May 27, 1968) (daughter of Mary Buckley), on January 21, 1975 he had his third grandchild Samuel Walker (born Jan 21, 1975) (son of Virginia Buckley), on December 14, 1977 he had his fourth grandchild Alyssa Huang (born Dec 14, 1977) (adopted daughter of Virginia Buckley), on September 6, 1978 he had his fifth grandchild Christopher Walker (born Sep 6, 1978) (son of Virginia Buckley).

On May 29, 1987 his step-brother Jorge Davis died at the age of 77 from a stroke.

On February 27, 1988 his brother Juan Buckley died at the age of 79 from old age.

On 1990 he finally retired from his job after 67 years,

On January 2, 1999 he turned 100 becoming a centenarian.

On September 16, 2001 his sister Serena Buckley died at the age of 93 from a stroke.

On June 25, 2002 his sister Nicole Buckley died at the age of 85 from natural causes she was his last surviving sibling.

On July 31, 2004 his wife Hillary Buckley died at the age of 110 in her sleep they were together for 81 years and married for 75 years.

On July 23, 2008 his daughter Mary Buckley died at the age of 77 from cancer.

On January 2, 2009 he turned 110 becoming a supercentenarian.

On October 31, 2017 Arthur Buckley died at the age of 118 years, 302 days his date of death was exactly 83 years after his father's death.

Trivia[]

If he was a real person Arthur Buckley would have became the oldest validated living man in the world on June 12, 2013 after the death of Jiroemon Kimura who is the oldest validated man ever at 116 years, 54 days but if Buckley was a real person he would have surpassed Kimura on February 26, 2015 and on April 6, 2015 upon the death of Gertrude Weaver he would have became the oldest validated living person in the world and at the time of his death would have been the last surviving person born in the 1800s and would have been the 3rd oldest validated person ever (as of June 2025 he would now be 5th oldest validated ever) the next oldest validated living person would have been Nabi Tajima born on August 4, 1900 and the next oldest validated living man would have been Francisco Núñez Olivera born on December 13, 1904.
