Derek Charles Howsen (April 1, 1903 - May 15, 1974), nicknamed "The Howzer," was an American professional football quarterback, military officer, museum worker and owner. He spent his entire 13-year playing career (1925 - 1938) with the New York Giants, spawning a dynasty with the franchise in which he led the team to win two championships in a row. He won many awards throughout his career. Howsen retired from football at the age of 35 to pursue a career in the United States Marine Corps, in which he rose to the rank of Brigadier General. In his later years, he practiced extreme Machiavellianism, opening a museum to display many unique artifacts, three of which included his two championship rings and the taxidermized body of his late son.
