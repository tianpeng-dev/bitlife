Thomas Bater (November 19, 1922 - June 25, 2021), also nicknamed Tommy Bates, was an American baseball player who pitched for the Tampa Bay Mantas.

Early Life[]

Thomas was concieved after a failed pull-out. His mother, Sara (1896 - 1988) was a receptionist, and his father, Clark (1875 - 1956) was a policy analyst. He had a big brother, Clark Jr. (1920 - 2002), an accountant for Alchemy Electronics.

Legal trouble[]

In 1962, after finishing his 18th season with the Tampa Bay Mantas, Bater robbed $13,954,833 from a Western Railway train. He was never accused of it as a crime when it was discovered that the money he stole happened to be bribe money from mob bosses.

Thomas and Clark made fun of school voleyball players during a game in 2000. Thomas was arrested for disorderly conduct. He tried to bribe the police with a million dollars, but it didn't work. He hired Stroker and Caulfield to represent him in court and pled not guilty. Thomas was convicted of disorderly conduct and bribery and was sentenced to two years in Salt Lake City Asylum. He was released in 2002.

Death[]

Bater died at the age of 98 on June 25, 2021. He is survived by his wife, seven children and eleven grandchildren.
