Wyatt Christopher Schunkar (October 17, 1940 - May 2, 2024) was an American financial advisor, aviator, burglar, pickpocket, and bank robber. During his 20-year career working for Doolittle Trust Bank, throughout the 1960s and 1970s, he embezzled over $50,000 because he believed the company was part of a corrupt system. After an early retirement at the age of 42, he resumed his vigilante career of stealing money from criminals and corrupt people, which he started during childhood, upgrading from mere house burglaries and pickpocketing to bank robberies. He robbed Doolittle Trust five times over the course of several years and spent a total of fourteen years in prison, from which he escaped numerous times.

Early life and education[]

Wyatt was born in Clarksville, Philadelphia to Gretchen (1900 - 1982) and Christopher "Chris" Schunkar (1916 - 1994). His mother was a real estate agent, while his father was a restaurateur and World War II draft dodger. He had an older brother named Kyrie (1937 - 2013), a teacher. The family owned a cat named Tasha (1938 - 1950). He was best friends with Daniel Lopez (1940 - 2014), whom he met as a toddler.

Schunkar went to University of Pennsylvania, Class of 1962. Majoring in finance, he joined the hipsters clique and became a member of the Lambda Theta Phi fraternity. Instead of doing any extracurriculars, Wyatt worked as a cafeteria worker for the school, aiming to make extra money for his education and future career. He graduated with high honors and received noteworthy recognition at the ceremonies.

Financial career[]

Upon graduating from UPenn, Schunkar applied for a job at Doolittle Trust Bank in 1966 so he could have a chance of employment to avoid being drafted into the military. His interviewer happened to be a fellow frat member of his, and he was hired on the spot.

Personal life[]

At his first middle school dance in 1951, Schunkar met Brigitte Li (born 1940), a psychologist for Clarksville Public Schools. After seven years of dating, they married in 1958 when they were both 18. Wyatt used the money he had collected during his early pickpocketing and burglary career to fund the wedding. They remained married for sixty-four years until their divorce in 2022 while Schunkar was in prison. They had six children together:

  • Baker (born 1960) - IT Support for Blueprint Electronics
  • Marquis (born 1969) - Massage therapist for Mesosphere Salon & Organics
  • Charlotte (born 1970) - Hairdresser at Mesosphere Salon & Organics
  • LaSondra (born 1974) - Grocer for Jones Grocery
  • Valeria (born 1979) - Financial analyst for Valley Financial
  • Stanley (born 1983) - Mobile app developer at Handy Development

Legal issues[]

Schunkar's side job as a "Robin Hood"-styled thief began at age 8 in 1948, at which point he had begun pickpocketing from thugs, outlaws, perverts, and others. He also profited from mischievous acts, such as one time when he and Daniel sold fake pot to some college students. At age 10 (1950), Schunkar committed his first burglary at the home of a serial killer. He retrieved an electronic device and a guitar from the killer's home and traded them at his local pawn shop for $389. The next year, he scored bigger by burglarizing a drug dealer's home, receiving $18,505 for trading in $9,137 in cash, a guitar, a teddy bear, a computer, and a television. He managed to do it yet again with another drug dealer's home at age 12, scoring $272 more than the previous year. Schunkar would continue this yearly tradition until the age of 19, by which point he accumulated a net worth of over tens of thousands of dollars. He would later resume his burglaries on occasion during his financial career.

In 1982, Schunkar thew his own retirement party at his house as a way to cope with the recent passing of his mother. Police came to the property to report a noise complaint. After a search of the house, Wyatt was arrested on charges for embezzlement, for which a warrant had been issued by the Clarksville Police Department for some time. Schunkar unsuccessfully tried to bribe the police with $11,900. This added bribery to the charges. He hired Colombo and Diaz to represent him in court and pled not guilty to the charges. After a moderate trial, Schunkar was convicted of bribery and embezzlement. In addition to a sentence of two years in Cruz Detention Center, he was forced to pay back the $56,059 he had stolen over the twenty years he worked for Doolittle Trust.

A year into Schunkar's sentence, Brigitte gave birth to their sixth child, Stanley, in 1983 as the result of a conjugal visit the year before. After another conjugal visit, Schunkar escaped from Cruz Detention Center. In 1984, he burglarized a pimp's house, stealing $8,914 in cash, a guitar, two computers and a teddy bear, which he traded for $16,432. In 1985, Schunkar was shot at while flying in his Cessna over a marijuana farm. He managed to safely make an emergency landing. It is speculated that the shooter was an undercover cop working for the Clarksville P.D., posing as the farmer of the marijuana field, as part of the search for Schunkar. The event occurred around the time of the Andrew C. Thorton II drug smuggling and pilot death incident. After being on the run for three years, Schunkar was captured by authorities in 1986 and sent to Clarksville Federal Penitentiary for one year to finish the second half of his original two-year sentence. He was released in 1987.

Shortly after his release from Clarksville Federal Penitentiary, Schunkar committed his first bank robbery, at the age of 47, at none other than Doolittle Trust. Disguised as a security guard with a toy gun as a weapon, he took $13,880 from the bank and got away by train. Three years later in 1990, he robbed Doolittle again. He disguised himself as a military general and even created a persona (named "General Winston") that he actually had served in the United States Army since the Vietnam War and pulled off the gimmick that he was on a secret mission to recover stolen cash as ordered by President George H. W. Bush. He got away with $20,110 and used his local bus route as his getaway. In 1994, some time after he attended his father's funeral (of which he was a main speaker), he robbed Doolittle once more, disguised as a banker and escaped with $19,150. In 1996, Schunkar's string of robberies was almost revealed when he was arrested for drug possession at a house party he was holding. He was acquitted on the charges due to a lack of evidence, as well as having a strong alibi provided by himself and his family.

Schunkar would rob Doolittle Trust Bank two more times, in 1998, disguised as a cop, escaped by train with $166,670, and in 2000, where he donned his fake military persona again and got away with $36,890. In a flawed sixth attempt in 2002, he was arrested. Police found him in knight's armor, which was his disguise, and confiscated his weapon of choice, a sword. Schunkar was now charged with five counts of armed robbery, one count of attempted armed robbery, multiple counts of felony burglary, and stolen valor for his masquerading as a military general. He pled not guilty to all the charges, with Armstrong and Associates representing him in court. After a lengthy trial, Schunkar was found guilty on all the charges and sentenced to 29 years at Morning Glory Island, an island-based prison that is often described as the "Philadelphian Alcatraz." Shortly after his conviction, however, Schunkar successfully appealed the court's decision, with the help of the firm Cruz & McGregor, and was released. Cruz & McGregor would become subject to a class-action lawsuit by Doolittle Trust for letting Schunkar get out of prison earlier than intended.

In 2003, Schunkar made yet another attempt to rob Doolittle. The arresting officers found him in a nun outfit. During his interrogation, Schunkar said he came up with the disguise when thinking back to a childhood moment when he dressed up as a nun at a 1948 costume party with Daniel. In an interview, Daniel elaborated that Wyatt would even get into character as a nun, with the alter ego "Sister Wynonna." The crossdressing community has since praised Schunkar for his clever trick. He was sent back to Morning Glory Island for 29 years. In 2005, Schunkar incited a riot that killed one person and injured 27 others. In the aftermath of the riot, he escaped from the island. After being on the run for five years this time, Schunkar was caught trying to rob Doolittle Once again in 2010. He escaped in 2012 by using the same riot method from before. He would spend the next few years laying low.

In 2015, Schunkar committed his first burglary in many years. From a thug's home, he stole a teddy bear, $2,313 in cash, and a computer. He traded the items and received only $414 more than what he stole in cash. In 2016, after throwing another house party, Schunkar was captured and sent to Clarksville Federal Penitentiary for 25 years. In 2018, he joined the Bitizen Mafia prison gang. That same year, he incited a riot, which added nine years to his sentence. Schunkar would spend the last eight years of his life in jail. Had he lived, his release date in 2050 would have left him at the age of 110, and he would have been eligible for parole in his late 80s or early 90s.

Death[]

Schunkar died in prison on May 2, 2024, at the age of 83. He is survived by his six children and any potential grandchildren. He had spent a grand total of 14 years in prison. Schunkar's life and crimes have been the subject of many forms of media, such as informative documentaries and award-winning feature films.
