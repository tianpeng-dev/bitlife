Baron
William Nilsson
(b. June 11, 1898 — d. August 27, 1917) was a Swedish baron, born to
Lars Nilsson
and Molly Nilsson, and the legal heir to one-fifth of his father's estate. He was one of the successors of the Nilsson branche.
The Baron of Marstrand, inheritor of the Counts of Rosenqvist.
Childhood
[
]
William at age 6
William at age 12
William was born in Gothenburg, Sweden in 1898, 9 months after his parents' wedding night at Crosspen Estate with 675 guests. After his birth, he was designated the Baron of Marstrand and his father's main heir due to the fact that he was older than all of his siblings with the exception of
Matilda
. At the age of 7 (1905) he was enrolled in Taubeskolan Primary School, one of the best in the city. A year later he was diagnosed with epilepsy. William received special attention from his father, who often took him on outings, including riding bikes, flying, etc. Soon he receives a modeling offer from his father, which he accepts. From 14 to 16 years old he was transferred to home schooling in etiquette, literacy and other aspects of noble life. Then he was enrolled in Hvitfeldtska Secondary School. In 1916, he entered the university to major in accounting.
