Jasmin Stanley (April 29, 1939 - May 17, 2024) was an American casino worker and gambler. She thrived in the dealing industry for fifty years, working for Comet Hotel & Casino from the mid-1960s to the mid-2010s. Stanley was also a hardcore gambler on the personal level, dealing over thousands of dollars and having mixed luck at blackjack.

Early life[]

Jasmin was born in Las Vegas, Nevada as the product of a one night stand. Her mother was Nakita Stanley (1903 - 1987), an exotic dancer who served as one of the numerous mentors of fellow stripper Uma Dingleberry. She never met her biological father. She had a half-brother named Bartholomew "Bart" Stanley (1936 - 2019), a United States Coast Guard veteran who served during the Vietnam War and other notable military engagements from the mid-1950s to the late 1990s, and two stepsisters named Elena and Velma from her mother's marriage to insurance agent and World War I draft dodger Franklin "Frank" Groper (1899 - 1971), which lasted from 1944 to 1970, a year before Groper passed away to old age.

Throughout her childhood, Jasmin was very ambitious and outgoing. In middle school, she was a member of the book club, chess club, animal rights club, journalism club, and even the video games club, referring to the early video games of the 1950s. She didn't do any extracurriculars in high school, instead opting for part-time employment as an ice cream scooper for Cafe Avalon from 1955 until her graduation in 1957. In her youth, she also went on a spree of "fighting crime with crime," whereas she pickpocketed the degenerates and corrupt people of society.

Gambling and hustling in Vegas[]

After graduating from high school in 1957, Stanley wanted to enter the dealing industry as soon as possible. However, this proved to me more difficult than expected, since she kept getting rejected from any casinos in the city. She also was unable to get a job as an exotic dancer, which she partially wanted to do to briefly follow in her mother's footsteps. Unable to find employment, she spent the next two years in community college. During these years, she had started gambling. She would both win and lose a few thousand dollars playing blackjack. After graduating from community college, Stanley found work as a flight attendant for Climax Air.

For the next six years, Stanley would go on to work in five other jobs. In 1960, she quit her job as a flight attendant to become a bus driver for the city. A year after that, she became a nail technician for Spring Salon & Organics. From 1962 to 1963, she worked as a security guard for Meyer Ideas. Then, she became a housekeeper for Moorehead of Las Vegas. Her last job before becoming a casino dealer was being an escort for Thoroughbred Escorts from 1964 to 1965. The timing of this occupation was slightly controversial, since Stanley was married at the time.

Casino career[]

In 1965, at long last, Stanley finally managed to enter the casino industry, securing a job as a dealer at the Comet Hotel & Casino. She received several promotions throughout her career, becoming the floor supervisor in 1975, pit boss in 1984, assistant manager in 1990, and manager in 1998.

Stanley retired from Comet Hotel & Casino in 2015 after fifty years of professional dealing. Despite her retirement, her personal gambling would continue.

Personal life[]

In her last year of elementary school, Stanley started dating her first childhood best friend, Master Chu (born 1938), who would go on to become a grocer and a mixed-martial artist, partly due to his unique name. They married in 1957 and had three children together:

  • Amber (born 1957) - Retired travel agent
  • Cole (born 1959) - Retired police officer
  • Yusef (born 1961) - Retired trucker

Stanley bought her first home, an intimate houseboat at 278 Gay Drive, in 1967. She rented out the property for tenancy, and she got her first tenant, Taylor Juarez (born 1947), a professional mermaid with one past eviction and a criminal record of forgery.

Death[]

Stanley suffered a stroke on May 17, 2024. She died a few hours later in the hospital, aged 85. She is survived by her husband, three children, and five grandchildren. Her net worth was $1,777,252.
