Dr.
Harold George Pickler
M.D. (May 24, 1861 - January 8, 1942) was a veterinarian from Oklahoma City. He worked for the Oklahoma City Veterinary Clinic for fifty years, and worked five years for Sellers Veterinary Hospital.

Childhood and education
[
]
Harry was born in Oklahoma City to financial advisor Fabian Pickler (1812 - 1887) and Abby Pickler (1810 - 1901). He had an older brother, Warren (1841 - 1914), a reporter, and a nephew, Young (1866 - 1941), a restaurant worker.
Despite not having pets in his youth, Pickler wanted to become a vet when he grew up, having grown up studying animals. He served in the cancer awareness club at St. Juanita's High School, and in college, he joined the animal rights club. He graduated university in 1883 with an undergraduate degree in biology. He then attended veterinary school from 1883 to 1887.
Career
[
]
In 1887, a frat brother hired Pickler for the position of Veterinarian at Oklahoma City Veterinary Clinic. He befriended most of his coworkers throughout the years. He was promoted to Sr. Veterinarian in 1893.
Pickler bought several pets in the early 1910s of his career. In 1911, he bought a betta fish named Jeff, a rabbit called Jose, a goldfish named Robin, and a boxer called Aliyah. A year later, he bought another goldfish, simply named Goldie. He let his fish go into the wild, feeling that they should swim in their natural habitat.
Pickler often presented Aliyah and Jose to his co-workers during meetings and coventional events for eco-friendly research purposes, and he also occasionally showed them to young students in school during the environmental events and festivities of the year.
In 1937, Pickler retired from Oklahoma City Veterinary Clinic. He then began working for Sellers Veterinary Hospital, serving for five years until his death.
Personal life
[
]
In 1880, during his second year at university, Pickler met Heather Mayberry (born 1861), soon to be a lobbyist for Absolute Zero Systems. He helped her study for an exam in the class of Dr. Judge. Although she failed it, Mayberry appreciated Pickler's help. They began dating after that, and in 1889, heather proposed to Harry. He accepted and they married 1891. They went to Oktoberfest for their honeymoon. They had two sons together, Damien Pickler (1896 - ????), a biotechnologist for McDonagh Engineering, and Ezekiel (1901 - 1906). Ezekiel died at five years old, having succomed to a three year battle of whooping cough. Harry felt extreme guilt for Ezekiel's death, having forgotten to get him diagnosed and treated in time.
Death
[
]
On January 8, 1942, Pickler was diagnosed with bunions. Unable to fight it with normal treatment, he sought a witch doctor, who fed him a snake. It killed him. An investigation on the witch doctor is currently underway. Pickler was survived by his son, wife, and granddaughter, Kim (born 1930).
