Dennis Howsen
(1921 - 1994) was an American writer whose career spanned five decades from the late 1930s to the late 1980s. He was the father of football player
Derek Howsen
.
Biography
[
]
Dennis was born in 1921 in Virginia to a devout but non-racist Southern family. His father, Charles Howsen III (c. 1900 - c. 1970s), was a young World War I veteran who suffered from combat fatigue (later known as PTSD) in the first few years after the war. His great-grandfather, Charles Howsen (c. 1840s - c. 1910s), was a Civil War veteran who originally fought for the Confederacy, but later switched sides, realizing that racism was wrong and did not align with his beliefs of universal equality and human rights. Charles Sr's son and Dennis' grandfather, Charles Jr. (c. 1870s - c. 1940s), was a Spanish-American War veteran and post-Civil War ideologist. Dennis developed a passion for writing at a young age and began writing stories during childhood based on his experiences growing up during the Roaring Twenties and Great Depression. Howsen cited John Steinbeck's
Of Mice and Men
, of which he was gifted one of the many first sold copies for his sixteenth birthday, as one of his all-time favorite books.
Howsen began writing books professionally after graduating from high school in 1939. When the United States entered the Second World War two years later, Dennis would be drafted into the military, but he was still allowed to keep his job as a writer so long as he worked hard and maintained good conduct in his service. Most of the stories he wrote during his service would reflect on his experiences of fighting the Japanese in the Pacific. He used his writing skills for his platoon, writing numerous telegrams for several missions and other situations, and would also write the screenplays of numerous Allied propaganda films. Howsen received his discharge at the end of the war in late 1945 and received numerous awards in both combat and writing categories.
In his teens, Howsen met his girlfriend,
Camille
, a magician. After the war, they married and had three sons, including
Donald
(born 1952), Derek (April 1, 1953 - May 15, 2024), and
Antonio
(born 1960).
...
In 1987, after publishing his last professional book, Howsen retired. He would spend the last years of his life spending time with his family, especially his grandchildren. He would also continue to write stories as a hobby and share them with his grandchildren. Howsen died from natural causes in 1994 at the age of 73. Camille would follow eighteen years later, passing in 2012, followed by Derek in 2024. He is survived by his two other sons and his grandchildren.
