Dr. Elizabeth Marie Baskett M.D. (March 31, 1984 - January 6, 2022) was an American mobster of the Irish Samuel Family, a former Jr. Psychiatrist and Navy veteran. During her middle school, high school and college years, she was a basketball wannabe, but was unable to make any team despite her talent. After finishing medical school, she joined the US Navy and served for one year before going AWOL to pursue her career as a psychiatrist, only to be caught and sent to jail for five years.

Early life and medical school[]

Lisa was born in Mint City, the birthplace of women's basketball pioneer Lusia Harris, to Navy enlistee Krystal Baskett (later Herron) (born 1961) and mail carrier Jay Baskett (born 1962). Her parents divorced when she was two, but she still got to spend plenty of time with them, as well as her half/step sisters and step parents.

Baskett became interested in basketball for several reasons. One, she was born in the same city as Lusia Harris. Two, her last name sounded athletic to her. Throughout her education, she never made a cut for any basketball team. She worked out at gyms day and night, and even participated in her University's weightlifting club from 2002 to 2004, but her talent for strength still didn't help her.

After graduating university in 2006, Krystal payed for Baskett's tuition for medical school when she was rejected of a scholarship. During her fifth year in 2011, she bought her cottage house on a mortgage, and paid it off during her sixth year. To celebrate the payoff, she threw a party for fourteen guests. Everyone had a great time.

Navy and psychology career[]

Baskett graduated medical school in 2013. She decided she would serve a few years in the Navy with her mother. She started off as an ensign and was promoted to lieutenant junior grade. During her second year, she witnessed horrific fates of foreigners, especially during crises such as ISIS. Having seen enough, she deserted her platoon. After her desertion, Baskett was hired as a Jr. Psychiatrist for Mint City Health. She befriended several of her coworkers and worked hard at her job. In 2015, she was arrested, charged, tried and convicted of her desertion and relieved from her job. She was released in 2020.

Samuel Family[]

Following her release from prison, Baskett was unable to find another medical job because of her criminal past. She decided that she would try to fight the corrupted parts of society; she performed menial taks for the Irish mob family Samuel for 37 days, and the family took her in. Her first crime in five years was burglarizing the home of another mobster. She stole his teddy bear and watch, and contributed her full earnings of nearly $20,000, recieving her cut of almost $2,000. Baskett later attempted to pickpocket a creeper, but the creeper attacked her and got away.

Personal life[]

During a gym workout in her freshmen year of high school, Baskett met Gilbert Maggot (born 1984), who would become an insurance agent for Catamaran Insurance Group. After seven years of dating, they married in 2005. Their son, Marquis Maggot, was born in 2014. Gilbert supposedly had a brief military stint during the War on Terror, either in Afghanistan or Iraq, but there is currently no official proof of these claims.

In 2016, one year into her sentence, Baskett was permitted a conjugal visit from her husband. By the time she was released, Marquis was a year into elementary school. Baskett told her son that he is bright and apologized for being absent for several years of his young life. They spent as much time as possible together to make up for what had been lost. The same applied to the rest of her family and her friends.

Death[]

On January 6, 2022, one year after the Capitol insurrection, a Samuel "snitch" told Baskett that the family had pure evil plans to carry out a similar attack against the democracy of the United States. The snitch offered her $11,300 as hush money to get away. When the family found out, Baskett offered to use her phycology experience to get them help. The Samuel family rejected her and beat her senseless. They ripped her ankle, stretched her eyeball, and they eventually threw her lifeless body into the sea.

The suspects of Lisa's murder awaited trial in custody starting from January 6, 2022. Her funeral was attended by all her friends and loved ones. She is survived by her parents and stepparents, her husband, her son, and her four half/stepsisters. Eventually, all of the suspects were found guilty of the murder, and most of them were sentenced to life in prison; some received lighter sentences for helping the police with the investigation.

Krystal retired from the Navy in 2023. She dedicated her retirement speech to Lisa.
