Nations

Launched on iOS:

September 2018 (default)
Nations are the representation of real-world nations within the game that bitizens can inhabit (not all nations are countries, some are territories, but the majority are countries). Players can emigrate to 8 nations that are randomized every time player press the "Emigrate" button in the activities.

As of April 21, 2023, BitLife Version 3.8.7, the Golden Passport became an item available for purchase in the BitLife Marketplace, allowing players to emigrate to their country of choice via a complete selection instead of the typical randomized 8 countries.

When a character emigrates to another nation, they can renovate but cannot use property in the former location and they may can travel to funerals and can visit people outside their country without emigrating. If a character has a lover and kids, they may vacation or emigrate with the character if they desire, with added cost for multiple people. The player also has to find a new career in the current location. If a character is on the run from crimes, the crimes will not affect their ability to find jobs, adopt, and join the military, provided that they emigrated legally. Some locations represent their local currencies such as Euros (€) and Pounds (£), but most use the standard Dollar symbol.

  • On May 10, 2020, BitLife released the following countries: Algeria, Bangladesh, Bolivia, Croatia, Georgia, Hungary, Jordan, Qatar, Slovakia, and Zimbabwe. 10 more countries are counted.

Royalty[]

Some countries have kings/queens, dukes/duchesses, emperors/empresses, etc. If you have God Mode, you can choose to be born into royalty. If you do not own God Mode, you can still marry into royalty, or very rarely, be born into it.

Countries with royalty include:

  • Belgium
  • Denmark
  • Japan
  • Jordan
  • Kuwait
  • Malaysia
  • Morocco
  • Netherlands
  • Norway
  • Qatar
  • Saudi Arabia
  • Spain
  • Sweden
  • Thailand
  • United Arab Emirates
  • United Kingdom

Death/estate tax[]

Death/estate tax is where, when you move on to a new generation, your previous generation's money is taxed. This means that if you leave 100,000 to your child, then you become your child, you will not be given the full 100,000. This tax can be irritating, particularly for players attempting to get high wealth achievements.

Countries without death/estate tax*:

  • Australia (also has highest currency worth)

Annual Tax[]

In Bitlife, you might have noticed that your player character doesn't take home the full salary that they earn. A portion of it goes to taxes (alongside other expenses).
