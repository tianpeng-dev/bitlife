John Carson Carballo
(February 10, 1933 - December 12, 2021), sometimes referred to as
John Wayne Carballo
, was an American baseball player and actor. He joined as a pitcher for the Texas Panhandlers in 1951 after he graduated from high school. After he retired from baseball in 1959, Carballo attended college with a degree in music, graduating in 1963, and from there pursued a successful acting career spanning almost sixty years.

Early Life
[
]
Johnny was born to travel agent Sophie Carballo (1901 - 1985) in Los Angeles. His father was never known. He had several half-sisters, one named Carly (born 1929).
At age eight, Carballo took up voice lessons. He practiced voices of iconic characters, cartoons or live-action. He was a student actor in Damon Middle School, a star for being the lead role in all his plays. He joined the choir and baseball team ag St. Velma's High School. He sang songs from his favorite musicians, and he was a star student pitcher, striking out most of the opposing batters.
Baseball Career
[
]
In July 1951, Carballo made himself eligible for the pro baseball draft. He was drafted in the second round by the Texas Panhandlers. He recieved and accepted a $1.2 million contract, joining the team as a pitcher. During one of his exercises that same year, he lacerated his calf muscle and had to undergo surgery. When a natural disaster devastated the Panhandlers home park in 1952, Carballo chipped in by organizing a volunteer clean-up effort. Because of his surgery, he missed 53 of 162 games of that season. The Panhandlers finished 21st in the league. In a game during the 1953 season, he struck out the catcher on a curveball to secure a win against the Oklahoma Players. He struck out another catcher in 1956 with a two-seam fastball, thus beating the Cleveland Buckers.
In 1957, the Panhandlers traded Carballo to the Detroit Bikers. In 1958, critics claimed that he was malnourished, to which he responded that he would hit the gym more often.
In 1959, Johnny initially accepted a contract with the Chicago Chubs. Later that year, he retired from professional baseball, one of the youngest athletes to do so at the age of twenty-six.
Upon retiring, Carballo subsequently enrolled in Los Angeles University in a music program. He joined the drama club again and successfully rushed the Alpha Epsilon Pi fraternity.
Acting
[
]
Upon his 30th birthday after graduating college, Carballo pursued an acting career in 1963.
Personal life
[
]
Carballo met Jeanette Jordan (born 1933) in his first year of middle school at age 11. They started out as best friends, but it quickly evolved into a romantic relationship that would last throughout middle and high school. At the beginning of his baseball career, they eloped. She went to college and eventually became a datebase administrator for Prominence Bank Corporation, working there from 1956 until retiring in 1999. They had 10 children together.
Death
[
]
Carballo died on December 12, 2021. He is survived by his wife, nine surviving children, seven grandchildren, neices, nephews, and half-sister.
