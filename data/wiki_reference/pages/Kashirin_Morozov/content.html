Kashirin Morozov (January 29, 1994 - May 17, 2024) was a Russian astronaut for Roscosmos. His promising career was cut short when he was killed during his first research mission at the age of 30. Prior to becoming an astronaut, he previously worked as a firefighter, police officer, computer programmer, and even served in the Russian Air Force (formerly Soviet Air Forces) as a distinguished fighter pilot. He deserted the military in his third year of service when he was denied a discharge in response to landmine explosion injuries he suffered from his first and only deployment.

Childhood[]

Kashirin was born in Kazan, Russia to stay-at-home mother Akinfeeva Yurievich (born 1975) and firefighter Ustinov Morosov (born 1976). He had a stepfather named Boris Putin (born 1976), a receptionist whom Akinfeeva married from 1996 to 1999. He also had a half-brother named Igor Fedorov (born 2000), a cameraman for Maestro TV.

Personal life[]

In his fourth year of middle school, Morozov met Veronika Gorbachev, an eventual police officer. After dating for eight years, Gorbachev proposed in 2016, to which Morozov accepted, and they married that same year. Their son, Ilya, was born in 2024.
